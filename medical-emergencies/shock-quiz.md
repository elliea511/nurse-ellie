# Shock Practice Quiz

Test your knowledge of shock types, recognition, and priority nursing interventions.

---

## Question 1

A nurse is caring for a client who is at risk for shock. Which of the following findings should the nurse identify as an early sign of shock?

<p class="answer-choice">A. Restlessness and tachycardia</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. Mottled skin and absent pulses</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. Severe hypotension and organ failure</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. Bradycardia and widened pulse pressure</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: A</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">Early shock can cause restlessness, anxiety, confusion, tachycardia, tachypnea, cool clammy skin, delayed capillary refill, weak pulses, and decreased urine output. Severe hypotension, mottled skin, absent pulses, and organ failure are later findings. Bradycardia with widened pulse pressure is more concerning for increased ICP, not typical early shock.</p>
</details>

## Question 2

A nurse is caring for a client who may be developing shock. Which of the following assessment findings is the best indicator of tissue perfusion?

<p class="answer-choice">A. Capillary refill greater than 3 seconds</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. Urine output of 20 mL/hr</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. Weak peripheral pulses</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. Cool, clammy skin</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: B</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">Urine output is the best indicator of tissue perfusion because the kidneys need adequate blood flow to make urine. The adult goal is at least 30 mL/hr. Capillary refill, peripheral pulses, and skin temperature are also indicators of perfusion, but urine output is a major sign of kidney perfusion.</p>
</details>

## Question 3

A nurse is caring for a client who has severe bleeding from a leg wound after a motor vehicle crash. The client is pale, cool, clammy, and tachycardic. Which of the following actions should the nurse take first?

<p class="answer-choice">A. Start or maintain IV access.</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. Apply direct pressure to the wound.</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. Administer oxygen as prescribed.</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. Keep the client warm.</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: B</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">This client is showing signs of hypovolemic shock from blood loss. All of these are appropriate early interventions, but the nurse should first control the source of bleeding by applying direct pressure to the wound. The nurse should also support oxygenation, maintain IV access, keep the client warm, monitor vital signs, and prepare for fluids or blood products as ordered.</p>
</details>

## Question 4

A nurse is caring for a client who has hypovolemic shock after a gastrointestinal bleed. Which of the following findings should the nurse expect?

<p class="answer-choice">A. Cool clammy skin, tachycardia, and decreased urine output</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. Warm dry skin, bradycardia, and bounding pulses</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. Hives, wheezing, and swelling of the lips</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. Chest pain, crackles, and jugular vein distention</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: A</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">Hypovolemic shock occurs when the client loses too much blood or fluid. Expected findings include tachycardia, tachypnea, cool clammy skin, weak pulses, decreased urine output, thirst, restlessness, confusion, and later hypotension. Hives, wheezing, and swelling suggest anaphylactic shock. Chest pain, crackles, and JVD suggest cardiogenic shock.</p>
</details>

## Question 5

A nurse is caring for a client who develops hives, wheezing, swelling of the lips, and hypotension after receiving an antibiotic. Which of the following actions should the nurse prepare to take first?

<p class="answer-choice">A. Administer IV fluids as prescribed.</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. Prepare to administer epinephrine as prescribed.</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. Administer antihistamines as prescribed.</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. Give oxygen as prescribed.</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: B</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">This client is showing signs of anaphylactic shock, which can cause airway swelling, bronchospasm, vasodilation, and poor perfusion. All of these actions may be used for anaphylactic shock, but epinephrine is the priority medication because it helps reverse airway swelling, bronchospasm, and severe vasodilation. The nurse should also maintain the airway, give oxygen as prescribed, establish IV access, give IV fluids as ordered, and prepare other medications such as antihistamines, corticosteroids, and bronchodilators as prescribed.</p>
</details>

## Question 6

A nurse is caring for a client who has cardiogenic shock after a myocardial infarction. Which of the following prescriptions should the nurse question?

<p class="answer-choice">A. Monitor lung sounds for crackles.</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. Place the client on a cardiac monitor.</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. Administer a large rapid IV fluid bolus.</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. Monitor urine output and mental status.</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: C</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">In cardiogenic shock, the heart cannot pump effectively. The nurse should question a large rapid IV fluid bolus because extra fluid can worsen fluid overload, crackles, pulmonary edema, and heart failure. Monitoring lung sounds, cardiac rhythm, urine output, and mental status are appropriate interventions.</p>
</details>

## Question 7

A nurse is caring for a client who has a wound infection, fever, tachycardia, warm flushed skin, and confusion. Which of the following types of shock should the nurse suspect?

<p class="answer-choice">A. Cardiogenic shock</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. Septic shock</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. Neurogenic shock</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. Obstructive shock</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: B</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">Septic shock occurs when infection causes widespread inflammation, vasodilation, capillary leak, and poor tissue perfusion. Early findings can include fever or low temperature, tachycardia, tachypnea, warm flushed skin, bounding pulses, and confusion.</p>
</details>

## Question 8

A nurse is caring for a client who has septic shock. Which of the following actions should the nurse expect to take?

<p class="answer-choice">A. Administer antibiotics as prescribed.</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. Restrict IV fluids to prevent fluid overload.</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. Place the client flat with the neck flexed.</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. Encourage oral fluids before IV treatment.</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: A</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">Septic shock is caused by infection, so antibiotics are a priority treatment. The nurse should obtain cultures as ordered before antibiotics if this does not delay care, administer IV fluids as ordered, monitor urine output and perfusion, and notify the provider of worsening signs.</p>
</details>

## Question 9

A nurse is caring for a client after a spinal cord injury. The client has hypotension, bradycardia, and warm dry skin. Which of the following types of shock should the nurse suspect?

<p class="answer-choice">A. Hypovolemic shock</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. Anaphylactic shock</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. Neurogenic shock</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. Cardiogenic shock</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: C</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">Neurogenic shock occurs when nervous system injury causes loss of vascular tone. Findings can include hypotension, bradycardia, warm dry skin, temperature regulation problems, and possible paralysis or spinal injury symptoms.</p>
</details>

## Question 10

A nurse is reviewing shock with a student nurse. Which of the following conditions should the nurse identify as a cause of obstructive shock?

<p class="answer-choice">A. Gastrointestinal bleeding</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. Severe allergic reaction</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. Acute myocardial infarction</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. Tension pneumothorax</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: D</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">Obstructive shock occurs when a mechanical problem blocks the heart from filling or pumping effectively. Causes include tension pneumothorax, cardiac tamponade, and pulmonary embolism.</p>
</details>

## Question 11

A nurse is comparing shock and increased intracranial pressure. Which of the following findings is more consistent with shock?

<p class="answer-choice">A. Decreased blood pressure and increased pulse</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. Increased systolic blood pressure and decreased pulse</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. Widened pulse pressure and irregular respirations</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. Bradycardia and decreased responsiveness</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: A</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">Shock causes poor tissue perfusion. The pulse usually increases as the body tries to compensate, and blood pressure can decrease, especially as shock worsens. Increased ICP is more associated with increased systolic blood pressure, decreased pulse, and decreased or irregular respirations.</p>
</details>
