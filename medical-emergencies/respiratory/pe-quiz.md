# Pulmonary Embolism Practice Quiz

<div class="nclex" markdown="1">

For priority questions, ask what is most urgent: oxygenation, circulation, bleeding risk, or patient safety.

## Question 1

A nurse is caring for a client who suddenly reports shortness of breath and sharp chest pain after being on bed rest following surgery. Which of the following findings should the nurse recognize as most concerning for a pulmonary embolism?

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> Productive cough with thick yellow sputum</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> Sudden dyspnea with tachycardia</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> Gradual swelling in both ankles</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> Wheezing that improves after coughing</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: B</strong></p>
<p class="quiz-rationale"><strong>Rationale:</strong> A pulmonary embolism often causes sudden shortness of breath, tachycardia, chest pain, anxiety, and possible low oxygen saturation. The key clue is the sudden onset, especially in a client with risk factors like immobility or recent surgery.</p>
</details>

## Question 2

A nurse is caring for a client who is suspected of having a pulmonary embolism. Which of the following actions should the nurse take first?

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> Place the client in high-Fowler's position.</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> Prepare the client for a CT scan.</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> Teach the client about long-term anticoagulant therapy.</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> Apply sequential compression devices to both legs.</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: A</strong></p>
<p class="quiz-rationale"><strong>Rationale:</strong> The nurse should first support breathing by placing the client in high-Fowler's position. A CT scan and anticoagulant therapy may be needed, but airway and breathing come first. Sequential compression devices are used for prevention, not as the first action during suspected acute PE.</p>
</details>

## Question 3

A nurse is reinforcing teaching with a client who is at risk for developing a pulmonary embolism. Which of the following instructions should the nurse include? Select all that apply.

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> Avoid sitting with the legs crossed for long periods.</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> Ambulate as soon as allowed after surgery.</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> Report sudden shortness of breath or chest pain.</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> Massage the calves to increase circulation.</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>E.</strong> Drink fluids as allowed to help prevent clot formation.</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: A, B, C, E</strong></p>
<p class="quiz-rationale"><strong>Rationale:</strong> Pulmonary embolism often occurs when a clot, usually from a DVT, travels to the lungs. The client should avoid prolonged venous stasis, ambulate early, stay hydrated if allowed, and report sudden shortness of breath or chest pain. The calves should not be massaged because this could dislodge a clot.</p>
</details>

## Question 4

A nurse is caring for a client who is suspected of having a pulmonary embolism. Which of the following diagnostic tests should the nurse expect to be used most commonly to confirm the diagnosis?

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> Chest x-ray</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> CT angiography</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> Leg ultrasound</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> D-dimer</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: B</strong></p>
<p class="quiz-rationale"><strong>Rationale:</strong> CT angiography is the most common test used to confirm a pulmonary embolism. A D-dimer can help rule out inappropriate clot formation, but an elevated D-dimer does not automatically mean PE. A chest x-ray may help rule out other causes, and a leg ultrasound can look for a DVT source.</p>
</details>

## Question 5

A nurse is reviewing laboratory results for a client who is being evaluated for a pulmonary embolism. Which of the following D-dimer results should the nurse recognize as elevated?

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> 100 ng/mL</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> 250 ng/mL</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> 400 ng/mL</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> 900 ng/mL</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: D</strong></p>
<p class="quiz-rationale"><strong>Rationale:</strong> A normal D-dimer is usually less than or equal to 500 ng/mL. An elevated D-dimer can indicate that clot breakdown may be occurring somewhere in the body. However, an elevated D-dimer does not automatically confirm a pulmonary embolism.</p>
</details>

## Question 6

A nurse is caring for a client who suddenly develops shortness of breath, pleuritic chest pain, tachycardia, and anxiety. The client had abdominal surgery 2 days ago. Which of the following actions should the nurse take first?

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> Check oxygen saturation and raise the head of the bed.</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> Review the client's most recent D-dimer result.</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> Notify the provider that the client may need anticoagulation.</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> Ask the client when the chest pain started and what makes it worse.</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: A</strong></p>
<p class="quiz-rationale"><strong>Rationale:</strong> The client has findings concerning for pulmonary embolism. The priority is to support oxygenation by checking oxygen saturation and positioning the client upright. Reviewing labs, notifying the provider, and further pain assessment may be appropriate, but respiratory support comes first.</p>
</details>

## Question 7

A nurse is reinforcing teaching with a client who is receiving heparin for a pulmonary embolism. Which of the following statements by the client indicates an understanding of the medication?

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> "This medication breaks apart the clot that has traveled to my lung."</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> "This medication helps prevent the clot from getting bigger."</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> "This medication filters clots before they can reach my lungs."</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> "This medication makes my body absorb the clot immediately."</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: B</strong></p>
<p class="quiz-rationale"><strong>Rationale:</strong> Heparin is an anticoagulant. It does not dissolve an existing clot. It helps prevent the clot from getting bigger and helps prevent new clots from forming. Thrombolytics are the medications that dissolve clots.</p>
</details>

## Question 8

A nurse is caring for a client who is receiving IV heparin for a pulmonary embolism. Which of the following laboratory values should the nurse monitor?

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> PT/INR</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> D-dimer</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> Bleeding time</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> aPTT</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: D</strong></p>
<p class="quiz-rationale"><strong>Rationale:</strong> Heparin therapy is monitored using the PTT/aPTT. A helpful memory trick is "two T's = heparin" because heparin is monitored with PTT/aPTT. The nurse should also monitor for signs of bleeding throughout therapy.</p>
</details>

## Question 9

A nurse is caring for a client who received a thrombolytic medication for a severe pulmonary embolism. Which of the following findings requires immediate notification of the provider?

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> Oozing from the IV site after the dressing is removed</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> Blood-tinged sputum after coughing forcefully</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> Sudden severe headache</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> Bruising on the forearm near a previous venipuncture site</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: C</strong></p>
<p class="quiz-rationale"><strong>Rationale:</strong> Thrombolytics dissolve clots but greatly increase the risk for bleeding. A sudden severe headache can indicate possible intracranial bleeding and should be reported to the provider immediately.</p>
</details>

## Question 10

A nurse is reinforcing teaching with a client who is prescribed anticoagulant therapy after a pulmonary embolism. Which of the following statements by the client indicates a need for further teaching?

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> "I will use an electric razor when shaving."</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> "I will use a soft toothbrush when brushing my teeth."</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> "I will take aspirin if I have mild pain."</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> "I will report black stools or blood in my urine."</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: C</strong></p>
<p class="quiz-rationale"><strong>Rationale:</strong> Aspirin and NSAIDs can increase bleeding risk and should be avoided unless prescribed. Clients taking anticoagulants should use bleeding precautions, including using an electric razor, using a soft toothbrush, and reporting black stools or blood in the urine.</p>
</details>

## Question 11

A nurse is caring for a client who has a pulmonary embolism. Which of the following findings should cause the nurse to suspect a saddle embolism?

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> Mild dyspnea that improves after sitting upright</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> Sudden severe shortness of breath with hypotension</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> Calf tenderness after walking in the hallway</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> Chest discomfort that occurs only with coughing</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: B</strong></p>
<p class="quiz-rationale"><strong>Rationale:</strong> A saddle embolism is a large clot that blocks blood flow where the pulmonary artery branches to both lungs. This can cause sudden severe respiratory distress, hypotension, shock, or cardiac arrest. This is an emergency requiring immediate intervention.</p>
</details>

## Question 12

A nurse is caring for a client who has a pulmonary embolism and has a prescription for IV heparin. Which of the following should be used to determine the infusion rate?

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> The client's D-dimer result and oxygen saturation</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> The client's weight and aPTT result</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> The client's chest pain level and respiratory rate</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> The client's CT angiography report and blood pressure</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: B</strong></p>
<p class="quiz-rationale"><strong>Rationale:</strong> Heparin infusion rates are commonly based on the client's weight and adjusted using the PTT/aPTT result. The nurse should monitor for bleeding and follow the prescribed heparin protocol when adjusting the infusion.</p>
</details>

## Question 13

A nurse is reinforcing discharge teaching with a client who had a pulmonary embolism and is prescribed anticoagulant therapy. Which of the following statements by the client indicates an understanding of the teaching?

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> "I should expect mild swelling in one calf while I am recovering."</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> "I will report blood in my urine or black stools."</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> "I can stop taking the medication once my breathing improves."</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> "I should use aspirin instead if I miss a dose of my anticoagulant."</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: B</strong></p>
<p class="quiz-rationale"><strong>Rationale:</strong> Clients taking anticoagulants after a pulmonary embolism should report signs of bleeding, including blood in the urine, black stools, unusual bruising, bleeding gums, or severe headache. Calf swelling can indicate DVT and should also be reported — it is not an expected finding. The client should not stop anticoagulants or substitute aspirin unless prescribed by the provider.</p>
</details>

## Question 14

A nurse is caring for a client who has a history of pulmonary embolism and cannot take anticoagulants due to active bleeding. Which of the following treatments should the nurse expect may be prescribed to help prevent future pulmonary emboli?

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> Vena cava filter</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> Chest tube insertion</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> Incentive spirometry</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> Pulmonary angiogram</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: A</strong></p>
<p class="quiz-rationale"><strong>Rationale:</strong> A vena cava filter may be used when anticoagulants cannot be used or are not effective. It helps prevent clots from traveling from the lower body to the lungs. It does not dissolve existing clots, but it can reduce the risk of another pulmonary embolism.</p>
</details>

## Question 15

A nurse is caring for a client who is being evaluated for a pulmonary embolism. The provider prescribes a V/Q scan. Which of the following explanations should the nurse give about this test?

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> "This test checks how well air moves into the lungs and how well blood flows through the lungs."</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> "This test measures the amount of air left in the lungs after exhaling."</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> "This test checks for fluid buildup in the lung tissue and pleural space."</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> "This test measures how strongly the lungs expand during inspiration."</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: A</strong></p>
<p class="quiz-rationale"><strong>Rationale:</strong> A V/Q scan compares ventilation (airflow) and perfusion (blood flow) through the lungs. A mismatch between the two can suggest a pulmonary embolism. A leg ultrasound checks for DVT, a D-dimer measures clot breakdown, and CT angiography uses contrast to directly visualize the pulmonary vessels.</p>
</details>

## Question 16

A nurse is reviewing arterial blood gas results for a client who is suspected of having a pulmonary embolism. Which of the following findings should the nurse expect?

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> Increased oxygen level with metabolic alkalosis</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> Hypoxemia with respiratory alkalosis</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> Hypercapnia with metabolic acidosis</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> Normal oxygen level with respiratory acidosis</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: B</strong></p>
<p class="quiz-rationale"><strong>Rationale:</strong> A pulmonary embolism can cause impaired gas exchange, leading to hypoxemia. Early in PE, the client may breathe faster due to anxiety and low oxygen, which can cause respiratory alkalosis from blowing off too much carbon dioxide.</p>
</details>

## Question 17

A nurse is reviewing risk factors for pulmonary embolism with a group of clients. Which of the following factors increase the risk for pulmonary embolism? Select all that apply.

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> Recent abdominal surgery with limited ambulation</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> Sitting for several hours during long-distance travel</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> Postpartum period with decreased mobility</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> Smoking while taking oral contraceptives</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>E.</strong> Using prescribed compression stockings after surgery</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: A, B, C, D</strong></p>
<p class="quiz-rationale"><strong>Rationale:</strong> Risk factors for pulmonary embolism include immobility, recent surgery, long-distance travel, pregnancy, postpartum period, smoking, oral contraceptive use, cancer, obesity, heart failure, and history of DVT or PE. Using compression stockings promotes circulation and lowers the risk of venous stasis, so it is a protective measure, not a risk factor.</p>
</details>

## Question 18

A nurse is caring for a client who has a massive pulmonary embolism and is not improving with medication therapy. Which of the following treatments should the nurse expect may be prescribed to remove the clot?

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> Placement of a vena cava filter</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> Surgical embolectomy</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> Administration of IV heparin</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> Administration of a thrombolytic medication</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: B</strong></p>
<p class="quiz-rationale"><strong>Rationale:</strong> A surgical embolectomy may be used to physically remove a pulmonary embolism when the clot is severe, life-threatening, or not responding to other treatment. A vena cava filter helps prevent clots from traveling to the lungs, heparin helps prevent the clot from getting larger, and thrombolytics help dissolve clots.</p>
</details>
