## Question 1

A nurse is caring for a client who suddenly develops right-sided weakness and difficulty speaking. Which of the following questions should the nurse ask first?

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> "Do you have a history of high blood pressure?"</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> "When were you last known to be well?"</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> "Have you had these symptoms before?"</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> "Are you currently taking any blood thinners?"</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: B</strong></p>
<p class="quiz-rationale"><strong>Rationale:</strong> For suspected stroke, the most important question is the last known well time. This helps determine whether the client may be eligible for thrombolytic therapy. Time matters because treatment has a strict time window.</p>
</details>

## Question 2

A nurse is reinforcing teaching with a client who had a transient ischemic attack. Which of the following statements by the client indicates a need for further teaching?

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> "I should call emergency services if the symptoms happen again."</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> "A TIA can be a warning sign that I am at risk for a stroke."</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> "If the symptoms go away quickly, I do not need to seek care."</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> "I should take my prescribed medications to help lower my stroke risk."</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: C</strong></p>
<p class="quiz-rationale"><strong>Rationale:</strong> A TIA is temporary, but it is still serious. Symptoms may resolve, but a TIA can be a warning sign of a future stroke. The client should seek emergency care if symptoms return, not wait to see if they resolve on their own.</p>
</details>

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> Symptoms are sudden, so emergency help should be called right away.</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>E.</strong> Symptoms improve after sitting quietly for several minutes.</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: A, B, C, D</strong></p>

## Question 4

A nurse is caring for a client who has suspected stroke symptoms that began 1 hour ago. Which of the following actions should the nurse expect before thrombolytic therapy is given?

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> Start thickened liquids to prevent aspiration.</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> Obtain a CT scan to rule out hemorrhage.</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> Encourage ambulation to prevent DVT.</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> Place the client in a flat position for cerebral perfusion.</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: B</strong></p>
<p class="quiz-rationale"><strong>Rationale:</strong> A CT scan is needed before thrombolytic therapy to determine whether the stroke is ischemic or hemorrhagic. Thrombolytics may be used for ischemic stroke but are not used for hemorrhagic stroke because they would worsen bleeding.</p>
</details>

## Question 6

A nurse is caring for a client who woke up with left-sided weakness and slurred speech. The client's partner states, "They were fine when they went to sleep last night." Which of the following times should the nurse identify as the client's last known well time?

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> The time the client arrived at the emergency department</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> The time the partner noticed the symptoms this morning</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> The time the client went to sleep without symptoms</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> The time the nurse completed the neurological assessment</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: C</strong></p>
<p class="quiz-rationale"><strong>Rationale:</strong> The last known well time is the last time the client was known to be without stroke symptoms. If the client wakes up with symptoms, the last known well time is when they went to bed without symptoms, not when the symptoms were discovered.</p>
</details>

## Question 7

A nurse is caring for a client who has right-sided weakness after a stroke. Which of the following areas of the brain should the nurse suspect was affected?

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> Left side of the brain</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> Right side of the brain</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: A</strong></p>

## Question 12

A nurse is reinforcing teaching about thrombolytic therapy for a client who is being evaluated for an ischemic stroke. Which of the following statements should the nurse include?

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> "Retavase is used to prevent future clots from forming."</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> "Retavase is a clot-busting medication that helps break down an existing clot."</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> "Retavase is used after hemorrhagic stroke to reduce bleeding around the brain."</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> "Retavase works by thinning the blood slowly over several months."</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: B</strong></p>
<p class="quiz-rationale"><strong>Rationale:</strong> Retavase is a thrombolytic, also called a clot-busting medication. Thrombolytics break down an existing clot. ## Question 13

A nurse is comparing thrombolytic therapy with anticoagulant therapy for a client who had an ischemic stroke. Which of the following statements should the nurse make?

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> "Thrombolytics break down an existing clot, while anticoagulants help prevent new clots from forming."</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> "Thrombolytics and anticoagulants both dissolve clots that are already blocking blood flow."</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> "Anticoagulants break apart the clot quickly, while thrombolytics prevent future clot formation."</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> "Anticoagulants such as rivaroxaban and apixaban are given to burst clots during the first few hours of a stroke."</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: A</strong></p>
<p class="quiz-rationale"><strong>Rationale:</strong> Thrombolytics are clot busters that break down an existing clot. <summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>

A nurse is caring for a client who is eating after being cleared by a swallow screen. Which of the following actions should the nurse take to reduce aspiration risk?

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> Place the client supine after meals.</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> Encourage the client to eat quickly to reduce fatigue.</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> Sit the client upright while eating.</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> Offer thin liquids before each bite of food.</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: C</strong></p>
<p class="quiz-rationale"><strong>Rationale:</strong> After a stroke, dysphagia can increase aspiration risk. When eating is allowed, the client should sit upright and follow the prescribed diet, which may include thickened liquids. The nurse should watch for coughing, choking, drooling, pocketing food, or a wet voice during meals.</p>
</details>
