# Basic Burns Practice Quiz

Test your knowledge of burn priorities, burn depth, airway concerns, fluid shifts, infection prevention, graft care, and burn medications.

---

## Question 1

A nurse is caring for a client who was rescued from a house fire in an enclosed room. The client has facial burns, singed nasal hairs, and a hoarse voice. Which of the following actions should the nurse take **first**?

<p class="answer-choice">A. Prepare for possible early intubation</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. Apply sterile dressings to the facial burns</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. Start calculating total body surface area burned</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. Administer prescribed IV pain medication</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: A</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">Facial burns, singed nasal hairs, and hoarseness are red flags for inhalation injury and airway edema. Airway is the priority because swelling can progress quickly and make intubation difficult later.</p>
</details>

## Question 2

A nurse is teaching a client about home care for a minor superficial burn. Which of the following statements by the client indicates a **need for further teaching**?

<p class="answer-choice">A. "I should avoid breaking any blisters that form."</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. "I can apply ice directly to the burn to stop the burning."</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. "I should avoid putting butter or home remedies on the burn."</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. "I can cool a small burn with cool water."</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: B</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">Ice should not be applied to burns because it can cause vasoconstriction, worsen tissue damage, and increase the risk for hypothermia.</p>
</details>

## Question 3

A nurse is assessing a client with a circumferential burn to the right arm. Which of the following findings requires **immediate notification of the provider**?

<p class="answer-choice">A. The client reports pain at the burn site</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. The burned area appears red and moist</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. The right hand is cool with a weak pulse</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. The client asks for pain medication before dressing changes</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: C</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">A cool extremity with a weak pulse can indicate impaired circulation from swelling and tight burned tissue. Circumferential burns can act like compartment syndrome and require urgent intervention.</p>
</details>

## Question 4

A nurse is caring for a client 60 hours after a major burn injury. Which of the following findings should the nurse identify as an **expected finding during the diuretic phase**?

<p class="answer-choice">A. Increased hematocrit</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. Decreased urine output</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. Increased potassium level</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. Increased urine output</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: D</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">The diuretic phase usually occurs around 48–72 hours as fluid shifts back into the intravascular space. Increased urine output is expected, but the nurse should also monitor for fluid overload and pulmonary edema.</p>
</details>

## Question 5

A nurse is reviewing medications prescribed for clients with burn injuries. Which of the following statements are correct? **Select all that apply.**

<p class="answer-choice">A. Sulfamylon can cause metabolic acidosis.</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. Gentamicin requires monitoring of BUN, creatinine, and urine output.</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. Santyl should continue after all necrotic tissue is gone to prevent infection.</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. Silver nitrate dressings should be kept moist.</p>
<p class="answer-choice">E. Silvadene can cause leukopenia, so WBC count should be monitored.</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: A, B, D, E</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">Sulfamylon can cause metabolic acidosis. Gentamicin is nephrotoxic, so kidney labs and urine output are monitored. Silver nitrate dressings must stay moist. Silvadene can cause leukopenia, so WBC count should be monitored. Santyl should be stopped when debridement is complete because it can damage healthy tissue.</p>
</details>

## Question 6

A nurse is caring for a client who has carbon monoxide poisoning after a house fire. Which of the following prescriptions should the nurse anticipate?

<p class="answer-choice">A. Apply oxygen at 2 L/min by nasal cannula</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. Administer high-flow oxygen</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. Encourage coughing and deep breathing every 2 hr</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. Place the client in Trendelenburg position</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: B</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">Carbon monoxide binds to hemoglobin instead of oxygen, causing tissue hypoxia. The priority treatment is high-flow oxygen to help displace carbon monoxide from hemoglobin and improve oxygen delivery.</p>
</details>

## Question 7

A nurse is assessing a client with a full-thickness burn to the anterior chest. Which of the following findings should the nurse expect?

<p class="answer-choice">A. Red, dry skin with no blisters and severe pain</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. Pink, moist skin with blisters and blanching</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. Dry, leathery skin with little or no pain in the center</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. Wet, bright red tissue with exposed nerve endings</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: C</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">Full-thickness burns destroy the epidermis and dermis. They often appear dry, tight, leathery, white/brown/black/charred, and may have little or no pain in the center because nerve endings are destroyed.</p>
</details>

## Question 8

## Question 10

A nurse is reviewing care for a client after skin grafting for a burn injury. Which of the following interventions should the nurse include? **Select all that apply.**

<p class="answer-choice">A. Immobilize the grafted area as prescribed.</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. Avoid pressure or shearing over the graft site.</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. Pack the graft site tightly to prevent drainage.</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. Monitor the graft for color, drainage, and adherence.</p>
<p class="answer-choice">E. Encourage rubbing the donor site to decrease itching.</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: A, B, D</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">After grafting, the nurse should protect the graft site, immobilize the area as prescribed, avoid pressure or shearing, and monitor color, drainage, and adherence. The graft site should not be packed because packing can damage or kill the graft. Donor sites should be protected from friction.</p>
</details>

## Question 12

A nurse is calculating the total body surface area burned for an adult client using the Rule of Nines. The client has burns to the entire right arm, anterior trunk, and perineum. Which of the following percentages should the nurse document?

<p class="answer-choice">A. 19%</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. 27%</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. 28%</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. 36%</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: C</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">In adults, each arm is 9%, the anterior trunk is 18%, and the perineum is 1%. 9% + 18% + 1% = 28% TBSA.</p>
</details>

## Question 13

A nurse is caring for a child who has burns to the head and upper chest. Which of the following statements by a newly licensed nurse indicates a **need for further teaching**?

<p class="answer-choice">A. "Children have different body proportions than adults."</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. "The Lund and Browder chart is more accurate for children."</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. "A child's head makes up a larger percentage of body surface area."</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. "The adult Rule of Nines is the best method for pediatric burn calculations."</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: D</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">The adult Rule of Nines is less accurate for children because they have different body proportions, especially a larger head percentage and smaller leg percentage. A pediatric burn chart, such as the Lund and Browder chart, is more accurate.</p>
</details>

## Question 14

A nurse is assessing a client during the shock phase after a major burn injury. Which of the following findings should the nurse expect? **Select all that apply.**

<p class="answer-choice">A. Decreased blood pressure</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. Increased urine output</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. Increased hematocrit</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. Increased potassium</p>
<p class="answer-choice">E. Risk for pulmonary edema</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: A, C, D</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">During the shock phase, fluid shifts from the intravascular space into the interstitial space. Expected findings include decreased blood pressure, decreased urine output, increased hematocrit, increased potassium, acidosis, and risk for hypovolemic shock. Pulmonary edema is more concerning during the diuretic phase.</p>
</details>

## Question 15

A nurse is caring for a client with a major burn injury who is entering the diuretic phase. Which of the following findings requires **immediate notification of the provider**?

<p class="answer-choice">A. Urine output increases from 25 mL/hr to 45 mL/hr</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. Hematocrit begins to decrease</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. Potassium level begins to decrease</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. New crackles and shortness of breath develop</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: D</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">During the diuretic phase, fluid shifts back into the intravascular space. Increased urine output, decreased hematocrit, and decreased potassium can be expected. New crackles and shortness of breath can indicate fluid volume overload or pulmonary edema and should be reported immediately.</p>
</details>

## Question 16

A nurse is providing discharge teaching to a client who had a donor site used for a skin graft. Which of the following statements by the client indicates an understanding of the teaching?

<p class="answer-choice">A. "The donor site may be more painful than the graft site."</p>

<p class="answer-choice">C. "The donor site is the area where the new skin was placed."</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. "The donor site should be kept under pressure to help the skin adhere."</p>

## Question 17

A nurse is caring for a client who is receiving silver nitrate dressings for burn wound care. Which of the following assessment findings is most important for the nurse to monitor?

<p class="answer-choice">A. Decreased pain at the burn site</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. Changes in sodium and potassium levels</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. Black discoloration on the dressing</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. Decreased amount of wound drainage</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: B</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">Silver nitrate can cause fluid and electrolyte shifts, so the nurse should monitor electrolytes, especially sodium and potassium. Black discoloration can occur with silver nitrate and does not automatically mean necrosis.</p>
</details>

## Question 18

A nurse is reviewing wound care prescriptions for a client with a burn injury. Which of the following prescriptions should the nurse question?

## Question 19

A nurse is teaching a client about nutrition during recovery from a major burn injury. Which of the following should the nurse include? **Select all that apply.**

<p class="answer-choice">A. Increase protein intake.</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. Increase calorie intake.</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. Include vitamin C for tissue repair.</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. Restrict fluids unless urine output is absent.</p>
<p class="answer-choice">E. Include zinc to support wound healing.</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: A, B, C, E</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">Burns increase metabolic demand. The client needs increased protein and calories, vitamin C for tissue repair, zinc for wound healing, and adequate fluids for hydration and perfusion. Fluids should not be restricted unless specifically prescribed.</p>
</details>

<details>

## Question 26

A nurse is assessing a client who has a burn injury. Which of the following findings should the nurse identify as consistent with a **fourth-degree burn**?

<p class="answer-choice">A. Red, dry skin with pain and no blisters</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. Moist, pink skin with blisters and blanching</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. Dry, leathery tissue with little pain in the wound center</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. Blackened tissue with visible muscle involvement</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: D</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">Fourth-degree burns extend beyond the skin into deeper structures such as muscle, tendon, or bone. The tissue may appear blackened or charred, and severe cases may require specialized treatment or possible amputation.</p>
</details>
