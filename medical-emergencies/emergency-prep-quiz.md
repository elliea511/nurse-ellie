# Emergency Preparedness & Crisis Intervention Practice Quiz

Test your knowledge of ED triage, primary survey priorities, disaster triage, human trafficking recognition, crisis intervention, suicide safety, violence prevention, and emergency preparedness.

---

## Question 1

A nurse is triaging clients in the emergency department. Which of the following clients should the nurse assess **first**?

<p class="answer-choice">A. A client who has chest pain, diaphoresis, and shortness of breath</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. A client who has severe abdominal pain, guarding, and a heart rate of 104/min</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. A client who has an open wrist fracture with moderate bleeding and stable vital signs</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. A client who has a fever, productive cough, and oxygen saturation of 93%</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: A</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">All of these clients need emergency evaluation, but chest pain with diaphoresis and shortness of breath is the highest priority because it may indicate an acute cardiac event with impaired oxygenation and perfusion. Severe abdominal pain, an open fracture, and possible pneumonia are serious, but this client is most likely to become life-threatening first.</p>
</details>

## Question 2

A trauma client arrives in the emergency department after a motor vehicle crash. The nurse notes active, severe bleeding from the client's leg. Which of the following actions should the nurse take **first**?

<p class="answer-choice">A. Assess the client's pupils and level of consciousness</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. Control the bleeding</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. Remove all clothing to assess for additional injuries</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. Obtain the client's full health history</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: B</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">The primary survey usually follows ABCDE, but with excessive bleeding, the priority changes to CAB. Massive hemorrhage can kill quickly, so the nurse should control severe bleeding first.</p>
</details>

## Question 3

A nurse is reviewing disaster triage categories after a mass casualty incident. Which of the following clients should the nurse assign to the **green** category?

<p class="answer-choice">A. A client who is not breathing after the airway is opened</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. A client who has respiratory distress and severe burns to the face</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. A client who is walking and has minor cuts to both arms</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. A client who has an open fracture with stable vital signs</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: C</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">Green is used for the "walking wounded." These clients have minor injuries and can usually wait while more unstable clients receive care first.</p>
</details>

## Question 4

<p class="answer-choice">A. Ask the accompanying person to explain the client's injuries</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. Tell the accompanying person that trafficking is suspected</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. Contact law enforcement before speaking with the client</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. Find a safe way to speak with the client alone</p>

<details>

<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: D</p>

</details>

## Question 5

A nurse is reviewing suicide precautions for a client who reports thoughts of self-harm. Which of the following actions should the nurse include? **Select all that apply.**

<p class="answer-choice">A. Ask the client directly about having a suicide plan.</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. Remove dangerous items from the client's environment.</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. Leave the client alone to decrease stimulation.</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. Document the client's exact statements.</p>
<p class="answer-choice">E. Initiate one-to-one observation if ordered.</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>

## Question 6

A nurse is performing the primary survey on a trauma client after a fall from a roof. The client is awake and moaning. Which of the following actions should the nurse take **first**?

<p class="answer-choice">A. Assess airway patency while maintaining cervical spine alignment</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. Obtain a full set of vital signs and pain rating</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. Inspect the client's back for bruising or bleeding</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. Ask the client about allergies and current medications</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: A</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">During the primary survey, the nurse starts with airway while protecting the cervical spine if trauma is suspected. Vital signs, posterior inspection, and history are important but are part of later assessment after immediate life threats are addressed.</p>
</details>

## Question 7

A nurse is assigning disaster triage tags after a tornado. Which of the following clients should receive a **red** tag?

<p class="answer-choice">A. A client who has a closed ankle fracture and is able to walk with assistance</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. A client who has respiratory distress but has a palpable pulse</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. A client who has an open forearm fracture with stable vital signs</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. A client who has no respirations after the airway is repositioned</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: B</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">A red tag is for a client who has a life-threatening condition but is likely to survive with immediate treatment. Respiratory distress is an immediate priority. A client who is not breathing after the airway is opened is tagged black.</p>
</details>

## Question 8

A nurse is caring for a client in the emergency department who states, "I do not feel safe going home with my partner." Which of the following responses should the nurse make?

<p class="answer-choice">A. "Why did you come to the hospital with them if you feel unsafe?"</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. "I am sure things will improve once everyone calms down."</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. "I believe you, and we will help keep you safe."</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. "You need to call the police before we can help you."</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: C</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">The nurse should believe the client's report and respond in a supportive, nonjudgmental way. The nurse should avoid blaming questions, false reassurance, or placing responsibility on the client before safety is addressed.</p>
</details>

## Question 9

## Question 11

A nurse is teaching a group of staff about disaster response. Which of the following statements by a staff member indicates an understanding of disaster triage?

<p class="answer-choice">A. "Clients who are walking and have minor injuries are treated before clients who cannot walk."</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. "The goal is to save the greatest number of people possible with the resources available."</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. "Clients who are unlikely to survive should receive the most staff and supplies."</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. "Disaster triage uses the same priorities as routine emergency department triage."</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: B</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">Disaster triage focuses on doing the greatest good for the greatest number. This means resources are used where they can help the most people survive.</p>
</details>

## Question 14

A nurse is caring for clients after an external disaster. Which of the following clients should the nurse assign to the **urgent** triage category?

<p class="answer-choice">A. A client who has a minor cut and is able to walk without assistance</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. A client who has no respirations after the airway is repositioned</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. A client who has severe abdominal pain, stable vital signs, and guarding</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. A client who has respiratory distress and weak peripheral pulses</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: C</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">Severe abdominal pain is serious and needs treatment, but if the client is stable and there is no immediate threat to life, the client is categorized as urgent. </details>

A nurse is caring for clients after an external disaster. Which of the following clients should receive a **yellow** disaster triage tag?

## Question 19

A nurse has completed the primary survey and stabilized a trauma client. Which of the following actions should the nurse expect during the **secondary survey**?

<p class="answer-choice">A. Open the airway while maintaining cervical spine alignment</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. Control life-threatening bleeding before assessing breathing</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. Obtain a full health history and perform a head-to-toe assessment</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. Assign the client a red, yellow, green, or black disaster tag</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: C</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">The primary survey is the quick life-threat check using ABCDE, or CAB for massive bleeding. The secondary survey occurs after stabilization and includes head-to-toe assessment, baseline vital signs, history, posterior inspection, labs, imaging, and consults.</p>
</details>

## Question 23

A nurse is caring for a client in the emergency department who speaks a different language than the nurse. The client's adult child offers to translate, but the nurse is concerned about possible abuse. Which of the following actions should the nurse take?

<p class="answer-choice">A. Ask the adult child to translate because they know the client best</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. Use gestures and avoid asking sensitive safety questions</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. Use an authorized translator to communicate with the client</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. Delay assessment until a family member chosen by the client arrives</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: C</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">The nurse should use an authorized translator, especially when safety, abuse, trafficking, or consent may be involved. Family members or visitors should not be used as translators in these situations because the client may not be able to speak freely.</p>
</details>
