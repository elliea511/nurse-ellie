# Hypertensive Crisis

| Topic | Key Point |
|---|---|
| What it is | Blood pressure is dangerously high |
| Common crisis range | Systolic BP 180 or higher and/or diastolic BP 120 or higher |
| Most important question | Is there acute target-organ damage? |
| Main danger | Damage to brain, heart, kidneys, eyes, or blood vessels |
| Priority | Assess symptoms and organ damage, not just the BP number |

The most important question is not just **“How high is the BP?”**

The most important question is:

**Is there acute target-organ damage?**

<div class="tip-box">
<strong>NCLEX Tip:</strong> The number matters, but symptoms and organ damage matter more.
</div>

---

# Hypertensive Emergency vs Hypertensive Urgency

| Type | Meaning | Treatment |
|---|---|---|
| Hypertensive emergency | Severe BP elevation with acute target-organ damage | IV antihypertensives and close monitoring |
| Hypertensive urgency / severe asymptomatic hypertension | Severe BP elevation without acute target-organ damage | Oral meds/adjustment and close follow-up |

## NCLEX Priority Cue

| Scenario | More Concerning? | Why |
|---|---|---|
| BP 190/120 with chest pain, neuro changes, or shortness of breath | Yes | Symptoms suggest target-organ damage |
| BP 190/120 with no symptoms | Still serious, but less emergent | No obvious acute organ damage |

---

# Target Organs

Hypertensive emergency can damage:

| Target Organ | What To Watch For |
|---|---|
| Brain | Stroke symptoms, confusion, seizure, altered LOC |
| Heart | Chest pain, MI symptoms, dysrhythmias, pulmonary edema |
| Kidneys | Decreased urine output, elevated creatinine, protein/blood in urine |
| Eyes | Blurred vision, retinal changes, vision loss |
| Blood vessels | Vascular injury, dissection risk |

---

# Signs of Target-Organ Damage

| System | Signs and Symptoms |
|---|---|
| Brain | Severe headache, confusion, altered mental status, seizures, vision changes, stroke symptoms, weakness, trouble speaking |
| Heart | Chest pain, shortness of breath, pulmonary edema, MI symptoms, dysrhythmias |
| Kidneys | Decreased urine output, elevated creatinine, blood or protein in urine |
| Eyes | Blurred vision, retinal changes, vision loss |

<div class="priority-box">
<strong>Priority Tip:</strong> High BP + chest pain, neuro changes, shortness of breath, seizure, or decreased urine output = possible hypertensive emergency.
</div>

---

# Causes / Risk Factors

| Cause / Risk Factor | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| Chronic hypertension | Long-term vessel damage increases risk |
| Missed BP medications | BP can rebound or become uncontrolled |
| Medication nonadherence | Common cause of severe BP elevation |
| Kidney disease | Kidneys help regulate BP and fluid |
| Pregnancy complications | Can indicate preeclampsia/eclampsia |
| Cocaine or stimulant use | Can sharply increase BP and cardiac risk |
| Severe pain or anxiety | Can raise BP |
| Certain medication interactions | Can worsen BP control |
| Endocrine disorders | Hormonal issues can raise BP |

---

# Assessment for Hypertensive Crisis

## First Assess

| Assessment | Why |
|---|---|
| Recheck BP correctly | Confirms the reading |
| BP in both arms if appropriate | Helps compare circulation |
| Heart rate | Detects compensation/dysrhythmia |
| Respiratory status | Screens for pulmonary edema/SOB |
| Oxygen saturation | Checks oxygenation |
| Chest pain | Possible cardiac injury |
| Neuro status / LOC | Possible brain involvement |
| Vision changes | Possible retinal or neuro involvement |
| Headache | Possible neuro involvement |
| Urine output | Kidney perfusion/damage |
| Medication history | Missed meds may explain crisis |
| Last dose of BP meds | Helps guide treatment |

# Questions To Ask

| Ask About | Why |
|---|---|
| Chest pain | MI/cardiac strain |
| Shortness of breath | Pulmonary edema/heart failure |
| Severe headache | Neuro involvement |
| Confusion | Altered mental status |
| Weakness | Stroke symptoms |
| Vision changes | Eye/brain involvement |
| Decreased urination | Kidney involvement |
| Pregnancy/postpartum status | Preeclampsia/eclampsia risk |
| Cocaine/stimulant use | Can trigger severe BP elevation |
| Missed antihypertensive meds | Common cause of crisis |

---

# Diagnostics

| Test | Why |
|---|---|
| ECG | Checks for MI, ischemia, dysrhythmias |
| Troponin | Checks for cardiac injury |
| BMP/CMP | Checks kidney function and electrolytes |
| Creatinine/BUN | Checks kidney injury |
| Urinalysis | Checks protein or blood in urine |
| Chest x-ray | Checks pulmonary edema or heart enlargement |
| CT head | Used if neuro symptoms are present |
| Eye exam | May show retinal damage |

<div class="tip-box">
<strong>NCLEX Tip:</strong> Neuro symptoms with severe BP may require a CT head to assess for stroke or bleeding.
</div>

---

# Treatment of Hypertensive Emergency

## Main Goal

Lower blood pressure safely and prevent further organ damage.

| Treatment | Purpose |
|---|---|
| IV antihypertensives | Lower BP in a controlled way |
| Continuous or frequent BP monitoring | Prevents BP from dropping too quickly |
| Cardiac monitoring | Watches for dysrhythmias or ischemia |
| Treat affected organ system | Focuses care on brain, heart, kidneys, etc. |
| Monitor kidney function | Detects renal injury |
| Monitor neuro status | Detects stroke/ICP-type changes |
| Monitor urine output | Tracks kidney perfusion |

## Important

Do not drop BP too fast unless there is a specific reason.

A sudden large BP drop can decrease perfusion to the:

| Organ | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| Brain | Can worsen neuro injury |
| Heart | Can worsen ischemia |
| Kidneys | Can worsen kidney injury |

---

# IV Medications That May Be Used

The specific medication depends on the client’s condition.

| Medication | Common Point |
|---|---|
| Labetalol | IV BP control; beta-blocking effect |
| Nicardipine | IV calcium channel blocker |
| Clevidipine | IV calcium channel blocker |
| Esmolol | Short-acting beta blocker |
| Hydralazine | IV vasodilator; also seen in pregnancy-related hypertension |
| Nitroglycerin | Helpful when chest pain/pulmonary edema is present |
| Nitroprusside | Potent IV vasodilator; close monitoring needed |

<div class="tip-box">
<strong>NCLEX Tip:</strong> You usually do not need to memorize every IV BP med. Focus on emergency vs urgency, organ damage, and safe BP reduction.
</div>

---

# Treatment of Hypertensive Urgency / Severe Asymptomatic Hypertension

If there is no acute organ damage, treatment is usually less aggressive.

| Treatment | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Oral antihypertensive medication | Lowers BP more gradually |
| Restart missed medications | Corrects nonadherence cause |
| Adjust home medications | Improves long-term control |
| Monitor BP | Tracks response |
| Close follow-up | Prevents future crisis |

<div class="tip-box">
<strong>NCLEX Tip:</strong> Do not rapidly drop BP in an asymptomatic client unless ordered for a specific reason.
</div>

# Nursing Priorities for Hypertensive Crisis

| Step | Priority |
|---|---|
| 1 | Recheck BP correctly |
| 2 | Assess for symptoms of organ damage |
| 3 | Assess neuro status |
| 4 | Assess chest pain and respiratory status |
| 5 | Place on cardiac monitor if emergency symptoms are present |
| 6 | Establish IV access if hypertensive emergency is suspected |
| 7 | Administer medications as prescribed |
| 8 | Monitor BP frequently |
| 9 | Monitor urine output |
| 10 | Implement safety precautions |

---

# Patient Teaching for Hypertension Prevention

| Teaching Point | Why |
|---|---|
| Take BP medications exactly as prescribed | Prevents rebound/uncontrolled BP |
| Do not stop medications suddenly | Can worsen BP quickly |
| Check BP at home | Helps catch problems early |
| Follow low-sodium diet if prescribed | Helps reduce fluid/BP |
| Avoid smoking | Reduces vessel damage |
| Limit alcohol | Helps BP control |
| Exercise as allowed | Supports cardiovascular health |
| Maintain healthy weight | Reduces BP strain |
| Manage stress | Helps reduce BP spikes |
| Keep follow-up appointments | Allows medication adjustment |
| Report severe headache, chest pain, SOB, weakness, or vision changes immediately | Possible organ damage |

---

# When to Seek Emergency Help

Seek emergency care for high BP with:

| Symptom | Possible Concern |
|---|---|
| Chest pain | MI/cardiac ischemia |
| Shortness of breath | Pulmonary edema/heart failure |
| Severe headache | Brain involvement |
| Confusion | Altered mental status |
| Weakness | Stroke symptom |
| Trouble speaking | Stroke symptom |
| Vision changes | Eye or brain involvement |
| Seizure | Brain involvement |
| Decreased level of consciousness | Brain involvement |
| Decreased urine output | Kidney involvement |
| Stroke symptoms | Brain attack |

# Quick NCLEX Review Chart

| Clue | Think |
|---|---|
| BP around 180/120 | Hypertensive crisis range |
| High BP + organ damage | Hypertensive emergency |
| High BP without symptoms | Hypertensive urgency / severe asymptomatic hypertension |
| Chest pain | Possible cardiac damage |
| Shortness of breath/crackles | Possible pulmonary edema |
| Confusion/seizure/stroke symptoms | Possible brain involvement |
| Decreased urine output | Possible kidney involvement |
| Vision loss/blurred vision | Possible eye/retinal involvement |
| Emergency treatment | IV antihypertensives + close monitoring |
| Urgency treatment | Oral meds/adjustment + follow-up |
| BP dropped too fast | Can decrease brain/heart/kidney perfusion |

---

# Most Important “What Do I Do First?” Points

| Situation | First / Priority Action |
|---|---|
| Severe BP reading | Recheck BP correctly |
| Severe BP + symptoms | Assess for target-organ damage |
| Chest pain or SOB | Assess cardiac/respiratory status and notify provider |
| Neuro changes | Assess LOC/neuro status and prepare for further evaluation |
| Suspected hypertensive emergency | Establish IV access, monitor closely, give IV meds as ordered |
| No organ damage symptoms | Avoid rapid BP drop unless ordered |
| Decreased urine output | Monitor renal function and report concern |
| Client stopped meds | Teach not to stop BP meds suddenly |
