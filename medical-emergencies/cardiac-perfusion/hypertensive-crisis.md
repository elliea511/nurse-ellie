# Hypertensive Crisis

A hypertensive crisis means blood pressure is dangerously high.

It is often around:

* Systolic BP 180 or higher
* Diastolic BP 120 or higher

The most important question is not just “How high is the BP?”

The most important question is:

**Is there acute target-organ damage?**

---

## Hypertensive Emergency vs Hypertensive Urgency

| Type | Meaning | Treatment |
|---|---|---|
| Hypertensive emergency | Severe BP elevation with acute target-organ damage | IV antihypertensives and close monitoring |
| Hypertensive urgency / severe asymptomatic hypertension | Severe BP elevation without acute target-organ damage | Oral meds/adjustment and close follow-up |

<div class="nclex">
<strong>NCLEX Tip:</strong> The number matters, but symptoms and organ damage matter more.
</div>

A BP of 190/120 with chest pain, neuro changes, or shortness of breath is more urgent than a high BP with no symptoms.

---

## Target Organs

Hypertensive emergency can damage:

| Target Organ | Examples of Damage |
|---|---|
| Brain | Stroke symptoms, confusion, seizures, altered mental status |
| Heart | Chest pain, MI symptoms, dysrhythmias, pulmonary edema |
| Kidneys | Decreased urine output, elevated creatinine, blood/protein in urine |
| Eyes | Blurred vision, retinal changes, vision loss |
| Blood vessels | Vascular injury |

---

## Signs of Target-Organ Damage

### Brain

* Severe headache
* Confusion
* Altered mental status
* Seizures
* Vision changes
* Stroke symptoms
* Weakness
* Trouble speaking

### Heart

* Chest pain
* Shortness of breath
* Pulmonary edema
* MI symptoms
* Dysrhythmias

### Kidneys

* Decreased urine output
* Elevated creatinine
* Blood or protein in urine

### Eyes

* Blurred vision
* Retinal changes
* Vision loss

---

## Causes / Risk Factors

| Category | Examples |
|---|---|
| Chronic disease | Chronic hypertension, kidney disease, endocrine disorders |
| Medication-related | Missed BP medications, medication nonadherence, certain medication interactions |
| Pregnancy-related | Pregnancy complications |
| Substance-related | Cocaine or stimulant use |
| Stress response | Severe pain or anxiety |

---

## Assessment for Hypertensive Crisis

### First Assess

| Assessment | Why |
|---|---|
| Blood pressure in both arms if appropriate | Confirms and compares readings |
| Heart rate | Checks cardiac response |
| Respiratory status and oxygen saturation | Screens for pulmonary edema/respiratory distress |
| Chest pain | Possible heart involvement |
| Neuro status and level of consciousness | Possible brain involvement |
| Vision changes and headache | Possible neuro/eye involvement |
| Urine output | Kidney perfusion/damage |
| Medication history and last BP med dose | Missed meds may explain crisis |

### Ask About

| Ask About | Concern |
|---|---|
| Chest pain | MI/cardiac damage |
| Shortness of breath | Pulmonary edema/heart failure |
| Severe headache | Brain involvement |
| Confusion | Altered mental status |
| Weakness or trouble speaking | Stroke symptoms |
| Vision changes | Eye/brain involvement |
| Decreased urination | Kidney involvement |
| Pregnancy/postpartum status | Pregnancy-related hypertension |
| Cocaine/stimulant use | Severe BP elevation trigger |
| Missed antihypertensive medications | Common cause of crisis |

---

## Diagnostics

| Test | Why |
|---|---|
| ECG | Checks for MI, ischemia, dysrhythmias |
| Troponin | Checks for cardiac injury |
| BMP/CMP | Checks kidney function and electrolytes |
| Creatinine/BUN | Checks kidney injury |
| Urinalysis | Checks protein or blood in urine |
| Chest x-ray | Checks pulmonary edema or heart enlargement |
| CT head | If neuro symptoms are present |
| Eye exam | May show retinal damage |

---

## Treatment of Hypertensive Emergency

### Main Goal

Lower blood pressure safely and prevent further organ damage.

### Important

Do not drop BP too fast unless there is a specific reason.

A sudden large BP drop can decrease perfusion to the brain, heart, or kidneys.

### Treatment

| Treatment | Purpose |
|---|---|
| IV antihypertensives | Lowers BP in a controlled setting |
| Continuous or frequent BP monitoring | Prevents BP from dropping too quickly |
| Cardiac monitoring | Watches for dysrhythmias/ischemia |
| Treat the affected organ system | Focuses care on the damaged system |
| Monitor kidney function | Detects renal injury |
| Monitor neuro status | Detects worsening brain involvement |
| Monitor urine output | Tracks kidney perfusion |

---

## IV Medications That May Be Used

Examples include:

- Labetalol
- Nicardipine
- Clevidipine
- Esmolol
- Hydralazine
- Nitroglycerin
- Nitroprusside

The specific medication depends on the client’s condition.

---

## Treatment of Hypertensive Urgency / Severe Asymptomatic Hypertension

If there is no acute organ damage, treatment is usually less aggressive.

Treatment may include:

- Oral antihypertensive medication
- Restarting missed medications
- Adjusting home medications
- Monitoring BP
- Close follow-up

Do not rapidly drop BP in an asymptomatic client unless ordered for a specific reason.

---

## Nursing Priorities for Hypertensive Crisis

| Step | Priority |
|---|---|
| 1 | Recheck BP correctly |
| 2 | Assess for symptoms of organ damage |
| 3 | Assess neuro status |
| 4 | Assess chest pain and respiratory status |
| 5 | Place on cardiac monitor if emergency symptoms are present |
| 6 | Establish IV access if hypertensive emergency is suspected |
| 7 | Administer medications as prescribed |
| 8 | Monitor BP frequently |
| 9 | Monitor urine output |
| 10 | Implement safety precautions |

---

## Patient Teaching for Hypertension Prevention

Teach the client:

* Take BP medications exactly as prescribed.
* Do not stop medications suddenly.
* Check BP at home.
* Follow low-sodium diet if prescribed.
* Avoid smoking.
* Limit alcohol.
* Exercise as allowed.
* Maintain healthy weight.
* Manage stress.
* Keep follow-up appointments.
* Report severe headache, chest pain, SOB, weakness, or vision changes immediately.

---

## When to Seek Emergency Help

The client should seek emergency care for high BP with:

| Emergency Symptom | Possible Concern |
|---|---|
| Chest pain | Heart damage/MI |
| Shortness of breath | Pulmonary edema/heart failure |
| Severe headache | Brain involvement |
| Confusion | Altered mental status |
| Weakness | Stroke symptom |
| Trouble speaking | Stroke symptom |
| Vision changes | Eye/brain involvement |
| Seizure | Brain involvement |
| Decreased level of consciousness | Brain involvement |
| Decreased urine output | Kidney involvement |
| Stroke symptoms | Brain attack |

## Quick NCLEX Review Chart

| Clue | Think |
|---|---|
| BP around 180/120 | Hypertensive crisis range |
| High BP + acute organ damage | Hypertensive emergency |
| High BP without acute organ damage | Hypertensive urgency/severe asymptomatic hypertension |
| Chest pain | Heart involvement |
| Shortness of breath or pulmonary edema | Heart/lung involvement |
| Confusion, seizure, stroke symptoms | Brain involvement |
| Decreased urine output | Kidney involvement |
| Blurred vision or vision loss | Eye involvement |
| Emergency treatment | IV antihypertensives and close monitoring |
| Urgency treatment | Oral meds/adjustment and close follow-up |
| Dropping BP too fast | Can decrease brain, heart, or kidney perfusion |

---

## Most Important “What Do I Do First?” Points

| Situation | First / Priority Action |
|---|---|
| Severe BP reading | Recheck BP correctly |
| Severe BP + symptoms | Assess for target-organ damage |
| Chest pain or shortness of breath | Assess cardiac/respiratory status |
| Neuro changes | Assess LOC/neuro status |
| Suspected hypertensive emergency | Establish IV access and prepare for IV meds as ordered |
| No organ damage symptoms | Avoid rapid BP drop unless ordered |
| Decreased urine output | Monitor renal function and report concern |
| Client stopped meds | Teach not to stop BP meds suddenly |
