# Environmental Emergencies Practice Quiz

Test your knowledge of heat illness, cold injuries, drowning, bites, stings, and altitude sickness with NCLEX-style practice questions.

---

## Question 1

A nurse is caring for a client who has heavy sweating, weakness, dizziness, nausea, pale cool clammy skin, and orthostatic hypotension after working outside in hot weather. Which of the following conditions should the nurse suspect?

<p class="answer-choice">A. Heat exhaustion</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. Heat stroke</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. Hypothermia</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. High altitude sickness</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: A</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">Heat exhaustion is usually caused by dehydration from heavy sweating and not replacing fluids and electrolytes. Findings can include headache, weakness, nausea or vomiting, heavy sweating, pale cool clammy skin, dizziness, tachycardia, and orthostatic hypotension.</p>
</details>

## Question 2

A nurse is caring for a client who has a temperature of 105°F, hot skin, confusion, and seizure activity after being outside in extreme heat. Which of the following actions should the nurse take first?

<p class="answer-choice">A. Encourage the client to drink a sports drink.</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. Begin rapid cooling and support ABCs.</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. Cover the client with warm blankets.</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. Give salt tablets to replace sodium loss.</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: B</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">Heat stroke is a medical emergency. A high temperature with altered mental status, seizures, or loss of consciousness requires rapid cooling and support of ABCs. Oral fluids are not the priority if the client has altered mental status or seizure activity. Salt tablets should not be given.</p>
</details>

## Question 3

## Question 5

A nurse is caring for a client who has a core temperature of 93°F, confusion, muscle weakness, and slurred speech after being outside in cold weather. Which of the following conditions should the nurse suspect?

<p class="answer-choice">A. Hypothermia</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. Frostbite</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. Heat exhaustion</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. High altitude sickness</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: A</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">Hypothermia occurs when core body temperature is below 95°F. Findings can include shivering, slurred speech, mental slowing, confusion, muscle weakness, bradycardia, hypotension, dysrhythmias, and decreased respiratory rate.</p>
</details>

<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: B</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">The nurse should not pop frostbite blisters because this can increase the risk for infection and further tissue damage. ## Question 34

A nurse is reviewing frostbite injuries. The nurse knows which type of frostbite can lead to gangrene and possible amputation?

<p class="answer-choice">B. Mild frostbite with redness and swelling</p>

## Question 35

## Question 37

A nurse is caring for a client who was pulled from the water after a drowning event. Which of the following actions should the nurse take?

<p class="answer-choice">A. Encourage the client to drink fluids to clear the airway.</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. Delay assessment if the client is awake and coughing.</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. Place the client flat and avoid warming measures.</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. Administer oxygen and monitor lung sounds.</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: D</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">Drowning is an oxygenation emergency. The nurse should assess ABCs, administer oxygen, monitor lung sounds and oxygen saturation, prepare for an artificial airway if needed, and monitor for pulmonary edema, ARDS, dysrhythmias, CNS depression, infection, and hypothermia if the water was cold.</p>
