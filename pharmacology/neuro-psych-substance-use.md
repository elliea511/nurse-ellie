# Neurologic, Psychiatric & Substance-Use Medications

<p class="mh-subtitle">Antidepressants, antipsychotics, mood stabilizers, seizure medications, Parkinson’s drugs, and substance-use treatment</p>

ATI commonly tests these medications through **serotonin syndrome, neuroleptic malignant syndrome, extrapyramidal symptoms, lithium toxicity, agranulocytosis, seizures, CNS depression, and dangerous medication interactions.**

### Antidepressants

<div class="detail-grid" markdown="1">

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>SSRIs</strong>

* Fluoxetine
* Sertraline
* Escitalopram
* Common first-line antidepressants

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Most-Tested SSRI Risks</strong>

* Suicidal thoughts early in therapy
* Serotonin syndrome
* Sexual dysfunction
* GI upset
* Sleep changes

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>MAOIs</strong>

* Phenelzine
* Avoid tyramine-rich foods
* Risk for hypertensive crisis
* Requires careful interaction teaching

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Tyramine Foods to Avoid</strong>

* Aged cheese
* Smoked or cured meats
* Pepperoni
* Avocado
* Some fermented foods

</div>

</div>

<div class="detail-table">
  <table>
    <thead>
      <tr>
        <th>Emergency</th>
        <th>Key Findings</th>
      </tr>
    </thead>
    <tbody>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>Serotonin syndrome</strong></td>
        <td>Agitation, confusion, fever, sweating, diarrhea, tremor, hyperreflexia, clonus</td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>Hypertensive crisis</strong></td>
        <td>Severe headache, very high blood pressure, palpitations, chest pain</td>
      </tr>
    </tbody>
  </table>
</div>

<div class="detail-callout" markdown="1">
<strong>ATI Priority:</strong> Hold serotonergic medications and notify the provider for suspected serotonin syndrome.
</div>

<div class="detail-callout" markdown="1">
<strong>Interaction Cue:</strong> Combining an SSRI with sumatriptan, tramadol, linezolid, an MAOI, or St. John’s wort can increase serotonin syndrome risk.
</div>

</div>

### Antipsychotics

<div class="detail-grid" markdown="1">

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>First-Generation Example</strong>

* Chlorpromazine
* Higher risk for EPS
* Photosensitivity
* Anticholinergic effects
* Reduced sweating

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Second-Generation Examples</strong>

* Risperidone
* Paliperidone
* Clozapine
* Lower EPS risk, but higher metabolic risk

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Metabolic Effects</strong>

* Weight gain
* Hyperglycemia
* Hyperlipidemia
* Metabolic syndrome
* Monitor fasting glucose and lipids

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Other Important Effects</strong>

* Hyperprolactinemia
* Gynecomastia
* Orthostatic hypotension
* Sedation
* Anticholinergic effects

</div>

</div>

<div class="detail-table">
  <table>
    <thead>
      <tr>
        <th>Reaction</th>
        <th>Recognition</th>
        <th>Common Response</th>
      </tr>
    </thead>
    <tbody>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>Acute dystonia</strong></td>
        <td>Neck spasm, jaw stiffness, oculogyric crisis</td>
        <td>Benztropine or diphenhydramine</td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>Akathisia</strong></td>
        <td>Severe restlessness, cannot sit still</td>
        <td>Provider may prescribe beta blocker or benzodiazepine</td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>Parkinsonism</strong></td>
        <td>Rigidity, tremor, shuffling gait</td>
        <td>Benztropine or amantadine</td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>Tardive dyskinesia</strong></td>
        <td>Lip smacking, tongue movement, facial grimacing</td>
        <td>Notify provider; may be irreversible</td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>NMS</strong></td>
        <td>High fever, severe rigidity, confusion, unstable vital signs</td>
        <td>Stop antipsychotic and obtain emergency treatment</td>
      </tr>
    </tbody>
  </table>
</div>

<div class="detail-grid" markdown="1">

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Clozapine Danger</strong>

* Agranulocytosis
* Monitor ANC
* Report fever or sore throat
* Also watch for myocarditis and seizures

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Chlorpromazine Teaching</strong>

* Use sunscreen.
* Avoid overheating.
* Increase fluids and fiber.
* Take with food if GI upset occurs.

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Risperidone / Paliperidone</strong>

* Monitor weight and glucose.
* Watch prolactin effects.
* Report abnormal movements.
* Long-acting injections require schedule adherence.

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>NMS Priority</strong>

* Stop medication.
* Notify provider.
* Support airway and circulation.
* Cooling and medications such as dantrolene may be used.

</div>

</div>

<div class="detail-callout" markdown="1">
<strong>ATI Priority:</strong> Fever plus severe muscle rigidity in a client taking an antipsychotic means NMS until proven otherwise.
</div>

</div>

### Mood Stabilizers

<div class="detail-table">
  <table>
    <thead>
      <tr>
        <th>Medication</th>
        <th>Priority Danger</th>
        <th>Key Teaching</th>
      </tr>
    </thead>
    <tbody>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>Lithium</strong></td>
        <td>Neurologic toxicity</td>
        <td>Maintain fluids and consistent sodium intake</td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>Valproic acid</strong></td>
        <td>Hepatotoxicity and pancreatitis</td>
        <td>Report abdominal pain or jaundice</td>
      </tr>
    </tbody>
  </table>
</div>

<div class="detail-grid" markdown="1">

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Lithium Early Toxicity</strong>

* Nausea
* Diarrhea
* Fine tremor
* Weakness
* Drowsiness

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Lithium Worsening Toxicity</strong>

* Coarse tremor
* Ataxia
* Confusion
* Slurred speech
* Severe vomiting or diarrhea

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Lithium Teaching</strong>

* Maintain consistent sodium intake.
* Drink adequate fluids.
* Avoid dehydration.
* Report vomiting, diarrhea, or heavy sweating.
* Take with meals or milk if needed.

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Valproic Acid Monitoring</strong>

* Liver function
* Platelets
* Abdominal symptoms
* Signs of pancreatitis

</div>

</div>

<div class="detail-callout" markdown="1">
<strong>ATI Priority:</strong> Severe abdominal pain, jaundice, anorexia, or repeated vomiting with valproic acid can indicate organ injury.
</div>

<div class="detail-callout" markdown="1">
<strong>Do Not Miss:</strong> Low sodium and dehydration increase lithium toxicity.
</div>

</div>

### Seizure Medications

<div class="detail-table">
  <table>
    <thead>
      <tr>
        <th>Medication</th>
        <th>Most-Tested Risk</th>
        <th>Key Teaching</th>
      </tr>
    </thead>
    <tbody>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>Phenytoin</strong></td>
        <td>Gingival hyperplasia, toxicity, IV cardiac effects</td>
        <td>Good oral hygiene; do not stop abruptly</td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>Carbamazepine</strong></td>
        <td>Bone marrow suppression, Stevens-Johnson syndrome</td>
        <td>Report fever, sore throat, bruising, or rash</td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>Levetiracetam</strong></td>
        <td>Behavioral changes, suicidal thoughts, drowsiness</td>
        <td>Report mood or behavior changes</td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>Valproic acid</strong></td>
        <td>Liver injury, pancreatitis, thrombocytopenia</td>
        <td>Keep liver and platelet monitoring appointments</td>
      </tr>
    </tbody>
  </table>
</div>

<div class="detail-grid" markdown="1">

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Phenytoin Safety</strong>

* Monitor serum levels as ordered.
* Encourage regular dental care.
* IV administration can cause hypotension and dysrhythmias.
* Do not discontinue abruptly.

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Carbamazepine Safety</strong>

* Monitor CBC.
* Report infection symptoms.
* Report unusual bruising.
* Stop and notify for severe rash as ordered.

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Levetiracetam Safety</strong>

* Can cause drowsiness.
* Watch for aggression or depression.
* Report suicidal thoughts.
* Use fall precautions when needed.

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Class Teaching</strong>

* Take consistently.
* Do not stop suddenly.
* Avoid driving until effects are known.
* Maintain seizure precautions as indicated.

</div>

</div>

<div class="detail-callout" markdown="1">
<strong>ATI Priority:</strong> A new rash with carbamazepine can indicate Stevens-Johnson syndrome and requires immediate follow-up.
</div>

</div>

### Parkinson’s and Spasticity Medications

<div class="detail-grid" markdown="1">

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Levodopa/Carbidopa</strong>

* Improves movement and ability to perform ADLs.
* Carbidopa reduces peripheral breakdown of levodopa.
* Does not cure Parkinson’s disease.

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Common Effects</strong>

* Orthostatic hypotension
* Nausea
* Dry mouth
* Tachycardia
* Vivid dreams
* Hallucinations

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Expected Teaching</strong>

* Urine, sweat, and saliva may darken.
* Change positions slowly.
* Report severe hallucinations.
* Abnormal involuntary movements can occur.

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Baclofen</strong>

* Decreases muscle spasticity.
* Can cause drowsiness, fatigue, and confusion.
* Use fall precautions.
* Do not stop abruptly.

</div>

</div>

<div class="detail-callout" markdown="1">
<strong>ATI Cue:</strong> The therapeutic effect of levodopa/carbidopa is improved movement, not increased alertness or seizure control.
</div>

</div>

### Substance-Use Medications

<div class="detail-grid" markdown="1">

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Disulfiram</strong>

* Causes an unpleasant reaction with alcohol.
* Supports abstinence.
* Alcohol must be avoided in drinks, medicines, and products.

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Disulfiram Reaction</strong>

* Nausea and vomiting
* Severe headache
* Flushing
* Palpitations
* Hypotension
* Respiratory depression in severe cases

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Naltrexone</strong>

* Helps reduce alcohol or opioid cravings.
* Used after detoxification.
* Client must be opioid-free before starting for opioid-use treatment.

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Withdrawal Priority</strong>

* Severe alcohol withdrawal can cause seizures.
* Implement seizure precautions.
* Prevent falls and injury.
* Support airway and vital signs.

</div>

</div>

<div class="detail-callout" markdown="1">
<strong>ATI Priority:</strong> During severe alcohol withdrawal, seizure and injury prevention come before long-term relapse teaching.
</div>

</div>

### Anticholinergic and Motion-Sickness Medications

<div class="detail-grid" markdown="1">

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Common Examples</strong>

* Scopolamine
* Dimenhydrinate
* Oxybutynin
* Ipratropium

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Anticholinergic Effects</strong>

* Dry mouth
* Blurred vision
* Constipation
* Urinary retention
* Tachycardia
* Photophobia

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Scopolamine Patch</strong>

* Apply behind the ear.
* Use a hairless area.
* Replace every 3 days.
* Remove the old patch first.

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Contraindication Cue</strong>

* BPH increases urinary-retention risk.
* Older adults have higher confusion and fall risk.
* Combining anticholinergics intensifies effects.

</div>

</div>

<div class="detail-callout" markdown="1">
<strong>ATI Tip:</strong> Dry mouth, blurred vision, constipation, and urinary retention usually point to anticholinergic effects.
</div>

</div>

### Migraine Medications

<div class="detail-grid" markdown="1">

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Sumatriptan</strong>

* Used to abort migraine attacks.
* Causes vasoconstriction.
* Not used for routine prevention.

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Priority Danger</strong>

* Chest pain
* Chest tightness
* Coronary vasospasm
* Serious cardiovascular symptoms

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Other Effects</strong>

* Drowsiness
* Dizziness
* Visual changes
* Possible bronchospasm

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Interaction</strong>

* Fluoxetine and other serotonergic drugs can increase serotonin syndrome risk.
* Report tremor, confusion, fever, or hallucinations.

</div>

</div>

<div class="detail-callout" markdown="1">
<strong>ATI Priority:</strong> Chest pain or tightness after sumatriptan can indicate vasospasm and requires immediate follow-up.
</div>

</div>

<div class="mh-columns wide-left" markdown="1">

<div class="key-findings" markdown="1">
<h3>📋 Neuro & Psych Big Picture</h3>

* SSRIs can cause serotonin syndrome and increased suicide risk early in therapy.
* MAOIs require tyramine restriction.
* Antipsychotics can cause EPS, tardive dyskinesia, metabolic syndrome, and NMS.
* Clozapine can cause agranulocytosis.
* Lithium toxicity worsens with dehydration and low sodium.
* Valproic acid can cause liver injury and pancreatitis.
* Seizure medications should not be stopped abruptly.
* Levodopa/carbidopa improves movement but can cause hallucinations and orthostatic hypotension.
* Disulfiram causes a severe reaction with alcohol.
* Anticholinergics cause dry mouth, blurred vision, constipation, and urinary retention.

</div>

<div class="mh-col" markdown="1">

<div class="report-now" markdown="1">
<h3>Report Immediately</h3>

* Fever, rigidity, confusion, or unstable vital signs
* Fever or sore throat while taking clozapine
* Coarse tremor, ataxia, or severe diarrhea with lithium
* Severe abdominal pain or jaundice with valproic acid
* New rash with carbamazepine
* Suicidal thoughts or major behavior changes
* Chest pain after sumatriptan
* Respiratory depression after alcohol exposure with disulfiram
* Severe urinary retention or confusion from anticholinergics

</div>

<div class="nclex-tip" markdown="1">
<h3>ATI Tip</h3>

Differentiate **expected effects** from emergencies: mild dry mouth is expected, but fever with rigidity, severe rash, coarse tremor, or agranulocytosis requires immediate action.

</div>

</div>

<div class="mh-table-card" markdown="1">

<h3>Quick Emergency Comparison</h3>

| Emergency                      | Key Clue                                             |
| ------------------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------- |
| **Serotonin syndrome**         | Hyperreflexia, clonus, agitation, sweating, diarrhea |
| **NMS**                        | Severe rigidity, high fever, altered mental status   |
| **Lithium toxicity**           | Coarse tremor, ataxia, confusion, severe GI symptoms |
| **Agranulocytosis**            | Fever and sore throat                                |
| **Tardive dyskinesia**         | Lip smacking and tongue movements                    |
| **Stevens-Johnson syndrome**   | New widespread or blistering rash                    |
| **Sumatriptan vasospasm**      | Chest pain or tightness                              |
| **Severe disulfiram reaction** | Hypotension or respiratory depression after alcohol  |

</div>

<div class="mh-columns" markdown="1">

<div class="quick-review" markdown="1">
<h3>Quick Review</h3>

1. Monitor suicide risk when antidepressant therapy begins.
2. Avoid tyramine with MAOIs.
3. Stop antipsychotics and seek emergency treatment for NMS.
4. Treat acute dystonia with benztropine or diphenhydramine.
5. Maintain consistent sodium and fluid intake with lithium.
6. Report severe abdominal pain with valproic acid.
7. Never abruptly stop seizure medications.
8. Report chest pain after sumatriptan.

</div>

<div class="do-not-miss" markdown="1">
<h3>Do Not Miss</h3>

* Do not confuse NMS with serotonin syndrome.
* Do not dismiss fever or sore throat with clozapine.
* Do not assume all tremors with lithium are harmless.
* Do not ignore abnormal mouth or tongue movements.
* Do not allow alcohol with disulfiram.
* Do not stop anticonvulsants suddenly.
* Do not combine multiple CNS depressants without considering respiratory risk.

</div>
