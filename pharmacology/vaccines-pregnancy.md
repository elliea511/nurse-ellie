### Infant and Childhood Vaccines

<div class="detail-table">
  <table>
    <thead>
      <tr>
        <th>Vaccine</th>
        <th>High-Yield Timing</th>
        <th>ATI Cue</th>
      </tr>
    </thead>
    <tbody>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>Hepatitis B</strong></td>
        <td>First dose at birth</td>
        <td>Newborns receive it regardless of maternal titer unless specific prophylaxis changes are ordered.</td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>Rotavirus</strong></td>
        <td>Begins at about 2 months</td>
        <td>Oral live vaccine; not given at birth.</td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>Influenza</strong></td>
        <td>Annually beginning at 6 months</td>
        <td>Yearly vaccination is expected.</td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>Varicella</strong></td>
        <td>First dose at 12–15 months</td>
        <td>Live vaccine; avoid in pregnancy and many immunocompromised clients.</td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>MMR</strong></td>
        <td>First dose at 12–15 months</td>
        <td>Live vaccine.</td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>Hepatitis A</strong></td>
        <td>Series begins at 12–23 months</td>
        <td>Not a live vaccine.</td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>HPV</strong></td>
        <td>Routine vaccination at 11–12 years</td>
        <td>Prevents HPV-related cancers and genital warts.</td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>Hib</strong></td>
        <td>Series during infancy</td>
        <td>Not a live vaccine.</td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>PCV</strong></td>
        <td>Series during infancy</td>
        <td>Not a live vaccine.</td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>IPV</strong></td>
        <td>Series during infancy and early childhood</td>
        <td>Inactivated polio vaccine.</td>
      </tr>
    </tbody>
  </table>
</div>

<div class="detail-callout" markdown="1">
<strong>ATI Priority:</strong> The newborn receives hepatitis B at birth. Rotavirus, MMR, and varicella begin later.

### Live Vaccines

<div class="detail-grid" markdown="1">

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Common Live Vaccines</strong>

* MMR
* Varicella
* Herpes zoster
* Rotavirus
* Intranasal influenza vaccine

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Avoid In</strong>

* Pregnancy
* Severe immunosuppression
* Prior anaphylaxis to a vaccine or its component

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Why They Matter</strong>

* Live organisms can replicate.
* Healthy clients usually tolerate them safely.
* Immunocompromised clients may develop infection.

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>ATI Recognition</strong>

* MMR = live
* Varicella = live
* Zoster = live vaccine precaution
* Pregnancy = do not give live vaccines

</div>

</div>

<div class="detail-table">
  <table>
    <thead>
      <tr>
        <th>Vaccine</th>

        <th>Immunocompromised Client</th>
      </tr>
    </thead>
    <tbody>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>MMR</strong></td>
        <td>Contraindicated</td>
        <td>Usually contraindicated with significant immunosuppression</td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>Varicella</strong></td>
        <td>Contraindicated</td>
        <td>Usually contraindicated with significant immunosuppression</td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>Herpes zoster</strong></td>
        <td>Contraindicated</td>
        <td>Depends on product and immune status; provider review required</td>
      </tr>

        <td><strong>Hepatitis A</strong></td>
        <td>Not live</td>
        <td>May be given if otherwise indicated</td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>Hib / PCV / IPV</strong></td>
        <td>Not live</td>
        <td>May generally be given if indicated</td>
      </tr>
    </tbody>
  </table>
</div>

<div class="detail-callout" markdown="1">
<strong>ATI Tip:</strong> When a pregnant or severely immunocompromised client is listed, immediately check whether the vaccine is live.
</div>

### Pregnancy Safety

<div class="detail-grid" markdown="1">

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Vaccines Considered Safe</strong>

* Tdap is preferred during pregnancy.
* Inactivated influenza vaccine is generally used.
* Td may be used when indicated.

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Vaccines to Avoid</strong>

* MMR
* Varicella
* Herpes zoster
* Other live vaccines

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>High-Yield Medications to Avoid</strong>

* Isotretinoin
* ACE inhibitors
* ARBs
* Warfarin
* Methotrexate

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Also High Risk in Pregnancy</strong>

* Valproic acid
* Tetracyclines
* Statins
* Live vaccines
* Other teratogenic medications

</div>

</div>

<div class="detail-table">
  <table>
    <thead>
      <tr>
        <th>Medication / Vaccine</th>
        <th>Why It Matters</th>
      </tr>
    </thead>
    <tbody>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>Isotretinoin</strong></td>
        <td>Severe birth-defect risk; requires pregnancy prevention and testing.</td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>ACE inhibitors / ARBs</strong></td>
        <td>Can harm fetal kidney development.</td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>Warfarin</strong></td>
        <td>Teratogenic and associated with fetal bleeding.</td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>Methotrexate</strong></td>
        <td>Interferes with rapidly dividing cells and is highly teratogenic.</td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>Valproic acid</strong></td>
        <td>Associated with major fetal malformations.</td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>Tetracyclines</strong></td>
        <td>Can affect fetal teeth and bone development.</td>
      </tr>
    </tbody>
  </table>
</div>

<div class="detail-callout" markdown="1">
<strong>ATI Priority:</strong> Live vaccines are contraindicated during pregnancy. Tdap is the preferred tetanus-containing vaccine during pregnancy.
</div>

</div>

### Immunocompromised Clients

<div class="detail-grid" markdown="1">

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Avoid</strong>

* Live vaccines during significant immunosuppression
* MMR
* Varicella
* Live intranasal influenza

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Often Allowed</strong>

* Hepatitis A
* Hepatitis B
* Hib
* PCV
* IPV
* Inactivated influenza

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Why</strong>

* Inactivated vaccines cannot replicate.
* Live vaccines may cause infection when immunity is weak.

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Nursing Check</strong>

* Current immune status
* Chemotherapy or immunosuppressant use
* Recent transplant
* Provider instructions

</div>

</div>

<div class="detail-callout" markdown="1">
<strong>ATI Cue:</strong> An immunocompromised infant can still receive inactivated vaccines such as HepA, PCV, Hib, and IPV when scheduled.
</div>

</div>

### Vaccine Allergies

<div class="detail-grid" markdown="1">

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Major Contraindication</strong>

* Anaphylaxis after a previous dose
* Anaphylaxis to a vaccine component

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Common Components Tested</strong>

* Neomycin
* Gelatin
* Egg protein for selected vaccines
* Other product-specific ingredients

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Varicella Vaccine</strong>

* Contains neomycin and gelatin.
* Severe hypersensitivity to either is a contraindication.

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Assessment Before Giving</strong>

* Prior vaccine reactions
* Known anaphylaxis
* Pregnancy status
* Immune status
* Current moderate or severe illness

</div>

</div>

<div class="detail-callout" markdown="1">
<strong>ATI Priority:</strong> A severe neomycin or gelatin allergy matters before varicella vaccination.
</div>

</div>

<div class="mh-columns wide-left" markdown="1">

<div class="key-findings" markdown="1">
<h3>📋 Vaccines & Pregnancy Big Picture</h3>

* Hepatitis B begins at birth.
* Influenza begins annually at 6 months.
* MMR and varicella begin around 12–15 months.
* Hepatitis A begins around 12–23 months.
* HPV is routinely given around 11–12 years.
* MMR, varicella, rotavirus, and zoster are live-vaccine categories.
* Live vaccines are avoided during pregnancy.
* Significant immunosuppression usually means avoiding live vaccines.
* Tdap is recommended during pregnancy.
* Prior anaphylaxis to a vaccine or component is a contraindication.

</div>

<div class="mh-col" markdown="1">

<div class="report-now" markdown="1">
<h3>Report Immediately</h3>

* Wheezing or throat tightness after vaccination
* Facial or tongue swelling
* Hypotension
* Generalized hives with breathing symptoms
* Severe altered mental status or seizure
* Signs of anaphylaxis

</div>

<div class="nclex-tip" markdown="1">
<h3>ATI Tip</h3>

Most vaccine questions can be solved by checking three things: **age, live-vaccine status, and contraindications.**

</div>

</div>

<div class="mh-columns" markdown="1">

<div class="quick-review" markdown="1">
<h3>Quick Review</h3>

1. Give hepatitis B at birth.
2. Begin annual influenza vaccination at 6 months.
3. MMR and varicella are live vaccines.
4. Avoid live vaccines during pregnancy.
5. Avoid live vaccines with significant immunosuppression.
6. Tdap is recommended during pregnancy.
7. Check prior anaphylaxis before vaccination.
8. Neomycin and gelatin allergies matter for varicella.

</div>

<div class="do-not-miss" markdown="1">
<h3>Do Not Miss</h3>

* Do not give MMR or varicella during pregnancy.
* Do not assume every vaccine is live.
* Do not skip routine inactivated vaccines solely because a client is immunocompromised.
* Do not confuse rotavirus timing with hepatitis B timing.
* Do not ignore a prior anaphylactic reaction.
* Do not treat local soreness as the same as anaphylaxis.

</div>
