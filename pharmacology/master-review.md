### Never-Do Medication Safety Rules

<div class="detail-table">
  <table>
    <thead>
      <tr>
        <th>Never Do This</th>
        <th>Correct Principle</th>
      </tr>
    </thead>
    <tbody>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>Give potassium IV push</strong></td>
        <td>Dilute and infuse with a pump.</td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>Crush extended-release or enteric-coated tablets</strong></td>
        <td>Keep the dosage form intact unless verified safe.</td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>Give benzathine penicillin IV</strong></td>
        <td>It is administered IM only.</td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>Leave a controlled substance at the bedside</strong></td>
        <td>Keep it attended and secure.</td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>Waste a controlled substance without a witness</strong></td>
        <td>Use required witnessed disposal.</td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>Continue a transfusion during a suspected reaction</strong></td>
        <td>Stop the transfusion first.</td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>Document an incident report in the chart</strong></td>
        <td>Document objective client facts only.</td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>Use room number as a client identifier</strong></td>
        <td>Use two client-specific identifiers.</td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>Give rapid insulin without food available</strong></td>
        <td>Match the dose to meal availability.</td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>Give a live vaccine during pregnancy</strong></td>
        <td>Use pregnancy-safe vaccine guidance.</td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>Write a trailing zero</strong></td>
        <td>Write 5 mg, not 5.0 mg.</td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>Guess at an unclear order</strong></td>
        <td>Clarify before administering.</td>
      </tr>
    </tbody>
  </table>

<div class="key-findings" markdown="1">
<h3>📋 Last-Minute Big Picture</h3>

* Respiratory depression is the priority opioid danger.
* Hypoglycemia is the priority insulin danger.
* Hypokalemia increases digoxin toxicity.
* Low sodium and dehydration increase lithium toxicity.
* Fever and sore throat can signal agranulocytosis.
* Severe rigidity and high fever suggest NMS.
* Hyperreflexia and clonus suggest serotonin syndrome.
* Aminoglycosides damage kidneys and hearing.
* Fluoroquinolones can rupture tendons.
* Severe abdominal pain can indicate pancreatitis or liver injury.
* Anticoagulants require bleeding precautions.
* Medication safety comes before paperwork.

</div>

<div class="mh-col" markdown="1">

<div class="report-now" markdown="1">
<h3>Report Immediately</h3>

* Respiratory depression
* Severe bradycardia or hypotension
* Angioedema
* New dysrhythmia
* Uncontrolled bleeding
* Severe abdominal pain
* Markedly decreased urine output
* Fever with sore throat
* High fever with severe rigidity
* Coarse tremor or ataxia
* Hearing loss
* New severe rash

</div>

<div class="nclex-tip" markdown="1">
<h3>ATI Tip</h3>

Do not choose an answer only because it is medically correct. Choose the action that addresses the **most immediate safety threat at the correct time**.

<div class="quick-review" markdown="1">
<h3>Final Quick Review</h3>

1. Identify the medication class.
2. Find the biggest safety risk.
3. Check the required vital sign or laboratory value.
4. Decide whether the medication should be given or held.
5. Recognize the antidote if one exists.
6. Prioritize airway, breathing, circulation, neurologic status, and bleeding.
7. Separate expected effects from toxicity.
8. Choose the action that prevents harm first.

</div>

<div class="do-not-miss" markdown="1">
<h3>Do Not Miss</h3>

* Respirations before opioids
* Pulse before beta blockers and digoxin
* Meal availability before rapid insulin
* Potassium with diuretics, ACE inhibitors, and digoxin
* Kidney function with aminoglycosides, metformin, and amphotericin B
* CBC with clozapine, antithyroid drugs, and zidovudine
* Liver function with valproic acid, isoniazid, and methotrexate
* Pregnancy status before live vaccines and teratogenic drugs
