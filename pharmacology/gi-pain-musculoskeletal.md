# GI, Pain & Musculoskeletal Medications

<p class="mh-subtitle">Opioids, NSAIDs, proton pump inhibitors, gout medications, muscle relaxants, and osteoporosis drugs</p>

ATI commonly tests these medications through **respiratory depression, GI bleeding, cardiovascular risk, correct administration, constipation, sedation, and severe abdominal symptoms.**

### Opioids

<div class="detail-grid" markdown="1">

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Common Examples</strong>

* Morphine
* Oxycodone
* Hydromorphone
* Fentanyl
* Codeine

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Priority Danger</strong>

* Respiratory depression
* Low oxygen saturation
* Excessive sedation
* Hypotension
* Aspiration risk

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Common Adverse Effects</strong>

* Constipation
* Nausea
* Drowsiness
* Urinary retention
* Orthostatic hypotension

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Monitor</strong>

* Respiratory rate
* Oxygen saturation
* Sedation level
* Blood pressure
* Pain level
* Bowel function

</div>

</div>

<div class="detail-table">
  <table>
    <thead>
      <tr>
        <th>Situation</th>
        <th>Nursing Action</th>
      </tr>
    </thead>
    <tbody>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>Low respiratory rate</strong></td>
        <td>Hold opioid, support breathing, notify provider, and prepare naloxone.</td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>Severe sedation</strong></td>
        <td>Reassess before another dose and implement fall precautions.</td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>Constipation</strong></td>
        <td>Use fluids, fiber, activity, and a stool softener if prescribed.</td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>BPH or urinary hesitancy</strong></td>
        <td>Risk for urinary retention is increased.</td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>Breakthrough cancer pain</strong></td>
        <td>May require an additional prescribed dose despite scheduled medication.</td>
      </tr>
    </tbody>
  </table>
</div>

<div class="detail-callout" markdown="1">
<strong>Antidote:</strong> Naloxone.
</div>

<div class="detail-callout" markdown="1">
<strong>ATI Priority:</strong> Respiratory depression is more urgent than nausea, constipation, or routine sedation.
</div>

</div>

### NSAIDs and Aspirin

<div class="detail-grid" markdown="1">

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Common Examples</strong>

* Ibuprofen
* Naproxen
* Celecoxib
* Aspirin

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Priority Risks</strong>

* GI bleeding
* Kidney injury
* Fluid retention
* Hypertension
* Cardiovascular events

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Report</strong>

* Black stools
* Vomiting blood
* Severe abdominal pain
* Decreased urine output
* Chest pain
* Unusual bruising

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Patient Teaching</strong>

* Take with food when appropriate.
* Avoid combining multiple NSAIDs.
* Limit alcohol.
* Report bleeding or kidney changes.

</div>

</div>

<div class="detail-table">
  <table>
    <thead>
      <tr>
        <th>Medication</th>
        <th>Most-Tested ATI Cue</th>
      </tr>
    </thead>
    <tbody>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>Celecoxib</strong></td>
        <td>Increases myocardial-infarction risk and is contraindicated with significant heart disease.</td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>Aspirin</strong></td>
        <td>Avoid in children or adolescents with viral illness because of Reye syndrome.</td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>Aspirin toxicity</strong></td>
        <td>Tinnitus, sweating, nausea, and rapid breathing.</td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>Allergy history</strong></td>
        <td>Celecoxib can cause reactions in clients with sulfonamide or salicylate allergy.</td>
      </tr>
    </tbody>
  </table>
</div>

<div class="detail-callout" markdown="1">
<strong>ATI Priority:</strong> Black stools, hematemesis, or chest pain are more urgent than routine GI upset.
</div>

</div>

### Proton Pump Inhibitors

<div class="detail-grid" markdown="1">

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Common Examples</strong>

* Omeprazole
* Esomeprazole
* Pantoprazole
* Medication names often end in **-prazole**

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>What They Do</strong>

* Suppress gastric-acid secretion.
* Treat GERD and ulcers.
* Promote healing.

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Administration</strong>

* Take before the first meal of the day.
* Swallow delayed-release forms whole.
* Do not crush or chew.
* Food can reduce absorption.

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Long-Term Risks</strong>

* Osteoporosis and fractures
* Low magnesium
* Vitamin B12 deficiency
* Increased infection risk

</div>

</div>

<div class="detail-callout" markdown="1">
<strong>ATI Priority:</strong> Long-term therapy should use the lowest effective dose for the shortest necessary duration.
</div>

</div>

### Gout Medications

<div class="detail-grid" markdown="1">

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Colchicine</strong>

* Used for acute gout inflammation.
* Decreases inflammatory response.
* Does not directly relieve pain like an opioid.

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Common Effects</strong>

* Nausea
* Vomiting
* Diarrhea
* Abdominal cramping

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Priority Danger</strong>

* Severe abdominal pain
* Significant diarrhea
* GI cellular injury
* Bone marrow toxicity with severe exposure

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Hold / Report</strong>

* Severe abdominal pain
* Persistent diarrhea
* Weakness
* Signs of bleeding or infection

</div>

</div>

<div class="detail-callout" markdown="1">
<strong>ATI Priority:</strong> Severe abdominal pain can indicate GI cellular damage and requires the medication to be stopped and the provider notified.
</div>

</div>

### Muscle Relaxants

<div class="detail-grid" markdown="1">

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Baclofen</strong>

* Antispasmodic
* Decreases muscle spasticity
* Often used with multiple sclerosis or spinal conditions

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Common Adverse Effects</strong>

* Drowsiness
* Fatigue
* Weakness
* Confusion
* Dizziness

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Patient Teaching</strong>

* Avoid driving until effects are known.
* Avoid alcohol and other CNS depressants.
* Use fall precautions.
* Do not stop suddenly.

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Therapeutic Effect</strong>

* Reduced muscle spasms
* Improved comfort
* Improved mobility
* Not increased mental alertness

</div>

</div>

<div class="detail-callout" markdown="1">
<strong>ATI Cue:</strong> The expected benefit of baclofen is decreased spasticity, not improved alertness or decreased heart rate.
</div>

</div>

### Osteoporosis Medications

<div class="detail-grid" markdown="1">

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Alendronate</strong>

* Bisphosphonate
* Reduces bone loss
* Used for osteoporosis

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Correct Administration</strong>

* Take immediately after arising.
* Take on an empty stomach.
* Use 240 mL or 8 oz plain water.
* Swallow whole.

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>After Taking</strong>

* Sit or stand upright for at least 30 minutes.
* Wait at least 30 minutes before eating or drinking.
* Avoid antacids close to the dose.
* Do not lie down.

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Priority Danger</strong>

* Esophageal irritation
* Esophageal ulceration
* Chest pain
* Difficulty swallowing

</div>

</div>

<div class="detail-callout" markdown="1">
<strong>ATI Priority:</strong> Alendronate questions are usually testing prevention of esophageal injury.
</div>

</div>

<div class="mh-columns wide-left" markdown="1">

<div class="key-findings" markdown="1">
<h3>📋 GI, Pain & Musculoskeletal Big Picture</h3>

* Opioids can cause life-threatening respiratory depression.
* Opioids also commonly cause constipation, sedation, hypotension, and urinary retention.
* NSAIDs can cause GI bleeding and kidney injury.
* Celecoxib increases cardiovascular risk.
* Aspirin can cause Reye syndrome and salicylate toxicity.
* PPIs are taken before meals and delayed-release forms should not be crushed.
* Long-term PPI therapy can increase fracture risk.
* Severe abdominal pain with colchicine can indicate toxicity.
* Baclofen reduces spasticity but causes CNS depression.
* Alendronate must be taken upright with plain water.

</div>

<div class="mh-col" markdown="1">

<div class="report-now" markdown="1">
<h3>Report Immediately</h3>

* Low respiratory rate or oxygen saturation
* Difficult to arouse
* Black stools or vomiting blood
* Chest pain while taking celecoxib
* Tinnitus with aspirin
* Severe persistent abdominal pain
* Markedly decreased urine output
* Chest pain or trouble swallowing after alendronate
* Severe confusion or weakness with baclofen

</div>

<div class="nclex-tip" markdown="1">
<h3>ATI Tip</h3>

When pain-medication findings are listed together, prioritize **respiratory depression, severe hypotension, GI bleeding, and altered level of consciousness**.

</div>

</div>

<div class="mh-columns" markdown="1">

<div class="quick-review" markdown="1">
<h3>Quick Review</h3>

1. Respiratory status comes first with opioids.
2. Naloxone reverses opioid effects.
3. NSAIDs can cause GI bleeding and kidney injury.
4. Aspirin toxicity often begins with tinnitus.
5. PPIs are usually taken before the first meal.
6. Do not crush delayed-release PPI forms.
7. Severe abdominal pain with colchicine requires follow-up.
8. Alendronate requires plain water and upright positioning.

</div>

<div class="do-not-miss" markdown="1">
<h3>Do Not Miss</h3>

* Do not give another opioid dose to a client with respiratory depression.
* Do not combine opioids with alcohol or sedating antihistamines without considering CNS depression.
* Do not ignore black stools with NSAID use.
* Do not give aspirin to a child with a viral illness.
* Do not crush delayed-release medications.
* Do not dismiss severe colchicine GI symptoms.
* Do not abruptly stop baclofen.
* Do not allow a client to lie down after alendronate.

</div>
