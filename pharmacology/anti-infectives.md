# Anti-Infective Medications

<p class="mh-subtitle">Antibiotics, antivirals, antifungals, tuberculosis medications, allergies, and class-specific toxicities</p>

ATI usually tests anti-infective medications by asking about **allergic reactions, nephrotoxicity, ototoxicity, tendon rupture, infusion reactions, liver injury, completing therapy, and medication-specific patient teaching.**

### Penicillins and Cephalosporins

<div class="detail-grid" markdown="1">

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Common Examples</strong>

* Amoxicillin
* Penicillin G benzathine
* Ceftriaxone
* Cefotetan

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Most-Tested Risks</strong>

* Allergic reaction
* Rash
* Diarrhea
* Anaphylaxis
* Possible cross-sensitivity

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Patient Teaching</strong>

* Complete the entire prescription.
* Report a rash immediately.
* Report wheezing or swelling.
* Monitor for severe diarrhea.

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Administration Safety</strong>

* Penicillin G benzathine is given IM.
* Never administer benzathine penicillin IV.
* Verify allergy history before giving.

</div>

</div>

<div class="detail-table">
  <table>
    <thead>
      <tr>
        <th>Situation</th>
        <th>ATI Meaning</th>
      </tr>
    </thead>
    <tbody>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>Severe penicillin allergy</strong></td>
        <td>Report before giving a cephalosporin because cross-reactivity can occur.</td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>New rash with amoxicillin</strong></td>
        <td>Possible allergic reaction; notify the provider.</td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>Severe watery diarrhea</strong></td>
        <td>Possible antibiotic-associated colitis; report promptly.</td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>Client feels better early</strong></td>
        <td>Continue the full prescribed course.</td>
      </tr>
    </tbody>
  </table>
</div>

<div class="detail-callout" markdown="1">
<strong>ATI Priority:</strong> A rash, wheezing, facial swelling, or throat tightness after an antibiotic can indicate allergy or anaphylaxis.
</div>

</div>

### Aminoglycosides

<div class="detail-grid" markdown="1">

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Common Examples</strong>

* Gentamicin
* Amikacin
* Tobramycin

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Priority Dangers</strong>

* Nephrotoxicity
* Ototoxicity
* Balance problems
* Medication accumulation

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Monitor</strong>

* Peak and trough levels
* Creatinine and BUN
* Urine output
* Hearing
* Tinnitus or dizziness

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Report</strong>

* Hearing loss
* Tinnitus
* Vertigo
* Decreased urine output
* Elevated trough level

</div>

</div>

<div class="detail-callout" markdown="1">
<strong>Peak vs. Trough:</strong> Peak evaluates whether the medication reaches a therapeutic level. Trough evaluates whether too much medication remains before the next dose.
</div>

<div class="detail-callout" markdown="1">
<strong>ATI Priority:</strong> An elevated trough level means the medication is not clearing and toxicity risk is increasing.
</div>

</div>

<div class="mh-detail orange" id="panel-fluoroquinolones" markdown="1">

### Fluoroquinolones

<div class="detail-grid" markdown="1">

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Common Examples</strong>

* Ciprofloxacin
* Levofloxacin
* Medication names often end in **-floxacin**

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Priority Danger</strong>

* Tendinitis
* Tendon rupture
* Joint or tendon pain
* Higher risk in older adults and steroid users

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Patient Teaching</strong>

* Report tendon pain immediately.
* Avoid strenuous activity if pain develops.
* Complete the full prescription.
* Increase fluids if not contraindicated.

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Absorption Interactions</strong>

* Antacids
* Iron
* Calcium
* Magnesium
* Separate these from the antibiotic as directed.

</div>

</div>

<div class="detail-callout" markdown="1">
<strong>ATI Priority:</strong> New heel, shoulder, or tendon pain is not expected soreness. Hold the medication and notify the provider.
</div>

</div>

### Tetracyclines

<div class="detail-grid" markdown="1">

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Common Example</strong>

* Doxycycline
* Broad-spectrum antibiotic

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Priority Teaching</strong>

* Take with a full glass of water.
* Remain upright after taking.
* Use sunscreen.
* Complete the prescription.

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Avoid / Separate</strong>

* Antacids
* Iron
* Calcium supplements
* Milk close to the dose if directed

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Contraindications</strong>

* Pregnancy
* Young children when tooth development is a concern
* Use can discolor developing teeth

</div>

</div>

<div class="detail-callout" markdown="1">
<strong>ATI Priority:</strong> A full glass of water and upright positioning reduce the risk for esophageal irritation and ulceration.
</div>

</div>

<div class="mh-detail purple" id="panel-vancomycin" markdown="1">
<a class="mh-detail-close" href="#">✕</a>

### Vancomycin

<div class="detail-grid" markdown="1">

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Priority Dangers</strong>

* Nephrotoxicity
* Infusion reaction
* Hypotension
* Possible ototoxicity

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Infusion Reaction</strong>

* Flushing
* Urticaria
* Tachycardia
* Hypotension
* Often caused by rapid infusion

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Monitor</strong>

* Kidney function
* Urine output
* Blood pressure
* Infusion rate
* Drug levels when ordered

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Priority Action</strong>

* Stop or slow infusion as ordered.
* Notify the provider.
* Monitor airway and blood pressure.
* Administer ordered supportive medications.

</div>

</div>

<div class="detail-callout" markdown="1">
<strong>ATI Priority:</strong> Hypotension during a vancomycin infusion is more urgent than nausea or chills.
</div>

</div>

### Antifungals

<div class="detail-grid" markdown="1">

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Amphotericin B</strong>

* Used for serious systemic fungal infections.
* Considered high risk because of severe toxicities.

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Priority Dangers</strong>

* Nephrotoxicity
* Hypokalemia
* Anemia
* Hypotension
* Dysrhythmias

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Monitor</strong>

* Kidney function
* Urine output
* Potassium
* CBC
* Blood pressure

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Report</strong>

* Decreased urine output
* Severe weakness
* Palpitations
* Dizziness
* Hypotension

</div>

</div>

<div class="detail-callout" markdown="1">
<strong>ATI Cue:</strong> Think “Ampho-terrible”: kidneys, potassium, blood count, and blood pressure.
</div>

</div>

### Tuberculosis Medications

<div class="detail-table">
  <table>
    <thead>
      <tr>
        <th>Medication</th>
        <th>Most-Tested Risk</th>
        <th>Key Teaching</th>
      </tr>
    </thead>
    <tbody>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>Isoniazid</strong></td>
        <td>Hepatotoxicity, peripheral neuropathy</td>
        <td>Avoid alcohol; vitamin B6 may be prescribed</td>
      </tr>
      <tr>
        <td><strong>Rifampin</strong></td>
        <td>Hepatotoxicity, many interactions</td>
        <td>Body fluids can turn red-orange</td>
      </tr>
    </tbody>
  </table>
</div>

<div class="detail-grid" markdown="1">

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Isoniazid Teaching</strong>

* Avoid alcohol.
* Report jaundice or abdominal pain.
* Maintain good nutrition.
* Take pyridoxine if prescribed.

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Rifampin Teaching</strong>

* Urine, sweat, saliva, and tears may turn red-orange.
* Soft contact lenses can stain.
* Report liver-injury symptoms.
* Review medication interactions.

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Therapy Rules</strong>

* Take all medications exactly as prescribed.
* Multiple drugs are often required.
* Do not stop because symptoms improve.
* Keep follow-up appointments.

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Report Immediately</strong>

* Jaundice
* Dark urine
* Severe abdominal pain
* Persistent nausea
* Numbness or tingling

</div>

</div>

<div class="detail-callout" markdown="1">
<strong>ATI Priority:</strong> Daily alcohol intake is an important assessment because isoniazid and alcohol both increase liver-injury risk.
</div>

</div>

<div class="mh-detail pink" id="panel-antivirals" markdown="1">

### Antivirals

<div class="detail-grid" markdown="1">

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Acyclovir</strong>

* Treats herpes infections.
* Reduces symptoms and viral shedding.
* Does not cure herpes.

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Acyclovir Safety</strong>

* Encourage fluids if not contraindicated.
* Monitor kidney function.
* Begin treatment early when prescribed.
* Continue safer-sex practices.

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Zidovudine</strong>

* Antiretroviral medication.
* Helps suppress HIV replication.
* Does not eliminate transmission risk.

</div>

<div class="detail-mini-card" markdown="1">
<strong>Zidovudine Danger</strong>

* Severe anemia
* Neutropenia
* Monitor CBC before and during therapy
* Report fatigue, pallor, or infection signs

</div>

</div>

<div class="detail-callout" markdown="1">
<strong>ATI Teaching:</strong> An undetectable HIV viral load does not mean protection can be stopped during sexual activity.
</div>

</div>

<div class="mh-columns wide-left" markdown="1">

<div class="key-findings" markdown="1">
<h3>📋 Anti-Infective Big Picture</h3>

* Penicillins and cephalosporins can cause allergic reactions.
* A severe penicillin allergy matters before giving a cephalosporin.
* Aminoglycosides damage kidneys and hearing.
* Fluoroquinolones can cause tendon rupture.
* Doxycycline can injure the esophagus and affect developing teeth.
* Vancomycin can cause hypotension and flushing if infused too rapidly.
* Amphotericin B can damage kidneys and lower potassium.
* Isoniazid and rifampin can injure the liver.
* Zidovudine can cause anemia and neutropenia.
* Most anti-infectives must be taken for the full prescribed course.

</div>

<div class="mh-col" markdown="1">

<div class="report-now" markdown="1">
<h3>Report Immediately</h3>

* Rash, wheezing, facial swelling, or throat tightness
* Severe watery diarrhea
* Hearing loss, tinnitus, or vertigo
* Decreased urine output
* New tendon pain
* Hypotension or flushing during vancomycin
* Jaundice or severe abdominal pain
* Severe weakness or pallor with zidovudine
* Fever or infection signs with neutropenia

</div>

<div class="nclex-tip" markdown="1">
<h3>ATI Tip</h3>

When an antibiotic question lists several expected effects, prioritize **allergy, kidney injury, hearing loss, severe diarrhea, tendon injury, or unstable vital signs**.

</div>

<div class="mh-columns" markdown="1">

<div class="quick-review" markdown="1">
<h3>Quick Review</h3>

1. Verify allergies before giving antibiotics.
2. Complete the full prescription.
3. Monitor aminoglycoside peaks and troughs.
4. Report hearing changes immediately.
5. Stop activity and report tendon pain with fluoroquinolones.
6. Give doxycycline with water and keep the client upright.
7. Infuse vancomycin slowly.
8. Monitor liver function with tuberculosis medications.

</div>

<div class="do-not-miss" markdown="1">
<h3>Do Not Miss</h3>

* Never give benzathine penicillin IV.
* Do not ignore a new antibiotic rash.
* Do not dismiss elevated aminoglycoside trough levels.
* Do not continue strenuous activity with tendon pain.
* Do not take doxycycline with antacids or mineral supplements at the same time.
* Do not ignore hypotension during vancomycin.
* Avoid alcohol with isoniazid.
* Red-orange urine with rifampin is expected.

</div>
