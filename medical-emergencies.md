# Medical Emergencies Medication Study Guide

<div class="note">
  <strong>Big idea:</strong> Know the emergency, the priority medication, why it is used, and what the nurse must monitor.
</div>

---

## Anaphylaxis Medications

| Medication                     | Used For                          | Nursing Priority                         |
| ------------------------------ | --------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------- |
| **Epinephrine**                | Anaphylaxis                       | Give first; monitor airway, BP, HR, SpO₂ |
| **Diphenhydramine / Benadryl** | Hives, itching, allergic symptoms | Monitor sedation and fall risk           |
| **Corticosteroids**            | Inflammation/delayed reaction     | Monitor glucose and infection risk       |
| **Albuterol**                  | Wheezing/bronchospasm             | Assess lung sounds and HR                |

### Epinephrine

**Priority medication for anaphylaxis.**
Epinephrine opens the airway, increases BP, and helps reverse the severe allergic response.

**Nursing priorities:** airway, breathing, BP, HR, SpO₂, and possible repeat doses.

<div class="nclex">
  <strong>NCLEX Tip:</strong> In anaphylaxis, epinephrine comes first. Antihistamines and steroids help later, but they do not replace epinephrine.
</div>

### Diphenhydramine / Benadryl

Used for itching, hives, and allergic symptoms.
Monitor for **drowsiness**, dizziness, and fall risk.

### Corticosteroids

Decrease inflammation and help prevent delayed/worsening reaction.
They work slower than epinephrine.

### Albuterol

Used if the patient has wheezing or bronchospasm.
Monitor for **tachycardia**.

## Shock / Perfusion Medications

| Medication                    | Used For                                | Nursing Priority                             |
| ----------------------------- | --------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------- |
| **IV fluids**                 | Hypovolemia, sepsis, burns, dehydration | Monitor BP, lung sounds, edema, urine output |
| **Norepinephrine / Levophed** | Severe hypotension/shock                | Monitor BP closely and IV site               |
| **Dopamine**                  | Hypotension/bradycardia support         | Monitor HR, BP, rhythm                       |
| **Dobutamine**                | Poor cardiac contractility              | Monitor HR, BP, chest pain, perfusion        |
| **Antibiotics**               | Septic shock/infection                  | Cultures if ordered, then give promptly      |

### IV Fluids

Fluids increase circulating volume and improve perfusion.

**Watch for:** fluid overload, especially in heart failure or kidney disease.

### Norepinephrine / Levophed

A vasopressor used when BP remains low after fluids.

**Watch for:** hypertension, dysrhythmias, and tissue damage if IV infiltrates.

### Dopamine

Can support BP and heart rate depending on the dose/order.

**Watch for:** tachycardia and dysrhythmias.

### Dobutamine

Helps the heart pump more strongly.

**Used most with:** poor cardiac output/cardiogenic shock.

### Antibiotics

Used when shock is caused by infection.

<div class="nclex">
  <strong>Septic Shock Priority:</strong> Fluids + cultures if ordered + antibiotics + vasopressor if BP stays low.
</div>

## Increased ICP / Neuro Medications

| Medication             | Used For                      | Nursing Priority                                |
| ---------------------- | ----------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------- |
| **Mannitol**           | Increased ICP/cerebral edema  | Monitor neuro status, urine output, dehydration |
| **Hypertonic saline**  | Increased ICP/cerebral edema  | Monitor sodium, neuro status, fluid overload    |
| **Anticonvulsants**    | Seizures/seizure prevention   | Maintain seizure precautions                    |
| **Sedation/pain meds** | Agitation/pain increasing ICP | Monitor LOC and respirations                    |

### Mannitol

An osmotic diuretic that pulls fluid from brain tissue into the bloodstream so the kidneys can remove it.

**Watch for:** dehydration, electrolyte changes, and urine output.

### Hypertonic Saline

Pulls fluid out of swollen brain tissue.

**Watch closely:** sodium levels, fluid overload, and neuro status.

### Anticonvulsants

Used to prevent or treat seizures.

**Nursing priority:** seizure precautions and airway safety.

<div class="nclex">
  <strong>ICP Tip:</strong> Worsening LOC is often the earliest sign of worsening neuro status.
</div>

## Burns Medications / Treatments

| Medication/Treatment    | Used For                        | Nursing Priority                   |
| ----------------------- | ------------------------------- | ---------------------------------- |
| **Lactated Ringer’s**   | Burn fluid resuscitation        | Monitor urine output and perfusion |
| **IV opioids**          | Severe burn pain                | Monitor RR, sedation, BP           |
| **Silver sulfadiazine** | Burn wound infection prevention | Check sulfa allergy                |
| **Tetanus booster**     | Burn/wound protection           | Check vaccine history              |
| **Antibiotics**         | Infection if suspected/ordered  | Monitor wound, fever, WBC          |

### Lactated Ringer’s

Common fluid used for burn resuscitation.

**Priority:** maintain perfusion and urine output.

### Opioid Pain Medication

Burns are extremely painful. IV pain medication is often needed early.

**Watch for:** respiratory depression, sedation, and hypotension.

### Silver Sulfadiazine

Topical antimicrobial used on burn wounds.

**Important:** check for sulfa allergy.

### Tetanus Booster

May be needed depending on wound type and immunization status.

## Poisoning / Ingestion Medications

| Medication                    | Used For                                      | Nursing Priority                            |
| ----------------------------- | --------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------- |
| **Activated charcoal**        | Some ingestions                               | Avoid if aspiration risk/decreased LOC      |
| **Naloxone / Narcan**         | Opioid overdose                               | Monitor respirations; may need repeat doses |
| **Acetylcysteine / Mucomyst** | Acetaminophen overdose                        | Protects liver; monitor liver labs          |
| **Atropine**                  | Organophosphate poisoning/bradycardia         | Monitor HR, secretions, airway              |
| **Pralidoxime / 2-PAM**       | Organophosphate poisoning                     | Often given with atropine                   |
| **Flumazenil**                | Benzodiazepine reversal                       | Use cautiously; seizure risk                |
| **Fomepizole**                | Methanol/ethylene glycol poisoning            | Monitor labs/acidosis                       |
| **Glucagon**                  | Beta blocker overdose                         | Monitor HR, BP, glucose                     |
| **Calcium gluconate**         | Calcium channel blocker overdose/hyperkalemia | Monitor cardiac rhythm                      |
| **Sodium bicarbonate**        | Certain overdoses/acidosis                    | Monitor ABGs/electrolytes                   |

### Activated Charcoal

Binds some toxins in the GI tract to reduce absorption.

**Not for everything.** Avoid if decreased LOC or aspiration risk unless airway is protected.

### Naloxone / Narcan

Reverses opioid overdose, especially respiratory depression.

**Priority:** airway and breathing.

<div class="nclex">
  <strong>Opioid Overdose Cue:</strong> Decreased respirations + pinpoint pupils + decreased LOC = think naloxone.
</div>

### Acetylcysteine / Mucomyst

Antidote for acetaminophen overdose. Helps prevent liver damage.

### Atropine + Pralidoxime / 2-PAM

Used for organophosphate poisoning.

**Atropine helps dry secretions and increase HR.**
**2-PAM helps reverse neuromuscular effects.**

### Flumazenil

Reverses benzodiazepines, but can cause seizures.

**Airway support is still priority.**

### Fomepizole

Used for methanol or ethylene glycol poisoning.

### Glucagon

Used for beta blocker overdose.

**Watch for:** nausea/vomiting, HR, BP, and glucose.

### Calcium Gluconate

Used for calcium channel blocker overdose and some hyperkalemia situations.

**Priority:** cardiac monitoring.

### Sodium Bicarbonate

Used for specific overdoses or severe acidosis.

## Environmental Emergency Medications / Treatments

| Treatment               | Used For                            | Nursing Priority                    |
| ----------------------- | ----------------------------------- | ----------------------------------- |
| **Warm IV fluids**      | Hypothermia                         | Rewarm slowly; monitor rhythm       |
| **Cool IV fluids**      | Heat stroke/severe heat illness     | Rapid cooling; monitor temp and LOC |
| **Benzodiazepines**     | Seizures/agitation/shivering        | Monitor airway and sedation         |
| **Tetanus prophylaxis** | Wounds/bites/environmental injuries | Check vaccine history               |

### Warm IV Fluids

Used in hypothermia to help rewarm the body.

**Important:** handle the patient gently because hypothermia can trigger dysrhythmias.

### Cool IV Fluids

Used in heat stroke/severe heat illness.

**Priority:** rapid cooling and monitoring mental status.

### Benzodiazepines

May be used for seizures, severe agitation, or shivering depending on the situation.

**Watch for:** respiratory depression and sedation.

## Cardiac / EKG / Perfusion Medications

| Medication                   | Used For                         | Nursing Priority                  |
| ---------------------------- | -------------------------------- | --------------------------------- |
| **Aspirin**                  | Chest pain/possible MI           | Check allergy/bleeding risk       |
| **Nitroglycerin**            | Angina/chest pain                | Check BP first                    |
| **Oxygen**                   | Hypoxia/respiratory distress     | Monitor SpO₂                      |
| **Morphine**                 | Severe pain if ordered           | Monitor RR, BP, sedation          |
| **Beta blockers**            | HTN, angina, rate control        | Check HR and BP                   |
| **Calcium channel blockers** | HTN, angina, rate control        | Check HR/BP; watch edema          |
| **Adenosine**                | SVT                              | Rapid IV push; cardiac monitoring |
| **Atropine**                 | Symptomatic bradycardia          | Monitor HR/rhythm                 |
| **Amiodarone**               | Ventricular dysrhythmias         | Monitor ECG and BP                |
| **Epinephrine**              | Cardiac arrest/anaphylaxis/shock | CPR/defib when indicated          |

### Aspirin

Antiplatelet used for chest pain or suspected MI.

**Watch for:** allergy and bleeding.

### Nitroglycerin

Vasodilator used for angina/chest pain.

**Always check BP first.**
Do not give if hypotensive or if recent erectile dysfunction medication use.

<div class="nclex">
  <strong>Nitro Safety:</strong> Low BP + nitro = danger.
</div>

### Oxygen

Used if the patient is hypoxic or in respiratory distress.

**Do not assume every chest pain patient needs oxygen if SpO₂ is normal unless ordered.**

### Morphine

Used for severe pain if ordered.

**Watch for:** respiratory depression, hypotension, and sedation.

### Beta Blockers

Decrease HR, BP, and cardiac workload.

**Hold/clarify if:** HR or BP is too low.

### Calcium Channel Blockers

Used for BP control, angina, or rate control depending on the medication.

**Watch for:** hypotension, bradycardia, constipation, and edema.

### Adenosine

Used for SVT.

**Must know:** rapid IV push, followed by flush. It works very fast and may briefly make the patient feel chest pressure or a “pause.”

<div class="nclex">
  <strong>SVT:</strong> Stable = vagal maneuvers then adenosine. Unstable = synchronized cardioversion.
</div>

### Atropine

Used for symptomatic bradycardia.

**Symptoms matter:** hypotension, dizziness, syncope, chest pain, altered LOC.

### Amiodarone

Used for serious ventricular rhythms.

**Monitor:** ECG, BP, rhythm.

## Anticoagulants / Antiplatelets / Clot Medications

| Medication               | Used For                              | Nursing Priority                  |
| ------------------------ | ------------------------------------- | --------------------------------- |
| **Heparin**              | DVT, PE, ACS/MI                       | Monitor aPTT/anti-Xa and bleeding |
| **Enoxaparin / Lovenox** | DVT/PE prevention/treatment           | SubQ abdomen; do not massage      |
| **Warfarin / Coumadin**  | Long-term anticoagulation             | Monitor INR                       |
| **Clopidogrel / Plavix** | Antiplatelet/stent protection         | Monitor bleeding                  |
| **tPA / thrombolytics**  | Stroke, STEMI, massive PE if eligible | Major bleeding risk               |

### Heparin

Prevents clots from getting bigger.

**Monitor:** bleeding and aPTT/anti-Xa per facility.
**Antidote:** protamine sulfate.

### Enoxaparin / Lovenox

Low molecular weight heparin.

**Give:** subQ in abdomen.
**Do not:** massage injection site.

### Warfarin / Coumadin

Long-term anticoagulant.

**Monitor:** INR.
**Antidote:** vitamin K.
Teach consistent vitamin K intake.

### Clopidogrel / Plavix

Antiplatelet medication.

**Watch for:** bleeding and bruising.

### Thrombolytics / tPA

Break down clots.

**Major risk:** severe bleeding.

<div class="nclex">
  <strong>Anticoagulants vs Thrombolytics:</strong> Anticoagulants prevent clots from getting bigger. Thrombolytics break clots apart.
</div>

## Hypertension / Hypertensive Crisis Medications

| Medication         | Used For                   | Nursing Priority                  |
| ------------------ | -------------------------- | --------------------------------- |
| **Hydralazine**    | Hypertension               | Monitor BP and reflex tachycardia |
| **Labetalol**      | Severe HTN/crisis          | Monitor BP, HR, rhythm            |
| **Nitroprusside**  | Severe hypertensive crisis | Very close BP monitoring          |
| **ACE inhibitors** | HTN/cardiac protection     | Monitor cough, K⁺, renal labs     |
| **ARBs**           | HTN                        | Monitor K⁺, BP, renal labs        |
| **Diuretics**      | HTN/fluid overload         | Monitor I&O, weight, electrolytes |

### Hydralazine

Vasodilator used for hypertension.

**Watch for:** headache, dizziness, hypotension, reflex tachycardia.

### Labetalol

Used for severe hypertension.

**Watch for:** bradycardia and hypotension.

### Nitroprusside

Powerful IV vasodilator for hypertensive crisis.

**Requires:** close BP monitoring.

### ACE Inhibitors

Lower BP and reduce angiotensin II.

**Watch for:** cough, hyperkalemia, angioedema.

### ARBs

Lower BP by blocking angiotensin II receptors.

**Watch for:** hyperkalemia and kidney function changes.

### Diuretics

Remove excess fluid.

**Watch for:** dehydration and electrolyte imbalance.

## Pulmonary Embolism / DVT Medications

| Medication               | Used For                    | Nursing Priority                    |
| ------------------------ | --------------------------- | ----------------------------------- |
| **Heparin**              | DVT/PE treatment            | Prevents clot from growing          |
| **Enoxaparin / Lovenox** | DVT/PE prevention/treatment | Monitor bleeding                    |
| **Warfarin / Coumadin**  | Long-term anticoagulation   | Monitor INR                         |
| **tPA / thrombolytic**   | Massive/life-threatening PE | Major bleeding risk                 |
| **Oxygen**               | PE with hypoxia/distress    | Monitor SpO₂ and respiratory status |

### Anticoagulants

Heparin, enoxaparin, and warfarin help prevent the clot from getting larger and help prevent new clots.

**They do not dissolve the clot.**

### Thrombolytics

Used for severe/life-threatening PE if ordered.

**They break down clots but have a major bleeding risk.**

### Oxygen

Supports oxygenation while the clot is being treated.

## CABG / Post-Op Cardiac Medication Connections

| Medication Type                  | Why Used After CABG                  | Nursing Priority                  |
| -------------------------------- | ------------------------------------ | --------------------------------- |
| **Pain medication**              | Helps patient cough, breathe, move   | Monitor RR, sedation, pain relief |
| **Antibiotics**                  | Prevent/treat infection              | Monitor fever, WBC, incision      |
| **Antiplatelets/anticoagulants** | Prevent clot complications           | Monitor bleeding                  |
| **Beta blockers**                | Decrease cardiac workload            | Monitor HR and BP                 |
| **Statins**                      | Long-term CAD/cholesterol management | Monitor muscle pain/weakness      |

<div class="note">
  <strong>CABG Connection:</strong> Pain control matters because if the patient hurts too much to cough, deep breathe, or move, they are at higher risk for pneumonia, atelectasis, and complications.
</div>

# Final Medication Priority Chart

| Emergency / Situation                  | Medication to Think Of First               | What the Nurse Watches             |
| -------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------ | ---------------------------------- |
| **Anaphylaxis**                        | Epinephrine                                | Airway, BP, HR, SpO₂               |
| **Opioid overdose**                    | Naloxone                                   | Respirations, LOC, repeat sedation |
| **Acetaminophen overdose**             | Acetylcysteine                             | Liver labs, timing/amount ingested |
| **Increased ICP**                      | Mannitol / hypertonic saline               | Neuro status, sodium, urine output |
| **Septic shock**                       | Fluids + antibiotics + vasopressors        | BP, perfusion, urine output        |
| **Burns**                              | LR + IV pain meds                          | Urine output, pain, perfusion      |
| **Chest pain / possible MI**           | Aspirin, nitro if safe, oxygen if hypoxic  | EKG, BP, SpO₂, bleeding            |
| **SVT stable**                         | Vagal maneuvers, adenosine                 | Rhythm, BP, symptoms               |
| **Symptomatic bradycardia**            | Atropine                                   | HR, BP, symptoms                   |
| **V-fib / pulseless V-tach**           | Epinephrine + defibrillation               | CPR quality, rhythm, pulse         |
| **DVT / PE**                           | Anticoagulants                             | Bleeding, SpO₂, chest pain         |
| **Massive PE / eligible stroke/STEMI** | tPA / thrombolytic                         | Major bleeding, neuro status       |
| **Hypertensive crisis**                | Labetalol / nitroprusside / ordered IV med | BP, HR, neuro status               |
| **Hypothermia**                        | Warm IV fluids                             | Core temp, cardiac rhythm          |
| **Heat stroke**                        | Cooling / cool IV fluids                   | Temperature, LOC, hydration        |

# Final Must-Know Nursing Safety Chart

| Medication Group             | Biggest Safety Concern                     |
| ---------------------------- | ------------------------------------------ |
| **Epinephrine**              | Tachycardia, hypertension, dysrhythmias    |
| **Diphenhydramine**          | Sedation and falls                         |
| **Steroids**                 | Hyperglycemia and infection risk           |
| **Vasopressors**             | BP too high, dysrhythmias, IV infiltration |
| **Mannitol**                 | Dehydration and electrolyte imbalance      |
| **Hypertonic saline**        | Sodium changes and fluid overload          |
| **Opioids**                  | Respiratory depression                     |
| **Naloxone**                 | Opioid effects can return                  |
| **Nitroglycerin**            | Hypotension                                |
| **Adenosine**                | Brief pause/chest pressure feeling         |
| **Atropine**                 | Tachycardia                                |
| **Anticoagulants**           | Bleeding                                   |
| **Thrombolytics**            | Major/life-threatening bleeding            |
| **Diuretics**                | Dehydration and electrolyte imbalance      |
| **Beta blockers**            | Bradycardia and hypotension                |
| **Calcium channel blockers** | Hypotension, bradycardia, edema            |
