## Question 1

<p>A nurse in an emergency department is assessing a client who has multiple bruises in different stages of healing. The client’s partner answers every question and refuses to leave the room. Which action should the nurse take <strong>first</strong>?</p>

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> Ask the partner whether the injuries were caused by abuse</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> Request to speak with the client alone as part of routine assessment</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> Notify law enforcement immediately</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> Tell the client that the injuries appear suspicious</p>

<details class="quiz-answer-block">
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: B</strong></p>
<div class="quiz-rationale">
<p><strong>Rationale:</strong></p>
<p><strong>A. Incorrect:</strong> Confronting the partner can increase danger and may prevent the client from speaking openly.</p>
<p><strong>B. Correct:</strong> The nurse should separate the client from the accompanying person and perform a private, direct safety assessment.</p>
<p><strong>C. Incorrect:</strong> Law enforcement involvement depends on the client’s wishes, immediate danger, and mandatory-reporting requirements.</p>
<p><strong>D. Incorrect:</strong> This statement can sound accusatory and should not replace a private, trauma-informed assessment.</p>
</div>
</details>

## Question 2

<p>A nurse is interviewing a client who reports intimate partner violence. Which response should the nurse make?</p>

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> “Why have you stayed in this relationship?”</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> “You need to leave before the violence becomes worse.”</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> “No one deserves to be hurt. What concerns do you have about your safety today?”</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> “I promise that everything you tell me will remain confidential.”</p>

<details class="quiz-answer-block">
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: C</strong></p>
<div class="quiz-rationale">
<p><strong>Rationale:</strong></p>
<p><strong>A. Incorrect:</strong> “Why” questions can imply blame and increase shame.</p>
<p><strong>B. Incorrect:</strong> Telling the client what to do removes autonomy and may place the client at greater risk.</p>
<p><strong>C. Correct:</strong> This response validates the client, avoids blame, and assesses immediate safety.</p>
<p><strong>D. Incorrect:</strong> The nurse cannot promise absolute confidentiality because mandatory-reporting laws may apply.</p>
</div>
</details>

<div class="quiz-rationale">
<p><strong>Rationale:</strong></p>

<p><strong>D. Incorrect:</strong> Financial control can indicate exploitation, but immediate physical safety takes priority.</p>

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> Bruises on the shins of an active school-age child</p>

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> An explanation that is inconsistent with the injury pattern</p>

<p><strong>E. Incorrect:</strong> A witnessed injury over a bony area is more consistent with accidental trauma.</p>

## Question 6

<p>A nurse is caring for a client who reports that a partner recently strangled them. The client has a hoarse voice and neck tenderness. Which action should the nurse take <strong>first</strong>?</p>

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> Provide written information about shelters</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> Assess airway and breathing</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> Ask the client to develop a long-term safety plan</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> Photograph the injuries according to policy</p>

<details class="quiz-answer-block">
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: B</strong></p>
<div class="quiz-rationale">
<p><strong>Rationale:</strong></p>
<p><strong>A. Incorrect:</strong> Referral information is important after immediate medical threats are addressed.</p>

<p><strong>C. Incorrect:</strong> Safety planning follows stabilization.</p>
<p><strong>D. Incorrect:</strong> Documentation is important but must not delay assessment of a potential airway emergency.</p>

## Question 8

<p>A nurse is caring for a competent adult client who discloses intimate partner violence but declines police involvement. Which action should the nurse take?</p>

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> Notify police despite the client’s refusal in all cases</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> Respect the client’s decision, assess immediate danger, and provide resources</p>

<div class="quiz-rationale">
<p><strong>Rationale:</strong></p>

## Question 9

<p>A nurse is interviewing a child who may have experienced sexual abuse. Which action is appropriate?</p>

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> Ask detailed leading questions to obtain a complete history</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> Promise the child that the information will remain secret</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> Use simple, open-ended questions and allow the child to describe events in their own words</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> Ask the caregiver to remain present throughout the interview</p>

<details class="quiz-answer-block">
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: C</strong></p>
<div class="quiz-rationale">
<p><strong>Rationale:</strong></p>
<p><strong>A. Incorrect:</strong> Leading or repetitive questions can influence the child’s account and interfere with investigation.</p>
<p><strong>B. Incorrect:</strong> The nurse cannot promise secrecy because suspected child abuse must be reported.</p>
<p><strong>C. Correct:</strong> Open-ended, developmentally appropriate questions reduce suggestibility and support accurate disclosure.</p>
<p><strong>D. Incorrect:</strong> The suspected caregiver should not be present, and privacy may help the child speak safely.</p>

## Question 11

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> “Place each item of clothing in a separate paper bag.”</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> “Wash the clothing before giving it to law enforcement.”</p>

<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: A</strong></p>
<div class="quiz-rationale">
<p><strong>Rationale:</strong></p>

<p><strong>C. Incorrect:</strong> Plastic traps moisture and can degrade evidence.</p>
<p><strong>D. Incorrect:</strong> Undergarments may contain important forensic evidence.</p>
