## Question 1

<p>A nurse is caring for a client who is difficult to arouse after using heroin. The client has a respiratory rate of 6/min and pinpoint pupils. Which action should the nurse take <strong>first</strong>?</p>

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> Administer naloxone as prescribed</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> Encourage the client to drink fluids</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> Place the client in a quiet room</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> Ask when the client last used heroin</p>

<details class="quiz-answer-block">
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: A</strong></p>
<div class="quiz-rationale">
<p><strong>Rationale:</strong></p>
<p><strong>A. Correct:</strong> Respiratory depression, decreased level of consciousness, and pinpoint pupils indicate opioid overdose. Naloxone should be administered immediately while airway and breathing are supported.</p>
<p><strong>B. Incorrect:</strong> Oral fluids are unsafe for a client with decreased consciousness.</p>
<p><strong>C. Incorrect:</strong> A quiet environment does not treat respiratory depression.</p>
<p><strong>D. Incorrect:</strong> History-taking should not delay emergency treatment.</p>
</div>
</details>

## Question 2

<p>A nurse is monitoring a client who received naloxone for opioid overdose. Which finding requires the nurse’s <strong>immediate action</strong>?</p>

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> Nausea and abdominal cramping</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> Return of respiratory depression 30 minutes later</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> Irritability and sweating</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> Complaints of generalized body aches</p>

<details class="quiz-answer-block">
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: B</strong></p>
<div class="quiz-rationale">
<p><strong>Rationale:</strong></p>
<p><strong>A. Incorrect:</strong> These can occur with acute opioid withdrawal.</p>
<p><strong>B. Correct:</strong> Naloxone may wear off before the opioid does, causing recurrent respiratory depression. The client may need repeat dosing and continued airway support.</p>
<p><strong>C. Incorrect:</strong> Irritability and diaphoresis are expected withdrawal manifestations.</p>
<p><strong>D. Incorrect:</strong> Body aches are common with opioid withdrawal.</p>
</div>
</details>

<div class="quiz-rationale">
<p><strong>Rationale:</strong></p>

<p><strong>Rationale:</strong></p>

<div class="quiz-rationale">
