## Question 1

<p>A nurse is caring for a client who repeatedly reports severe abdominal pain despite multiple normal diagnostic tests. Which response should the nurse make?</p>

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> “The tests are normal, so the pain is not real.”</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> “You should stop focusing on your symptoms.”</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> “I understand the pain is distressing. Let’s discuss how it is affecting your daily life.”</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> “You need another complete medical workup.”</p>

<details class="quiz-answer-block">
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: C</strong></p>
<div class="quiz-rationale">
<p><strong>Rationale:</strong></p>
<p><strong>A. Incorrect:</strong> The symptoms are experienced as real and should not be dismissed.</p>
<p><strong>B. Incorrect:</strong> This is judgmental and nontherapeutic.</p>
<p><strong>C. Correct:</strong> The nurse validates the distress without reinforcing further unnecessary testing and shifts focus toward function.</p>
<p><strong>D. Incorrect:</strong> Repeated testing can reinforce illness behavior when serious medical causes have already been excluded.</p>
</div>
</details>
