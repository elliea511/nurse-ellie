# Therapeutic Communication Practice Quiz

Test your knowledge of therapeutic techniques, nontherapeutic pitfalls, and NCLEX communication traps.

---

## Question 1

A client says, "I feel like nobody cares about me." Which of the following responses by the nurse is **most therapeutic**?

<p class="answer-choice">A. "I'm sure your family cares about you."</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. "Why do you feel that way?"</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. "It sounds like you're feeling very alone right now."</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. "You shouldn't feel that way — you have so much to be grateful for."</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: C</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">Reflecting the client's feeling back to them validates the emotion and encourages further expression. Option A uses false reassurance, B asks "why" which can feel blaming, and D minimizes the client's feelings.</p>
</details>

## Question 2

A nurse is caring for a client with schizophrenia who says, "The government planted a chip in my brain." Which response is **most appropriate**?

<p class="answer-choice">A. "That is not possible. No one did that to you."</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. "That sounds very frightening. I don't see evidence of that."</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. "I believe you. Let's talk to the doctor about removing it."</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. "That must mean you're getting better if you can recognize what happened."</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: B</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">The nurse should acknowledge the client's feelings without arguing or agreeing with the delusion. Option A argues directly. Option C reinforces the delusion. Option D is nonsensical and minimizing.</p>
</details>

## Question 3

A client who was sexually assaulted says, "I should have fought harder. This is my fault." Which response is **most therapeutic**?

<p class="answer-choice">A. "It's not your fault. You did the best you could."</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. "Why didn't you call for help?"</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. "What you went through was not your fault, and I'm glad you're safe now."</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. "You should focus on the future, not the past."</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: C</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">This response clearly removes blame, validates the trauma, and expresses genuine care. Option A starts with "It's not" which can feel dismissive. Option B asks "why" which is nontherapeutic and blaming. Option D redirects away from feelings.</p>
</details>

## Question 4

A client says, "I've decided to stop taking my medication." Which response by the nurse **best** opens therapeutic dialogue?

<p class="answer-choice">A. "You really need to keep taking your medication or you will relapse."</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. "Tell me more about what led to that decision."</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. "I will have to tell the doctor right away."</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. "That is your right, but it's not a good idea."</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: B</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">Exploring the client's reasoning opens communication, builds trust, and allows the nurse to understand barriers to adherence. Options A and D lecture and give advice. Option C immediately threatens rather than engaging the client first.</p>
</details>

## Question 5

A nurse is caring for a client who is crying silently. Which action by the nurse is **most therapeutic**?

<p class="answer-choice">A. Ask the client to explain why they are crying</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. Leave the client alone to have privacy</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. Sit quietly with the client and offer presence</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. Tell the client that everything will be okay</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: C</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">Sitting quietly with the client uses therapeutic silence and conveys presence and support without pressure. Asking "why" is blaming. Leaving removes support. False reassurance dismisses the client's feelings.</p>
</details>

## Question 6

A client with anxiety says, "I can't do anything right. I'm a total failure." Which response is **least therapeutic**?

<p class="answer-choice">A. "It sounds like you're being very hard on yourself."</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. "Tell me more about what you mean by that."</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. "You're not a failure — look at everything you've accomplished."</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. "You seem to be feeling hopeless right now."</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: C</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">Telling the client they are not a failure and redirecting to accomplishments minimizes the feeling and gives unsolicited advice. All other options reflect or explore the client's feelings, which is therapeutic.</p>
</details>

## Question 7

A nurse asks a client, "Are you feeling better today?" This is an example of which nontherapeutic technique?

<p class="answer-choice">A. Minimizing</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. Giving advice</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. Asking a closed-ended question</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. False reassurance</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: C</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">Closed-ended questions can be answered with yes or no and limit therapeutic exploration. Open-ended questions like "How are you feeling today?" encourage the client to express thoughts and feelings.</p>
</details>

## Question 8

A client says, "I want to kill myself." Which is the **priority** nursing response?

<p class="answer-choice">A. "Don't say things like that — you have people who love you."</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. "Are you having thoughts of killing yourself?"</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. "I'll get the doctor right away."</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. "Things will look better in the morning."</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: B</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">When a client expresses suicidal ideation, the nurse's first priority is to directly assess intent. Asking directly about suicide does not increase risk and is the standard of care. The other options minimize, falsely reassure, or abandon the client before assessment.</p>
</details>

## Question 9

A nurse says, "I know exactly how you feel." This is an example of which nontherapeutic technique?

<p class="answer-choice">A. Giving advice</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. False reassurance</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. Expressing sympathy rather than empathy</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. Minimizing feelings</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: C</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">"I know exactly how you feel" is expressing sympathy and assumes the nurse shares the client's experience, which is nontherapeutic. Therapeutic communication uses empathy — acknowledging the client's experience without claiming to share it.</p>
</details>

## Question 10

A client who is grieving says, "At least she didn't suffer long." A student nurse responds, "At least you still have the rest of your family." The nurse educator should address this because it is an example of:

<p class="answer-choice">A. Giving false reassurance</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. Minimizing feelings</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. Changing the subject</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. Asking a "why" question</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: B</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">"At least you still have…" minimizes the client's grief by comparing it to what they still have. Minimizing is nontherapeutic because it dismisses the significance of the client's loss and discourages further expression.</p>
</details>

## Question 11

A client experiencing a manic episode is speaking rapidly and switching topics. Which communication technique should the nurse use?

<p class="answer-choice">A. Encourage the client to continue and express all feelings</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. Use short, simple, clear statements and redirect calmly</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. Ask detailed questions to keep the client focused</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. Remain silent until the client finishes speaking</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: B</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">During mania, the client has poor impulse control and disorganized thinking. Short, simple, calm communication is most effective. Detailed questions or long conversations can escalate agitation.</p>
</details>

## Question 12

A client who hears voices asks the nurse, "Do you hear them too?" Which response is **most therapeutic**?

<p class="answer-choice">A. "No, but let's talk about what the voices are saying."</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. "Of course not. You're the only one hearing things."</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. "Yes, I hear them. What are they saying?"</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. "Try to ignore them — they aren't real."</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: A</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">The nurse honestly states they do not hear the voices while redirecting to the content, which is a safety priority. Option B is honest but dismissive. Option C falsely agrees with the hallucination. Option D tells the client to ignore a real experience to them, which is nontherapeutic.</p>
</details>

## Question 13

A nurse says to a depressed client, "Don't worry, things will get better." This statement is an example of:

<p class="answer-choice">A. Therapeutic reassurance</p>

<p class="answer-choice">C. Giving advice</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. Reflecting</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: B</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">False reassurance is promising the client something the nurse cannot guarantee. This dismisses feelings and closes communication. Therapeutic communication acknowledges and validates what the client is experiencing.</p>
</details>

## Question 14

A client with a personality disorder demands to see a different nurse because "you're the only one who understands me." The nurse recognizes this as an example of:

<p class="answer-choice">A. Therapeutic alliance</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. Transference</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. Splitting</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. Manipulation</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: C</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">Splitting is the defense mechanism where a client sees staff as all-good or all-bad. The best response is team consistency and clear limit-setting. The nurse should not take a special role with the client.</p>
</details>

## Question 15

Which of the following is an example of a **therapeutic** communication technique? **Select all that apply.**

<p class="answer-choice">A. Offering general leads such as "Go on…"</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. Summarizing what the client has shared</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. Telling the client what they should do</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. Using silence to allow reflection</p>
<p class="answer-choice">E. Clarifying unclear statements</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: A, B, D, E</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">General leads, summarizing, silence, and clarifying are all therapeutic techniques that encourage expression and show active listening. Telling the client what to do is giving advice, which is nontherapeutic.</p>
</details>

A client angrily says, "You nurses don't care about anyone here." Which response is **most therapeutic**?

## Question 18

Which of the following nursing statements **best** demonstrates therapeutic limit-setting with an aggressive client?

<p class="answer-choice">A. "If you don't calm down, I will call security."</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. "I can see you are upset. I cannot allow anyone to be hurt. You may sit in the quiet room or walk with staff."</p>

<p class="answer-choice">D. "I know you don't mean what you're saying."</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: B</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">Therapeutic limit-setting acknowledges the emotion, states the limit clearly, and offers a safe choice. Options A and C are threatening. Option D is dismissive of the client's actual behavior.</p>
</details>

## Question 19

A client in crisis says, "I just need someone to listen to me." Which action best demonstrates active listening?

<p class="answer-choice">A. Nodding, making eye contact, and reflecting the client's feelings</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. Taking detailed notes during the conversation</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. Asking frequent questions to gather more information</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. Summarizing each point the client makes immediately after they speak</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: A</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">Active listening includes nonverbal cues (eye contact, nodding) and verbal reflection of feelings. Taking excessive notes can feel clinical and disconnected. Frequent questions interrupt expression. Immediate summarizing can feel mechanical.</p>
</details>

## Question 20

A nurse is caring for a client who has just been told they have a terminal illness. The client says nothing and stares at the floor. Which response is **most appropriate**?

<p class="answer-choice">A. "I know this is hard, but you need to stay positive."</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. "Would you like me to call your family?"</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. Sit quietly beside the client and offer presence</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. "Try to think about the good things in your life."</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: C</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">In moments of shock or grief, presence and silence are the most therapeutic responses. The client has not asked for family. Options A and D use minimizing statements that dismiss the client's experience.</p>
</details>
