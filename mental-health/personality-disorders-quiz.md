## Question 1

<p>A nurse is caring for a client who has borderline personality disorder. The client tells the nurse, “You are the only staff member who understands me. The night nurse is cruel and should be fired.” Which response should the nurse make?</p>

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> “I agree that the night nurse can be difficult.”</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> “Tell me more about why you dislike the night nurse.”</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> “All staff members will follow the same plan of care and limits.”</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> “You should discuss your concerns directly with the night nurse.”</p>

<details class="quiz-answer-block">
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: C</strong></p>
<div class="quiz-rationale">
<p><strong>Rationale:</strong></p>
<p><strong>A. Incorrect:</strong> Agreeing with the client reinforces splitting and damages staff consistency.</p>
<p><strong>B. Incorrect:</strong> Encouraging further criticism can deepen staff splitting rather than redirecting the client to the treatment plan.</p>
<p><strong>C. Correct:</strong> Borderline personality disorder is associated with splitting, in which others are viewed as all good or all bad. Consistent limits and communication among staff reduce manipulation and provide structure.</p>
<p><strong>D. Incorrect:</strong> Direct discussion may be appropriate later, but the priority is to maintain a unified staff approach.</p>
</div>
</details>

## Question 2

<p>A client who has antisocial personality disorder threatens another client after being told that phone privileges are over. Which action should the nurse take <strong>first</strong>?</p>

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> Explain how the threat may hurt the other client’s feelings</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> State the behavioral limit and consequence in a calm, firm manner</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> Ask the client to identify childhood experiences that caused the anger</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> Allow additional phone time to prevent escalation</p>

<details class="quiz-answer-block">
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: B</strong></p>
<div class="quiz-rationale">
<p><strong>Rationale:</strong></p>
<p><strong>A. Incorrect:</strong> Clients with antisocial personality disorder may have limited empathy. An appeal to another person’s feelings is unlikely to stop the immediate behavior.</p>
<p><strong>B. Correct:</strong> The nurse should clearly state the expected behavior and the consequence for violating the limit. Limits must be consistent, specific, and enforced without arguing.</p>
<p><strong>C. Incorrect:</strong> Exploring past experiences does not address the immediate threat.</p>
<p><strong>D. Incorrect:</strong> Giving additional privileges in response to threats reinforces manipulation and aggression.</p>
</div>
</details>

<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: C</strong></p>
<div class="quiz-rationale">
<p><strong>Rationale:</strong></p>

<p><strong>D. Incorrect:</strong> Allowing avoidance does not promote autonomy or confidence.</p>

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> “You need to apologize because the other client is upset.”</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> “Taking another client’s property is not permitted. The money must be returned.”</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> “Why do you think you have difficulty respecting other people’s belongings?”</p>

<div class="quiz-rationale">
