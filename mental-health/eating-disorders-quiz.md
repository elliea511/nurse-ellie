## Question 1

<p>A nurse is admitting a client who has anorexia nervosa. The client has a heart rate of 42/min, blood pressure of 84/50 mm Hg, and reports dizziness when standing. Which action should the nurse take <strong>first</strong>?</p>

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> Ask the client to describe body-image concerns</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> Initiate cardiac monitoring and notify the provider</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> Encourage the client to select a preferred meal</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> Discuss the long-term effects of malnutrition</p>

<details class="quiz-answer-block">
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: B</strong></p>
<div class="quiz-rationale">
<p><strong>Rationale:</strong></p>
<p><strong>A. Incorrect:</strong> Body-image assessment is important, but the client is physiologically unstable.</p>
<p><strong>B. Correct:</strong> Severe bradycardia, hypotension, and orthostatic symptoms indicate cardiovascular compromise and require immediate medical stabilization.</p>
<p><strong>C. Incorrect:</strong> Meal planning is not the first priority during hemodynamic instability.</p>
<p><strong>D. Incorrect:</strong> Teaching should occur after the client is medically stable.</p>
</div>
</details>

## Question 2

<p>A nurse is caring for a client who has anorexia nervosa and refuses breakfast. Which response should the nurse make?</p>

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> “You can skip this meal if you promise to eat lunch.”</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> “Why are you refusing to eat today?”</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> “The meal plan is part of your treatment. I will remain with you while you eat.”</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> “You need to eat because you are dangerously thin.”</p>

<details class="quiz-answer-block">
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: C</strong></p>
<div class="quiz-rationale">
<p><strong>Rationale:</strong></p>
<p><strong>A. Incorrect:</strong> Negotiating skipped meals reinforces avoidance.</p>
<p><strong>B. Incorrect:</strong> “Why” questions can sound judgmental.</p>
<p><strong>C. Correct:</strong> The nurse provides clear structure, maintains the treatment plan, and offers support without arguing.</p>
<p><strong>D. Incorrect:</strong> Focusing on appearance can increase defensiveness and body-image distortion.</p>
</div>
</details>

## Question 3

<p>A nurse is assessing a client who has bulimia nervosa. Which finding should the nurse identify as the <strong>highest priority</strong>?</p>

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> Enlarged parotid glands</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> Dental enamel erosion</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> Potassium level of 2.8 mEq/L</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> Calluses on the knuckles</p>

<details class="quiz-answer-block">
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: C</strong></p>
<div class="quiz-rationale">
<p><strong>Rationale:</strong></p>
<p><strong>A. Incorrect:</strong> Parotid enlargement is common with repeated vomiting but is not immediately life-threatening.</p>
<p><strong>B. Incorrect:</strong> Dental erosion requires treatment but is not the priority.</p>
<p><strong>C. Correct:</strong> Severe hypokalemia can cause lethal cardiac dysrhythmias.</p>
<p><strong>D. Incorrect:</strong> Knuckle calluses, or Russell sign, indicate self-induced vomiting but are less urgent than electrolyte instability.</p>
</div>
</details>

## Question 9

<p>A nurse is caring for a client with anorexia nervosa who begins nutritional rehabilitation. Which laboratory value should the nurse monitor most closely for refeeding syndrome?</p>

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> Phosphorus</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> Bilirubin</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> Calcium</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> Hemoglobin</p>

<details class="quiz-answer-block">
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: A</strong></p>
<div class="quiz-rationale">
<p><strong>Rationale:</strong></p>
<p><strong>A. Correct:</strong> Refeeding syndrome commonly causes a rapid intracellular shift of phosphorus, leading to hypophosphatemia and potentially respiratory or cardiac failure.</p>
<p><strong>B. Incorrect:</strong> Bilirubin is not the key marker.</p>
<p><strong>C. Incorrect:</strong> Calcium can change, but phosphorus is the hallmark concern.</p>
<p><strong>D. Incorrect:</strong> Hemoglobin does not identify refeeding syndrome.</p>
</div>
</details>
