# Mental Health NCLEX Quick Cues

<div class="note">

This page is the **fast review page** for mental health NCLEX questions.

Use it when you need to quickly decide: **What is therapeutic? What is unsafe? What should the nurse do first?**

</div>

---

## Biggest Mental Health Rule

<div class="warning">

**Safety always comes first.**
Before teaching, therapy, coping skills, discharge planning, or feelings — ask: **Is the patient or someone else unsafe right now?**

</div>

---

# Priority Order

| Priority | Think About |
|---|---|
| **1. Safety** | Suicide, homicide, violence, abuse, overdose, withdrawal, command hallucinations |
| **2. Physiological needs** | Airway, breathing, fluids, nutrition, sleep, electrolytes, medication toxicity |
| **3. Mental status / reality testing** | Hallucinations, delusions, confusion, poor judgment |
| **4. Therapeutic communication** | Calm, direct, nonjudgmental responses |
| **5. Coping / teaching** | Teach when calm and safe |

---

# Therapeutic Communication Rules

| Rule | Remember |
|---|---|
| **Do not ask "why"** | Sounds blaming |
| **Do not give advice** | Help patient explore choices |
| **Do not use false reassurance** | Avoid "Everything will be okay" |
| **Do not argue** | Especially with delusions |
| **Do not promise secrecy** | Safety concerns must be reported |
| **Do not minimize feelings** | Avoid "At least…" statements |
| **Use open-ended questions** | Encourages expression |
| **Focus on feelings** | Especially with delusions, grief, anxiety |
| **Use silence** | Gives patient time |
| **Set limits calmly** | Especially with mania, aggression, personality disorders |

---

## Common Wrong Answers

| Wrong Answer | Why It Is Wrong |
|---|---|
| "Why did you do that?" | Blaming |
| "Everything will be fine." | False reassurance |
| "You should…" | Giving advice |
| "That is not true." | Arguing |
| "I know exactly how you feel." | Not therapeutic |
| "At least it was not worse." | Minimizing |
| "I promise I won't tell." | Unsafe |
| "Calm down." | Dismissive |

# Suicide Cues

| Cue | What to Do |
|---|---|
| "I want to die." | Ask directly about suicide |
| "I won't be a bother much longer." | Assess suicide risk |
| Giving away belongings | Assess suicide risk |
| Sudden calm after depression | Assess suicide risk |
| Has plan + means | Stay with patient, remove harmful objects |
| Newly admitted after attempt | Search belongings/patient first |
| Command hallucinations | Ask what voices are saying |
| Refuses to agree to safety | Maintain close observation and notify team |

<div class="warning">

Highest suicide risk = **specific plan + lethal method + access to means + intent.**

</div>

---

# Abuse Cues

| Cue | What to Do |
|---|---|
| Partner/caregiver answers for patient | Interview patient alone |
| Injury explanation does not match injury | Further assess and document |
| Child has suspicious injuries | Report suspected abuse |
| Elder has bruises/weight loss | Collect data and report findings |
| Patient reports sexual assault | Believe patient, ensure safety, preserve evidence if applicable |
| Patient asks for secrecy | Explain safety concerns cannot be secret |
| Domestic violence | Assess safety and help with safety plan |

<div class="note">

Suspected **child abuse, elder abuse, and vulnerable adult abuse** must be reported. Suspicion is enough.

</div>

---

# Hallucination Cues

| Cue | Best Nursing Action |
|---|---|
| "I hear voices." | "What are the voices saying?" |
| Voices tell patient to self-harm | Stay with patient and maintain safety |
| Voices tell patient to hurt others | Maintain safety and notify team |
| Patient asks if nurse hears voices | "I do not hear them, but I understand they are real to you." |
| Patient is afraid of voices | Acknowledge fear and present reality |

<div class="warning">

Command hallucinations are priority because they may tell the patient to harm self or others.

</div>

---

# Delusion Cues

| Patient Says | Best Response |
|---|---|
| "The FBI is watching me." | "That sounds frightening. I do not see anyone watching you." |
| "My food is poisoned." | "You seem worried about the food." |
| "The TV is sending messages." | "I do not receive messages from the TV, but I understand this feels real." |
| "I am the president." | "I understand you believe that. Right now, we are in the hospital." |

## Delusion Rule

| Do | Avoid |
|---|---|
| Focus on feelings | Arguing |
| Present reality calmly | Agreeing |
| Assess safety | Proving them wrong |
| Redirect when possible | Laughing or joking |

---

# Anxiety and Panic Cues

| Anxiety Level | Nursing Focus |
|---|---|
| **Mild** | Teach and problem-solve |
| **Moderate** | Simple directions |
| **Severe** | Reduce stimuli, stay calm |
| **Panic** | Stay with patient, short commands, safety |

<div class="note">

Do not teach during panic. First decrease anxiety and maintain safety.

</div>

## Panic Response

Best response: "You are safe. I will stay with you. Take one slow breath with me."

---

# Depression Cues

| Cue | What to Do |
|---|---|
| Hopelessness | Assess suicide risk |
| "I am worthless" | Assess suicide risk |
| Sudden improvement | Assess suicide risk |
| Poor ADLs | Assist, then encourage independence |
| Poor appetite | Small, high-calorie meals |
| Starts antidepressant | Teach takes weeks; monitor suicide risk early |

<div class="warning">

Antidepressants can increase energy before mood improves, increasing suicide risk early in treatment.

</div>

---

# Mania Cues

| Cue | Nursing Priority |
|---|---|
| Not sleeping | Promote rest, low stimulation |
| Not eating | High-calorie finger foods |
| Constant activity | Fluids, safety, rest |
| Intrusive behavior | Set limits |
| Sexually inappropriate | Set firm boundaries |
| Agitated/escalating | Reduce stimuli, call for help |
| Delusions/hallucinations | Assess safety |

<div class="note">

Mania priority = **safety, low stimulation, rest, fluids, nutrition, clear limits.**

</div>

---

# Personality Disorder Cues

| Disorder / Pattern | Nursing Focus |
|---|---|
| **Paranoid** | Be honest, clear, consistent |
| **Schizoid** | Respect space, gradual interaction |
| **Schizotypal** | Support social skills, assess odd beliefs |
| **Antisocial** | Safety, firm limits, no manipulation |
| **Borderline** | Self-harm risk, boundaries, staff consistency |
| **Histrionic** | Set limits, assess suicide risk |
| **Narcissistic** | Avoid power struggles |
| **Avoidant** | Encourage gradual socialization |
| **Dependent** | Encourage decision-making |
| **OCPD** | Avoid power struggles, offer safe choices |

<div class="warning">

Splitting = patient tries to divide staff. Best response = **team consistency.**

</div>

---

# Eating Disorder Cues

| Cue | Think |
|---|---|
| Underweight + fear of gaining weight | Anorexia |
| Binge + purge | Bulimia |
| Binge without purging | Binge eating disorder |
| Eats nonfood items | Pica |
| Regurgitates food | Rumination |
| Restrictive intake without body image issue | ARFID |
| Low potassium | Dysrhythmia risk |
| Restarting nutrition after starvation | Refeeding syndrome |
| Tooth erosion/parotid swelling | Bulimia/purging |
| Lanugo/amenorrhea | Anorexia |

<div class="warning">

Eating disorders become priority when there are electrolyte problems, dehydration, bradycardia, hypotension, or dysrhythmias.

</div>

---

# Addiction / Substance Use Cues

| Situation | Priority |
|---|---|
| Opioid overdose | Airway/breathing, naloxone |
| Alcohol withdrawal | VS, seizure precautions, benzos as ordered |
| DTs | Medical emergency |
| Benzo/barbiturate withdrawal | Taper slowly, seizure precautions |
| Stimulant intoxication | Cardiac monitoring, reduce stimuli |
| Stimulant withdrawal | Assess depression/suicide risk |
| Disulfiram + alcohol | Medical emergency assessment |
| Wernicke/Korsakoff risk | Thiamine |

<div class="note">

Alcohol withdrawal and benzodiazepine/barbiturate withdrawal can be life-threatening because of seizure risk.

</div>

---

# Schizophrenia / Psychosis Cues

| Cue | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Positive symptoms | Hallucinations, delusions, bizarre behavior |
| Negative symptoms | Flat affect, avolition, alogia, anhedonia |
| Neologism | Made-up word |
| Clang association | Words linked by sound/rhyme |
| Word salad | Words mixed with no meaning |
| Loose associations | Thoughts do not connect |
| Concrete thinking | Takes words literally |
| Anosognosia | Does not believe they are ill |

## Psychosis Priority

| Situation | First Action |
|---|---|
| Hearing voices | Ask what voices are saying |
| Command hallucinations | Safety |
| Delusions | Do not argue or agree |
| Poor hygiene | Support ADLs |
| Stopped meds | Assess relapse risk |
| Fever + rigidity on antipsychotic | Suspect NMS |

---

# Medication Cues

| Medication / Class | Must Know |
|---|---|
| **SSRIs/SNRIs** | Takes weeks; serotonin syndrome; suicide risk early |
| **MAOIs** | Avoid tyramine; hypertensive crisis |
| **TCAs** | Anticholinergic effects; dangerous overdose |
| **Bupropion** | Seizure risk |
| **Benzodiazepines** | Sedation, falls, dependence, respiratory depression with alcohol/opioids |
| **Buspirone** | Takes weeks; not for immediate panic |
| **Lithium** | 0.6–1.2 mEq/L; toxicity; fluids/salt |
| **Antipsychotics** | EPS, tardive dyskinesia, NMS, metabolic effects |
| **Naloxone** | Opioid overdose reversal |
| **Disulfiram** | Avoid all alcohol |
| **Thiamine** | Alcohol-related Wernicke/Korsakoff prevention |

---

# Medication Emergency Cues

| Emergency | Clues | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| **Lithium toxicity** | Vomiting, diarrhea, tremor, confusion, ataxia, seizures | Hold lithium, notify provider |
| **Serotonin syndrome** | Fever, agitation, diarrhea, tremor, hyperreflexia | Hold med, notify provider |
| **NMS** | High fever, muscle rigidity, altered mental status, unstable VS | Emergency |
| **Tardive dyskinesia** | Lip smacking, tongue movements | Report |
| **Acute dystonia** | Neck spasm, eyes rolling upward | Report/treat |
| **Opioid overdose** | Respiratory depression, pinpoint pupils | Airway/breathing, naloxone |
| **DTs** | Hallucinations, seizures, severe withdrawal | Emergency |

---

# Restraints / Seclusion Cues

| Rule | Remember |
|---|---|
| Last resort only | Use after less restrictive measures fail |
| Not punishment | Never for convenience or staff frustration |
| Immediate danger only | Self or others |
| Monitor frequently | Airway, circulation, skin, vitals, toileting |
| Remove ASAP | As soon as patient is safe |
| Document clearly | Legal/safety requirement |

# "What Do I Do First?" Chart

| Scenario | First Action |
|---|---|
| Suicidal patient admitted | Search belongings/patient |
| "I want to die" | Ask directly about suicide |
| Plan + means | Stay with patient, remove objects |
| Voices present | Ask what voices are saying |
| Panic attack | Stay with patient |
| Manic patient not eating | Offer high-calorie finger foods |
| Manic patient intrusive | Set limits |
| Suspected abuse | Interview alone |
| Child/elder abuse | Report |
| Opioid overdose | Airway/breathing |
| Alcohol withdrawal | Monitor VS/seizure precautions |
| Fever + rigidity | Suspect NMS |

| Delusion | Do not argue; present reality |

# Final Takeaway

<div class="tip">

For mental health NCLEX questions, ask:

**1. Is anyone unsafe?** **2. Is this suicide, homicide, abuse, psychosis, overdose, withdrawal, or violence?** **3. Do I need to ask directly?** **4. Do I need to stay with the patient, remove objects, or call for help?** **5. Is this response therapeutic?** **6. Is this medication side effect expected or dangerous?**

</div>
