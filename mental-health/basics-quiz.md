# Mental Health Basics Practice Quiz

Test your knowledge of mental health foundational concepts, legal and ethical principles, and therapeutic relationships.

---

## Question 1

A nurse is preparing to admit a client to a psychiatric unit. Which action best demonstrates respect for the client's rights?

<p class="answer-choice">A. Inform the client of the unit rules and consequences for breaking them</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. Explain the client's rights, including the right to refuse treatment</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. Have the client sign all consent forms before providing any information</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. Ask the family to explain the admission process to reduce the client's anxiety</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: B</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">Clients have the right to be informed of their rights, including the right to refuse treatment, the right to confidentiality, and the right to be treated with dignity. Informing the client of rights first is foundational to ethical and legal nursing care.</p>
</details>

## Question 2

A client voluntarily admitted to a psychiatric unit states, "I want to leave the hospital right now." Which nursing response is most appropriate?

<p class="answer-choice">A. Tell the client they cannot leave until the provider discharges them</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. Notify the provider and document the request, as the client may leave with proper notice</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. Call security to prevent the client from leaving</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. Tell the client to wait 72 hours before requesting discharge</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: B</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">A voluntary client has the right to leave against medical advice with proper notice (usually 24–72 hours depending on state law). The nurse should notify the provider, who may petition for involuntary commitment if the client is a danger to self or others. Security cannot detain a voluntary client without legal cause.</p>
</details>

## Question 3

Which of the following best describes the concept of **least restrictive environment** in psychiatric care?

<p class="answer-choice">A. Clients should be housed in the quietest room available</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. Treatment should occur in the setting that allows the most freedom while ensuring safety</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. Restraints should only be applied in private rooms</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. Clients should receive the minimum amount of medication necessary</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: B</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">Least restrictive environment means clients receive care in the setting that provides the most independence while still maintaining safety. This principle guides decisions about hospitalization vs. outpatient care, and the use of interventions like restraints or seclusion only when less restrictive options have failed.</p>
</details>

## Question 4

A nurse is caring for a client with depression. The nurse says, "Tell me more about how you've been feeling." This is an example of which therapeutic communication technique?

<p class="answer-choice">A. Clarifying</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. Reflecting</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. Using a broad opening</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. Summarizing</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: C</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">A broad opening invites the client to choose what to focus on and encourages free expression. It is one of the most fundamental therapeutic communication techniques and signals the nurse's openness and interest in the client's perspective.</p>
</details>

## Question 5

Which of the following is an example of **countertransference**?

<p class="answer-choice">A. A client becomes emotionally dependent on the nurse and resists other staff</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. A nurse feels frustrated and impatient with a client who reminds her of her own mother</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. A client projects feelings of anger onto the nurse</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. A nurse maintains professional boundaries despite a client's attempts to create a personal relationship</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: B</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">Countertransference is when the nurse projects their own feelings or past relationships onto the client. This can interfere with therapeutic care. The nurse must be self-aware and seek supervision when countertransference is recognized. Option A is transference (client's feelings transferred to nurse).</p>
</details>

## Question 6

A client experiencing a psychotic episode is admitted involuntarily. The nurse understands that involuntary commitment is appropriate when:

<p class="answer-choice">A. The client refuses to take medications voluntarily</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. The family requests the client be admitted for evaluation</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. The client poses an imminent danger to self or others or is gravely disabled</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. The client's behavior is disruptive to others in the community</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: C</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">Involuntary commitment requires that the person be an imminent danger to themselves or others, or be so gravely disabled they cannot care for themselves. Refusing medication or family request alone does not justify involuntary commitment, which is a legal process that overrides client autonomy only under specific circumstances.</p>
</details>

## Question 7

A nurse overhears a colleague sharing a client's diagnosis with another client's family in the hallway. The nurse should:

<p class="answer-choice">A. Ignore it since the colleague likely had permission</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. Confront the colleague in the hallway immediately</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. Report the breach of confidentiality per facility policy</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. Document the incident in the client's chart</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: C</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">Sharing a client's confidential information without consent is a HIPAA violation. The nurse is obligated to report this through proper channels per facility policy, not ignore it or confront the colleague publicly. Documentation in the chart is not the primary response to another staff member's breach.</p>
</details>

## Question 8

The nurse is in the **working phase** of the therapeutic nurse-client relationship. Which activity is most characteristic of this phase?

<p class="answer-choice">A. Establishing trust and clarifying the goals of the relationship</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. Exploring the client's problems, coping patterns, and strengths in depth</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. Reviewing progress and preparing the client for discharge</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. Introducing self and explaining the nurse's role</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: B</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">The working phase is where most therapeutic work occurs: exploring problems, identifying patterns, developing coping skills, and making changes. The orientation phase establishes trust and goals. The termination phase reviews progress and prepares for discharge.</p>
</details>

## Question 9

A client asks the nurse, "Can you keep a secret? I want to tell you something but you can't tell anyone." What is the most appropriate response?

<p class="answer-choice">A. "Of course — everything you tell me stays between us."</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. "I can promise to keep your secret if it doesn't involve hurting yourself or others."</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. "I'm not able to promise secrecy, but I will keep information confidential unless there's a safety concern."</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. "Tell me first and I'll decide whether I can keep it secret."</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: C</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">The nurse must never promise absolute confidentiality because safety concerns must be reported. The nurse should be honest upfront: information will be kept confidential within the team, but safety disclosures must be shared. This maintains honesty and trust while protecting safety.</p>
</details>

## Question 10

Which behavior by the nurse best demonstrates **empathy** in the therapeutic relationship?

<p class="answer-choice">A. "I know exactly what you're going through."</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. "I feel so sorry for you — this must be terrible."</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. "It sounds like this situation has been exhausting and overwhelming for you."</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. "Don't worry — many people feel this way and they get through it."</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: C</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">Empathy means acknowledging and reflecting the client's emotional experience without claiming to share it. Option A is nontherapeutic (sympathy; assumes shared experience). Option B is pity/sympathy. Option D is false reassurance. Option C validates feelings without judgment.</p>
</details>

## Question 11

A nurse is caring for a client from a cultural background different from her own. The nurse's first priority is to:

<p class="answer-choice">A. Explain to the client that all clients on the unit are treated the same way</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. Ask the client about their cultural beliefs and preferences related to health and illness</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. Look up the cultural background online to prepare standard care</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. Assign the client to a nurse from the same culture</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: B</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">Culturally competent care begins with asking the individual client about their beliefs, values, and preferences — not assuming based on group membership. Each person is unique within their culture. Treating everyone "the same" ignores important cultural differences that affect care.</p>
</details>

## Question 12

Which of the following best describes **milieu therapy**?

<p class="answer-choice">A. Medication management in a hospital setting</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. Using the therapeutic environment itself as part of the treatment</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. Individual psychotherapy sessions with a psychiatrist</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. Group therapy focused on medication education</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: B</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">Milieu therapy uses the entire living environment — the physical space, staff interactions, peer relationships, unit routines, and group activities — as a therapeutic tool. Safety, structure, and community are key elements of a therapeutic milieu.</p>
</details>

## Question 13

A nurse uses **active listening** during a client interview. Which behaviors demonstrate active listening? **Select all that apply.**

<p class="answer-choice">A. Maintaining eye contact appropriate to the client's culture</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. Nodding and using minimal verbal encouragers such as "go on"</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. Completing documentation while the client speaks</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. Facing the client with an open posture</p>
<p class="answer-choice">E. Avoiding interrupting the client</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: A, B, D, E</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">Active listening involves nonverbal engagement (eye contact, open posture, nodding) and verbal acknowledgment while minimizing interruptions. Completing documentation while the client speaks signals distraction and is not therapeutic. Cultural sensitivity (Option A) is important because direct eye contact is not appropriate in all cultures.</p>
</details>

## Question 14

During the **termination phase** of the therapeutic relationship, a client becomes very clingy and asks the nurse to keep visiting after discharge. The best response is:

<p class="answer-choice">A. Agree to stay in touch since it will help the client's recovery</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. Acknowledge the client's feelings and clarify the professional boundaries of the relationship</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. Avoid the topic until the last day to prevent distress</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. Refer the client to another nurse to manage the boundary issue</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: B</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">Termination can trigger feelings of loss and abandonment. The nurse should acknowledge these feelings with empathy while reinforcing professional boundaries. Agreeing to personal contact after discharge crosses professional boundaries. Termination should be discussed throughout the relationship, not avoided.</p>
</details>

## Question 15

Which of the following is an example of a **boundary violation** by the nurse?

<p class="answer-choice">A. Asking the client how their weekend was</p>
<p class="answer-choice">B. Accepting a small, inexpensive gift from a client at discharge</p>
<p class="answer-choice">C. Sharing personal contact information with a client so they can reach out after discharge</p>
<p class="answer-choice">D. Using the client's preferred name rather than their legal name</p>

<details>
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer">Answer: C</p>
<p class="quiz-rationale">Sharing personal contact information crosses the professional boundary by shifting the relationship from professional to personal. This can cause harm to both the client (dependency, confusion about the relationship) and the nurse (liability). Therapeutic relationships exist within a defined professional context and end at discharge.</p>
</details>
