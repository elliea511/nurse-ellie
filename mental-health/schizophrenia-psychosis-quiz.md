## Question 1

<p>A nurse is caring for a client who has schizophrenia. The client states, “The voices are telling me to stab my roommate tonight.” Which action should the nurse take <strong>first</strong>?</p>

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> Ask the client when the voices began</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> Tell the client that the voices are not real</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> Remove the roommate from the area and initiate immediate safety precautions</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> Encourage the client to attend a distraction activity</p>

<details class="quiz-answer-block">
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: C</strong></p>
<div class="quiz-rationale">
<p><strong>Rationale:</strong></p>
<p><strong>A. Incorrect:</strong> Further assessment is necessary, but the client has already disclosed a specific command to harm another person. Immediate protection comes first.</p>
<p><strong>B. Incorrect:</strong> Directly arguing about the hallucination can increase mistrust and does not address the danger.</p>
<p><strong>C. Correct:</strong> Command hallucinations involving violence create an immediate risk to others. The nurse should separate the potential victim, remain with the client, remove hazards, and obtain assistance.</p>
<p><strong>D. Incorrect:</strong> Distraction may be helpful only after immediate safety has been secured.</p>
</div>
</details>

## Question 2

<p>A client who has schizophrenia tells the nurse, “The television is sending secret messages specifically to me.” Which response should the nurse make?</p>

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> “The television is not sending you messages, so you should ignore it.”</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> “What special message is the television sending?”</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> “That sounds frightening. I do not see evidence that the television is communicating with you.”</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> “You should turn off the television whenever that happens.”</p>

<details class="quiz-answer-block">
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: C</strong></p>
<div class="quiz-rationale">
<p><strong>Rationale:</strong></p>
<p><strong>A. Incorrect:</strong> This response directly argues with the delusion and may increase defensiveness.</p>
<p><strong>B. Incorrect:</strong> Asking for details can reinforce the delusional belief.</p>
<p><strong>C. Correct:</strong> The nurse acknowledges the client’s feelings, presents reality briefly, and avoids validating the delusion.</p>
<p><strong>D. Incorrect:</strong> Avoiding the television may reinforce the belief that the television is actually communicating with the client.</p>
</div>
</details>

## Question 3

<p>A nurse is caring for a client who has schizophrenia and suddenly begins staring at the corner of the room, whispering, and appearing frightened. Which action should the nurse take <strong>first</strong>?</p>

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> Ask the client what they are experiencing</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> Administer a PRN antipsychotic immediately</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> Move the client to seclusion</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> Tell the client to stop responding to the hallucination</p>

<details class="quiz-answer-block">
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: A</strong></p>
<div class="quiz-rationale">
<p><strong>Rationale:</strong></p>
<p><strong>A. Correct:</strong> The nurse should first assess whether the client is hallucinating, what the client is hearing or seeing, and whether the hallucination includes commands to harm self or others.</p>
<p><strong>B. Incorrect:</strong> Medication may be needed, but assessment comes first unless the client is actively violent or medically unstable.</p>
<p><strong>C. Incorrect:</strong> Seclusion is restrictive and is not indicated solely because the client appears to be hallucinating.</p>
<p><strong>D. Incorrect:</strong> Telling the client to stop is nontherapeutic and does not assess safety.</p>
</div>
</details>

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> Flat affect</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> Auditory hallucinations</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> Avolition</p>

<p><strong>E. Incorrect:</strong> Delusions are positive symptoms.</p>

## Question 5 — Select All That Apply

<p>A nurse is planning care for a client who is experiencing acute psychosis. Which interventions should the nurse include?</p>

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> Use short, simple statements</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> Maintain a calm, low-stimulation environment</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> Frequently touch the client to provide reassurance</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> Avoid whispering or laughing near the client</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>E.</strong> Challenge delusional beliefs until the client admits they are false</p>

<details class="quiz-answer-block">
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: A, B, D</strong></p>
<div class="quiz-rationale">
<p><strong>Rationale:</strong></p>
<p><strong>A. Correct:</strong> Short, concrete communication is easier for a client with impaired concentration and disorganized thinking to process.</p>
<p><strong>B. Correct:</strong> Reducing stimulation can decrease anxiety, suspiciousness, and psychotic escalation.</p>
<p><strong>C. Incorrect:</strong> Unexpected touch can increase fear or paranoia.</p>
<p><strong>D. Correct:</strong> Whispering or laughing nearby can intensify suspiciousness and ideas of reference.</p>
<p><strong>E. Incorrect:</strong> Arguing with delusions can damage trust and increase agitation.</p>
</div>
</details>

## Question 6

<p>A nurse is caring for a client who has schizophrenia and has been standing motionless in the same position for several hours. The client does not respond to questions and resists attempts to reposition them. Which action should the nurse take <strong>first</strong>?</p>

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> Assess the client’s airway, hydration status, and vital signs</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> Encourage the client to attend group therapy</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> Explain that remaining still is inappropriate</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> Ask the client to describe what they are thinking</p>

<details class="quiz-answer-block">
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: A</strong></p>
<div class="quiz-rationale">
<p><strong>Rationale:</strong></p>
<p><strong>A. Correct:</strong> Prolonged immobility can occur with catatonia and creates risks for dehydration, malnutrition, pressure injury, thromboembolism, and medical instability. The nurse should first assess physiological status.</p>
<p><strong>B. Incorrect:</strong> Group therapy is inappropriate while the client is unresponsive and medically at risk.</p>
<p><strong>C. Incorrect:</strong> The behavior is not simply willful misconduct, and confrontation is nontherapeutic.</p>
<p><strong>D. Incorrect:</strong> Further mental-status assessment may be needed, but immediate physiological safety takes priority.</p>
</div>
</details>

## Question 7

<p>A client who has schizophrenia tells the nurse, “My food has been poisoned, so I am not eating.” Which response should the nurse make?</p>

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> “You need to eat or you will become ill.”</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> “What proof do you have that the food is poisoned?”</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> “You seem frightened. I can offer you sealed foods and drinks.”</p>

<div class="quiz-rationale">
<p><strong>Rationale:</strong></p>
<p><strong>A. Incorrect:</strong> Directly arguing with the delusion may increase mistrust.</p>

</div>
</details>

## Question 11

<p>A nurse is assessing a client who has schizophrenia and takes clozapine. Which finding should the nurse report to the provider <strong>immediately</strong>?</p>

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> Mild drowsiness after the evening dose</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> Increased appetite and weight gain</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> Fever and sore throat</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> Dry mouth</p>

<details class="quiz-answer-block">
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: C</strong></p>
<div class="quiz-rationale">
<p><strong>Rationale:</strong></p>
<p><strong>A. Incorrect:</strong> Mild sedation can occur with clozapine and should be monitored, but it is not the most urgent finding.</p>
<p><strong>B. Incorrect:</strong> Weight gain is a significant adverse effect and requires ongoing monitoring, but it is not as immediately dangerous as possible agranulocytosis.</p>
<p><strong>C. Correct:</strong> Fever and sore throat can indicate severe neutropenia or agranulocytosis. Clozapine should be held, and the provider should be notified immediately.</p>
<p><strong>D. Incorrect:</strong> Dry mouth is an expected anticholinergic effect and is not the highest priority.</p>
</div>
</details>

## Question 12 — Select All That Apply

<p>A nurse is teaching a client who is starting clozapine. Which instructions should the nurse include?</p>

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> Keep appointments for required blood monitoring</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> Report fever, chills, or sore throat promptly</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> Rise slowly from sitting or lying positions</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> Stop the medication abruptly if constipation occurs</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>E.</strong> Report severe constipation or abdominal pain</p>

<details class="quiz-answer-block">
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: A, B, C, E</strong></p>
<div class="quiz-rationale">
<p><strong>Rationale:</strong></p>
<p><strong>A. Correct:</strong> Ongoing absolute neutrophil count monitoring is required because clozapine can cause severe neutropenia.</p>
<p><strong>B. Correct:</strong> Infection symptoms can indicate agranulocytosis and require immediate evaluation.</p>
<p><strong>C. Correct:</strong> Clozapine can cause orthostatic hypotension, especially during initiation and dose increases.</p>
<p><strong>D. Incorrect:</strong> The client should not stop clozapine abruptly without provider guidance. Constipation should be reported and treated because severe gastrointestinal hypomotility can occur.</p>
<p><strong>E. Correct:</strong> Severe constipation, abdominal pain, or distention can indicate ileus or bowel obstruction.</p>
</div>
</details>

## Question 13

<p>A nurse is caring for a client who recently started haloperidol. The client develops a sustained upward deviation of the eyes and painful neck muscle contractions. Which action should the nurse take <strong>first</strong>?</p>

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> Administer benztropine as prescribed</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> Encourage the client to rest in bed</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> Reassure the client that the symptoms will resolve without treatment</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> Hold all fluids until the muscle contractions stop</p>

<details class="quiz-answer-block">
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: A</strong></p>
<div class="quiz-rationale">
<p><strong>Rationale:</strong></p>
<p><strong>A. Correct:</strong> Oculogyric crisis and torticollis indicate acute dystonia, an extrapyramidal reaction. An anticholinergic medication such as benztropine or diphenhydramine should be administered promptly.</p>
<p><strong>B. Incorrect:</strong> Rest does not reverse the muscle contractions or protect the airway.</p>
<p><strong>C. Incorrect:</strong> Acute dystonia can compromise the airway and requires immediate treatment.</p>
<p><strong>D. Incorrect:</strong> Withholding fluids does not treat dystonia and may worsen dehydration.</p>
</div>
</details>

## Question 14

<p>A client who takes an antipsychotic paces continuously and states, “I feel like I am crawling out of my skin. I cannot sit still.” Which adverse effect should the nurse suspect?</p>

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> Akathisia</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> Acute dystonia</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> Neuroleptic malignant syndrome</p>

<details class="quiz-answer-block">

<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: B</strong></p>
<div class="quiz-rationale">
<p><strong>Rationale:</strong></p>
<p><strong>A. Incorrect:</strong> Tardive dyskinesia causes involuntary repetitive movements such as lip smacking, tongue protrusion, and facial grimacing.</p>

<p><strong>C. Incorrect:</strong> Acute dystonia causes painful muscle spasms, torticollis, or oculogyric crisis.</p>
<p><strong>D. Incorrect:</strong> NMS causes hyperthermia, severe rigidity, altered mental status, and autonomic instability.</p>
</div>
</details>
