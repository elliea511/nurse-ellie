## Question 1

A nurse is caring for a client in the emergency department who is pacing, trembling, and stating, “I can’t breathe. I’m dying. Something is seriously wrong.” The client is unable to answer assessment questions and keeps looking toward the exit.

Which action should the nurse take **first**?

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> Stay with the client and give short, simple directions in a calm voice.</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> Teach the client how to identify irrational thoughts that trigger panic.</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> Ask the client to describe what happened before the symptoms began.</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> Explain that panic attacks are not life-threatening and will pass soon.</p>

<details class="quiz-answer-block">
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: A</strong></p>

<div class="quiz-rationale">
<p><strong>Rationale:</strong></p>
A is correct. This client is experiencing **panic-level anxiety**, so the priority is to **stay with the client, maintain safety, reduce stimuli, and use short directions**. The client cannot process teaching or detailed questions during panic.

B is incorrect. Teaching coping skills is appropriate **after** the panic decreases.
C is incorrect. Asking for details requires concentration the client does not currently have.
D is incorrect. This is false reassurance and may make the client feel dismissed.


</div>
</details>

## Question 2

A client with obsessive-compulsive disorder spends 45 minutes washing their hands before meals. The nurse notes redness and cracking on both hands. The client states, “If I don’t wash correctly, something terrible will happen.”

Which nursing response is most appropriate?

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> “You need to stop washing so much because your hands are becoming injured.”</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> “Let’s work on gradually shortening the time you spend washing your hands.”</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> “There is no reason to believe something bad will happen if you do not wash.”</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> “Why do you believe handwashing prevents something terrible from happening?”</p>

<details class="quiz-answer-block">
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: B</strong></p>

<div class="quiz-rationale">
<p><strong>Rationale:</strong></p>
B is correct. OCD rituals should **not be abruptly blocked**. The nurse should help the client gradually reduce ritual time while teaching coping strategies.

A is incorrect. Suddenly stopping the ritual can increase anxiety and worsen behavior.
C is incorrect. This argues with the client’s belief and may increase defensiveness.
D is incorrect. “Why” questions are nontherapeutic and can make the client feel judged.


</div>
</details>

## Question 4

A nurse is teaching a client who has generalized anxiety disorder and a new prescription for buspirone.

Which client statement indicates an understanding of the teaching?

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> “I should take this medication only when I feel a panic attack starting.”</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> “I should avoid taking this medication for more than a few days because it is addictive.”</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> “I will need to take this medication consistently before I notice the full effect.”</p>

<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: C</strong></p>

<div class="quiz-rationale">
<p><strong>Rationale:</strong></p>

D is incorrect. The client should not stop medication without provider guidance.

## Question 7

A nurse is providing discharge teaching to a client who has panic disorder and a new prescription for lorazepam.

Which statement by the client indicates the **greatest need for further teaching**?

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> “I will avoid drinking alcohol while taking this medication.”</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> “I will change positions slowly until I know how this medication affects me.”</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> “I will not stop this medication suddenly if I have been taking it regularly.”</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> “I can take an extra dose if I still feel anxious after the first dose.”</p>

<details class="quiz-answer-block">
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: D</strong></p>

<div class="quiz-rationale">
<p><strong>Rationale:</strong></p>
D is correct. The client should take benzodiazepines **only as prescribed**. Extra doses increase risk for excessive sedation, falls, dependence, and respiratory depression.

C is incorrect. Benzodiazepines should not be stopped abruptly after regular use due to withdrawal risk.

## Question 11

A nurse is reinforcing teaching with a client who has a specific phobia of elevators. The client states, “My provider said therapy may involve slowly helping me tolerate elevators, but I don’t understand how that helps.”

Which response should the nurse make?

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> “The goal is to avoid elevators until your anxiety fully resolves.”</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> “You will be exposed to elevators all at once until the fear disappears.”</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> “Gradual exposure while using relaxation can help reduce the fear response over time.”</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> “Medication is usually the only effective treatment for phobias.”</p>

<details class="quiz-answer-block">
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: C</strong></p>

<div class="quiz-rationale">
<p><strong>Rationale:</strong></p>
