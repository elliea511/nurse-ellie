# Personality Disorders

<div class="note">
Personality disorders are long-standing patterns of behavior, mood, thinking, and relationships that become <strong>rigid, maladaptive, and distressing.</strong>
For NCLEX, focus on: <strong>boundaries, limit-setting, safety, manipulation, splitting, team consistency, coping skills, and therapeutic communication.</strong>
</div>

---

## Big Picture

| Concept | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Personality trait** | Usual pattern of behavior or thinking |
| **Personality disorder** | Traits become rigid, unhealthy, and cause problems |
| **Long-standing pattern** | Usually not sudden; affects relationships and functioning |
| **External locus of control** | Patient blames others/events for their behavior |
| **Poor insight** | Patient may not believe they need treatment |
| **Main nursing focus** | Boundaries, safety, consistency, and coping skills |

<div class="note">
Personality disorder patients are usually not hospitalized for the personality disorder alone. They may be hospitalized for a coexisting issue such as depression, anxiety, substance use, self-harm, or suicide risk.
</div>

---

# Personality Disorder Clusters

| Cluster | Description | Disorders |
|---|---|---|
| **Cluster A** | Odd or eccentric | Paranoid, Schizoid, Schizotypal |
| **Cluster B** | Dramatic, emotional, erratic | Antisocial, Borderline, Histrionic, Narcissistic |
| **Cluster C** | Anxious or fearful | Avoidant, Dependent, Obsessive-Compulsive Personality |

<div class="tip">
<strong>A = Awkward/Odd &nbsp;&nbsp; B = Big emotions/Behavioral drama &nbsp;&nbsp; C = Concerned/Fearful</strong>
</div>

# General Assessment

| Assessment Area | Questions / Focus |
|---|---|
| Current stressors | "What is happening in your life that feels distressing?" |
| Support system | "Who is supportive of you?" |
| Reason for treatment | "What changes would you like to see?" |
| Coping methods | "How do you handle anxious or angry feelings?" |
| Self-harm risk | "Have you ever done anything to hurt yourself?" |
| Violence/criminal history | "Have you been in trouble with the legal system?" |
| Interaction style | Withdrawn, suspicious, manipulative, controlling, dependent |
| Objective/subjective data | What the nurse observes vs what patient reports |

---

## General Treatment Approach

| Treatment Focus | Why |
|---|---|
| Short-term problem focus | Patient may not believe they need long-term treatment |
| Clear limits | Reduces manipulation and unsafe behavior |
| Team consistency | Prevents splitting staff |
| Group therapy | Helps social skills if patient can participate |
| Role-playing | Practices healthier interactions |
| Medications if ordered | Antianxiety, antipsychotic, antidepressant, mood stabilizer depending on symptoms |
| Therapy | CBT, behavior therapy, psychotherapy |

<div class="warning">
<strong>NCLEX:</strong> The treatment team must work together as one team. Inconsistent staff responses increase manipulation and splitting.
</div>

---

# General Nursing Interventions

| Nursing Action | Why |
|---|---|
| Set firm, clear limits | Provides structure and safety |
| Be consistent | Prevents manipulation |
| Avoid power struggles | Keeps interaction therapeutic |
| Do not take insults personally | Patient behavior is part of disorder pattern |
| Encourage responsibility | Helps internal locus of control |
| Reinforce appropriate behavior | Builds healthier patterns |
| Document objectively | Avoids judgment |
| Assess self-harm/violence risk | Safety priority |
| Communicate with team | Prevents staff splitting |

---

# Cluster A: Odd / Eccentric

Cluster A includes paranoid, schizoid, and schizotypal personality disorders. The main theme is **social detachment, suspicion, odd behavior, or unusual beliefs.**

---

# Paranoid Personality Disorder

Paranoid personality disorder involves distrust and suspicion. The patient interprets others' motives as threatening.

## Assessment

| Finding | What It Means |
|---|---|
| Distrustful and suspicious | Believes others are trying to trick or harm them |
| Hypervigilant | Scans environment for danger |
| Overuses projection | Blames own hostile feelings on others |
| Prefers to be alone | Lack of trust |
| Jealous/suspicious of partner | Misinterprets behavior |
| Finds meaning in small events | Thinks minor events are threatening |
| Rigid beliefs | Rejects information they disagree with |
| Needs control | Control decreases fear |

---

## Nursing Care

| Nursing Action | Why |
|---|---|
| Be honest and consistent | Builds trust slowly |
| Explain care before doing it | Decreases suspicion |
| Allow participation in care planning | Increases sense of control |
| Avoid whispering/laughing nearby | May be misinterpreted |
| Use clear, simple language | Prevents misinterpretation |
| Do not argue about suspicious beliefs | Increases defensiveness |
| Teach "I" statements | Encourages responsibility |
| Help consider other explanations | Gently broadens thinking |

<div class="note">
For paranoid personality disorder, trust reduces anxiety. Be consistent, respectful, and clear.
</div>

---

# Schizoid Personality Disorder

Schizoid personality disorder involves social withdrawal and limited emotional expression.

## Assessment

| Finding |
|---|
| Withdrawn |
| Avoids relationships |
| Lacks interest in social interaction |
| Restricted emotional responses |
| Appears detached |
| May choose solitary jobs |
| Little interest in intimacy |
| Poor social skills |
| May have imaginary friends in childhood |

## Nursing Care

| Nursing Action | Why |
|---|---|
| Respect need for distance | Patient may prefer limited interaction |
| Do not force socialization suddenly | Can increase discomfort |
| Encourage gradual interaction | Builds tolerance |
| Use calm, nonintrusive approach | Reduces pressure |
| Support ADLs and functioning | Practical focus |
| Medications if ordered | Antidepressants/antipsychotics may be used |

---

# Schizotypal Personality Disorder

Schizotypal personality disorder involves social deficits plus odd beliefs, strange behavior, and unusual speech.

## Assessment

| Finding | What It Looks Like |
|---|---|
| Social anxiety | Acute discomfort around others |
| Odd beliefs | Magical thinking or unusual ideas |
| Ideas of reference | Believes unrelated events refer to them |
| Strange speech | Rambling, abstract, unusual wording |
| Aloof behavior | Socially withdrawn |
| Strange mannerisms | Odd dress/behavior |
| Suspiciousness | Anxiety in social settings |
| Little interest in sex | May appear indifferent |

---

## Nursing Care

| Nursing Action | Why |
|---|---|
| Gradual group involvement | Prevents overwhelm |
| Give positive feedback for appropriate behavior | Reinforces social skills |
| Support social attempts | Builds confidence |
| Do not mock odd beliefs | Maintains trust |
| Assess for psychosis | Symptoms can overlap |
| Meds if ordered | Antipsychotics, antidepressants, antianxiety meds |

---

# Cluster B: Dramatic / Emotional / Erratic

Cluster B includes antisocial, borderline, histrionic, and narcissistic personality disorders. The main theme is **impulsivity, intense emotions, manipulation, boundary problems, and relationship instability.**

<div class="warning">
Cluster B questions often test safety, limits, manipulation, splitting, and staff consistency.
</div>

---

# Antisocial Personality Disorder

Antisocial personality disorder involves disregard for and violation of the rights of others. The person must be at least **18 years old** and often has a history of conduct disorder as a teen.

## Assessment

| Finding | What It Means |
|---|---|
| Manipulative | Uses others for control |
| Lacks empathy | Does not care about others' rights |
| Superficially charming | May appear friendly to get needs met |
| Lies/cheats | Lack of conscience |
| Impulsive | Acts without thinking |
| Irresponsible | Does not follow rules/commitments |
| Aggressive when frustrated | May become abusive |
| No guilt/remorse | Does not feel badly about behavior |
| Criminal/legal history | Common pattern |

---

## Nursing Priorities

| Priority | Nursing Action |
|---|---|
| Safety | Protect staff and other patients |
| Unit control | Maintain structure and rules |
| Boundaries | Set firm limits |
| Teamwork | Do not allow staff manipulation |
| Anger outlets | Provide safe physical outlets |
| Accountability | Connect actions with consequences |

<div class="warning">
<strong>NCLEX:</strong> For antisocial personality disorder, safety for staff and other patients is the highest concern.
</div>

---

## Nursing Care

| Do | Avoid |
|---|---|
| Set firm limits | Bargaining |
| Enforce consequences consistently | Making exceptions |
| Stay calm and neutral | Power struggles |
| Document behavior objectively | Emotional reactions |
| Use team approach | Keeping secrets |
| Provide safe outlets for anger | Ignoring threats |

---

# Borderline Personality Disorder

Borderline personality disorder involves unstable mood, impulsivity, fear of abandonment, unstable relationships, and possible self-injury.

## Assessment

| Finding | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| Rapid mood swings | Emotional instability |
| Fear of abandonment | May cling, panic, or manipulate |
| Intense unstable relationships | Love/hate pattern |
| Splitting | Sees people as all good or all bad |
| Impulsive behavior | Substance use, unsafe sex, spending |
| Self-injurious behavior | Cutting or other harm |
| Suicidal ideas | Safety priority |
| Inappropriate anger | May become verbally/physically aggressive |
| Chronic emptiness/boredom | May trigger risky behavior |
| Projection | Cannot recognize own negative feelings |

---

## Splitting

Splitting means seeing people as **all good or all bad.**

Example: "This nurse is amazing. That nurse is evil."

<div class="warning">
<strong>NCLEX:</strong> Splitting requires consistent staff communication. Do not take sides.
</div>

---

## Nursing Care

| Nursing Action | Why |
|---|---|
| Assess suicide/self-harm risk | Safety priority |
| Maintain clear boundaries | Prevents dependency/manipulation |
| Be supportive but consistent | Helps abandonment fears without enabling |
| Do not reinforce dependency | Encourage independence |
| Teach coping/problem-solving | Reduces impulsive reactions |
| Encourage safe anger outlets | Reduces aggression/self-harm |
| Use team communication | Prevents splitting |
| Group/behavior therapy | Builds social skills and emotional regulation |

---

## Borderline Communication Examples

| Patient Says | Best Response |
|---|---|
| "You are the only nurse who cares." | "All of the staff are here to help you." |
| "If you leave, I'll hurt myself." | "Your safety is important. I will notify the team and stay with you while we make a safety plan." |
| "That nurse hates me." | "The treatment plan is the same with all staff." |
| "You don't care about me." | "I hear that you feel abandoned. I will return at the scheduled time." |

---

# Histrionic Personality Disorder

Histrionic personality disorder involves excessive emotional expression and attention-seeking behavior.

## Assessment

| Finding |
|---|
| Dramatic behavior |
| Exaggerated emotions |
| Needs attention |
| Temper tantrums when ignored |
| Flamboyant or sexually inappropriate behavior |
| Manipulates for affection |
| Difficulty maintaining relationships |
| May fake illness or develop somatic symptoms for attention |
| Suicidal gestures may occur |

<div class="warning">
Even if behavior seems attention-seeking, always assess suicidal thoughts and safety.
</div>

---

## Nursing Care

| Nursing Action | Why |
|---|---|
| Set limits on attention-seeking behavior | Maintains structure |
| Do not shame the patient | Keeps care therapeutic |
| Reinforce appropriate expression of feelings | Builds healthier coping |
| Encourage identification of true feelings | Reduces dramatic expression |
| Keep group therapy structured | Prevents disruption |
| Assess suicide risk | Gestures can still be dangerous |
| Meds if ordered | Antidepressants, antianxiety, antipsychotics may be used |

---

# Narcissistic Personality Disorder

Narcissistic personality disorder involves grandiosity, need for admiration, and lack of empathy.

## Assessment

| Finding | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Grandiose | Exaggerates importance |
| Needs admiration | Wants constant praise |
| Feels entitled | Expects special treatment |
| Arrogant | May talk down to others |
| Hypersensitive to criticism | Low self-esteem underneath |
| Lacks empathy | Difficulty caring about others' needs |
| Exploits others | Uses others for personal gain |
| Rationalizes failure | Blames or excuses problems |

---

## Nursing Care

| Nursing Action | Why |
|---|---|
| Avoid power struggles | Patient may become defensive |
| Do not take insults personally | Maintains professionalism |
| Set limits respectfully | Prevents special treatment demands |
| Be a role model for empathy | Supports treatment goal |
| Give realistic feedback | Avoids feeding grandiosity |
| Group therapy may help | Develops empathy |
| CBT may be used | Challenges distorted beliefs |

---

# Cluster C: Anxious / Fearful

Cluster C includes avoidant, dependent, and obsessive-compulsive personality disorders. The main theme is **fear, anxiety, insecurity, control, and difficulty with independence.**

---

# Avoidant Personality Disorder

Avoidant personality disorder involves social inhibition, feelings of inadequacy, and hypersensitivity to criticism or rejection.

## Assessment

| Finding |
|---|
| Feels inadequate |
| Fear of rejection |
| Fear of humiliation |
| Socially inhibited |
| Wants acceptance but avoids closeness |
| Self-depreciating |
| Feels inferior/different |
| Avoids social situations |
| Awkward and aware of poor social skills |

## Nursing Care

| Nursing Action | Why |
|---|---|
| Be accepting and friendly | Builds trust |
| Avoid criticism/shaming | Patient is highly sensitive |
| Teach social skills | Improves confidence |
| Teach assertiveness | Helps expression |
| Encourage gradual group involvement | Reduces avoidance |
| Give positive feedback | Reinforces effort |
| Meds if ordered | SSRIs, antianxiety meds |

---

# Dependent Personality Disorder

Dependent personality disorder involves submissive, clingy behavior and excessive need to be taken care of.

## Assessment

| Finding | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Clingy behavior | Fear of being alone |
| Low self-confidence | Does not trust own decisions |
| Difficulty making decisions | Needs reassurance |
| Passive in relationships | Avoids disagreement |
| Separation anxiety | Fear of losing caregiver/support person |
| Devalues own abilities | Feels unable to function alone |
| Takes care of others but not self | Maintains relationship by being needed |

---

## Nursing Care

| Nursing Action | Why |
|---|---|
| Encourage decision-making | Builds independence |
| Avoid doing everything for patient | Reinforces dependency |
| Give choices | Helps patient practice autonomy |
| Praise independent behavior | Builds confidence |
| Teach assertiveness | Helps express needs/opinions |
| Set boundaries | Prevents clinginess |
| Therapy/CBT | Develops new attitudes and behaviors |

Example: "I will support you while you choose which activity to attend today."

---

# Obsessive-Compulsive Personality Disorder

Obsessive-compulsive personality disorder is **not the same as OCD.** OCPD is a personality pattern of orderliness, perfectionism, rigidity, and control.

## OCPD vs OCD

| OCPD | OCD |
|---|---|
| Personality disorder | Anxiety/OCD-related disorder |
| Pattern of perfectionism/control | Obsessions and compulsions |
| Patient may think behavior is correct | Patient often recognizes rituals are excessive |
| Rigid and inflexible | Rituals reduce anxiety |
| Focused on rules/order | Focused on intrusive thoughts/rituals |

---

## OCPD Assessment

| Finding |
|---|
| Preoccupied with orderliness |
| Perfectionism |
| Rigid/inflexible |
| Needs control |
| Fears failure |
| Critical of self and others |
| Difficulty delegating |
| May struggle with relationships due to high standards |

## Nursing Care

| Nursing Action | Why |
|---|---|
| Avoid power struggles | Control is a major issue |
| Give clear expectations | Reduces anxiety |
| Allow some control when safe | Increases cooperation |
| Encourage flexibility | Helps functioning |
| Assess defense mechanisms | Guides interventions |
| Be aware of self-critical nature | Patient may be harsh on self/others |

---

# Anxious-Fearful Cluster Treatment

| Intervention | Why |
|---|---|
| Establish caring, consistent relationship | Builds trust |
| Set clear expectations | Reduces anxiety |
| Teach assertiveness | Promotes healthy communication |
| Expect decision-making | Builds independence |
| Identify positive qualities | Improves self-esteem |
| Give positive feedback | Reinforces progress |
| Teach stress management | Keeps anxiety level low |
| Teach relaxation | Supports coping |

---

# Personality Disorder Medications

Medications do not "cure" personality disorders, but may help with symptoms or comorbid disorders.

| Medication Type | Possible Use |
|---|---|
| **Antianxiety meds** | Anxiety, agitation |
| **Antidepressants** | Depression, anxiety, mood symptoms |
| **Mood stabilizers** | Mood swings, impulsivity |
| **Antipsychotics** | Paranoia, severe agitation, psychotic-like symptoms |
| **Naltrexone** | May be used to decrease self-injurious behavior in some cases |

# Priority Charts

## Cluster A Priority Chart

| Situation | First Nursing Action |
|---|---|
| Paranoid patient distrusts staff | Be consistent and explain care clearly |
| Paranoid patient refuses care due to suspicion | Allow participation and build trust |
| Schizoid patient avoids group | Encourage gradual interaction without forcing |
| Schizotypal patient has odd beliefs | Do not mock; assess safety and support functioning |

---

## Cluster B Priority Chart

| Situation | First Nursing Action |
|---|---|
| Antisocial patient threatens others | Maintain safety and set firm limits |
| Antisocial patient manipulates staff | Use team consistency |
| Borderline patient threatens self-harm | Assess suicide risk and maintain safety |
| Borderline patient splits staff | Communicate with team and keep plan consistent |
| Histrionic patient makes suicidal gesture | Assess suicide risk |
| Narcissistic patient insults nurse | Do not take personally; avoid power struggle |

---

## Cluster C Priority Chart

| Situation | First Nursing Action |
|---|---|
| Avoidant patient fears group | Support gradual exposure and give positive feedback |
| Dependent patient asks nurse to decide everything | Encourage patient to make choice |
| OCPD patient becomes rigid about routine | Avoid power struggle and offer safe choices |
| Anxious-fearful patient lacks assertiveness | Teach assertive communication |

---

# Therapeutic Communication

| Patient Statement | Best Response |
|---|---|
| "Everyone here is against me." | "You feel unsafe. I will explain what we are doing." |
| "You are the only good nurse." | "All staff are here to help you." |
| "If you leave, I'll hurt myself." | "I take that seriously. I will stay with you and notify the team." |
| "I cannot decide. You choose." | "I can help you look at the options, but I want you to choose." |
| "I deserve special treatment." | "The rules are the same for everyone." |
| "That group will reject me." | "It sounds scary to join. We can start with a small step." |

# Final Takeaway

<div class="tip">
For personality disorder questions, ask:
<br><br>
<strong>1. Is there a safety risk?</strong><br>
<strong>2. Does the patient need limits or boundaries?</strong><br>
<strong>3. Is the staff being consistent?</strong><br>
<strong>4. Is the patient manipulating, splitting, withdrawing, or depending?</strong><br>
<strong>5. How can I encourage responsibility and healthier coping?</strong>
</div>
