# Abuse and Suicide

<div class="note">
Abuse and suicide questions are <strong>safety-first</strong> questions.
Think: <strong>Is the patient safe? Do I need to ask directly? Do I need to remove harmful objects? Do I need to report suspected abuse?</strong>
</div>

---

## Big Picture

| Topic | Nurse Focus |
|---|---|
| **Suicide** | Ask directly, assess plan/means/lethality, prevent harm |
| **Self-injury** | Assess intent, triggers, coping, and safety |
| **Abuse** | Interview privately, assess safety, document, report when required |
| **Environment** | Remove harmful objects and maintain supervision |
| **Communication** | Calm, direct, nonjudgmental, supportive |

<div class="warning">
<strong>NCLEX:</strong> Safety comes before teaching, coping skills, or long-term therapy.
</div>

---

# Suicide Basics

## Key Terms

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Suicidal ideation** | Thinking about killing oneself |
| **Suicide attempt** | Self-harm with intent to die |
| **Suicide** | Intentional act of killing oneself |
| **Non-suicidal self-injury** | Self-harm without conscious intent to die |
| **Lethality** | How likely the method is to cause death or serious harm |

---

## Suicide Risk Factors and Warning Signs

| Risk Factor / Warning Sign | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| Previous suicide attempt | Strong predictor of future attempt |
| Family history of suicide | Increases risk |
| Depression, bipolar, schizophrenia, substance use | Common mental health links |
| Drug/alcohol use | Lowers impulse control |
| Impulsivity | Patient may act quickly |
| Giving things away | May show preparation |
| Sudden switch from sad to happy | May mean patient has decided on suicide |
| Statements like "I won't be a bother much longer" | Covert suicide clue |
| Specific plan | Higher risk |
| Access to method | Patient can carry out plan |
| Poor support system | Less protection |

---

## Suicide Assessment

Ask directly. Talking about suicide does **not** give the patient the idea.

| Question | What It Checks |
|---|---|
| "Are you thinking about killing yourself?" | Suicidal ideation |
| "Do you have a plan?" | Specificity |
| "What is your plan?" | Lethality |
| "Do you have access to that method?" | Means |
| "When were you planning to do this?" | Immediacy |
| "Have you attempted suicide before?" | History |
| "Who can support you right now?" | Support system |
| "Do you feel safe being alone?" | Current safety |

<div class="warning">
<strong>Highest risk:</strong> specific plan + lethal method + access to means + intent to act.
</div>

---

## Lethality

| Lower Lethality | Higher Lethality |
|---|---|
| Superficial cutting | Firearm |
| Small pill ingestion | Hanging |
| Vague plan | Carbon monoxide |
| No access to method | Serious overdose with access |
| No set time | Jumping from height |

<div class="note">
A self-inflicted gunshot wound is considered a highly lethal method.
</div>

# Care of the Suicidal Patient

## Priority Interventions

| Priority | Nursing Action |
|---|---|
| **Prevent harm** | Search patient/belongings and remove harmful objects |
| **Supervise** | Maintain 1:1 observation if indicated |
| **Assess** | Ask about plan, means, intent, and past attempts |
| **Safe environment** | Open door, safe room, remove ligature risks |
| **Notify team** | Provider, charge nurse, mental health team |
| **Document** | Exact statements, risk level, actions taken |

---

## Environmental Safety

Remove or monitor:
- Belts
- Shoelaces
- Razors
- Scissors
- Tweezers
- Nail files
- Matches/lighters
- Cords
- Glass
- Medications
- Perfume/shampoo
- Plastic bags
- Weapons

| Safety Guideline | Why |
|---|---|
| Search belongings with patient present | Safety + dignity |
| Use and count plastic utensils | Prevents hidden sharp objects |
| Keep door open | Allows observation |
| Avoid private room if unsafe | Reduces isolation risk |
| Use hang-proof/jump-proof bathroom if available | Prevents self-harm |
| Recheck after leaving unit | Patient may obtain unsafe items |

<div class="warning">
<strong>NCLEX:</strong> For a newly admitted suicidal patient, search belongings and remove harmful objects first.
</div>

---

## Therapeutic Communication for Suicide

| Patient Says | Best Response |
|---|---|
| "I don't want to live anymore." | "Are you thinking about killing yourself?" |
| "I have a way to end it." | "What is your plan?" |
| "I won't be a bother much longer." | "Are you having thoughts of suicide?" |
| "Promise you won't tell anyone." | "I cannot promise secrecy if your safety is at risk." |
| "I feel better now." | "I still need to make sure you are safe. Are you having suicidal thoughts?" |

Avoid:
- "You have so much to live for."
- "Everything will be fine."
- "You would never do that."
- "That is selfish."
- "Why would you do that?"
- Promising secrecy

# Non-Suicidal Self-Injury

Self-injury is intentional body harm without conscious intent to die, but the nurse still must assess suicide risk.

Examples: cutting, burning, scratching, picking wounds, hitting self.

## Nursing Care

| Nursing Action | Why |
|---|---|
| Treat injuries | Prevent infection/complications |
| Assess suicide intent | Self-harm can overlap with suicide risk |
| Identify triggers | Helps prevent recurrence |
| Use empathy | Reduces shame |
| Teach coping skills | Gives safer alternatives |
| Encourage reflection | Helps connect feelings and behavior |

Coping options: deep breathing, grounding, journaling, calling support person, walking, music/art, holding ice instead of cutting, crisis plan.

# Abuse Basics

| Type | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Physical abuse** | Bodily harm or harsh punishment |
| **Sexual abuse** | Unwanted sexual contact or exploitation |
| **Emotional/verbal abuse** | Words/actions that harm self-worth |
| **Neglect** | Basic needs are not met |
| **Medical neglect** | Needed care, meds, visits, or immunizations withheld |
| **Economic/financial abuse** | No access to money or resources |
| **Exploitation** | Using another person for profit |

<div class="note">
Substance abuse in the family increases the risk of abuse.
</div>

---

## Abuse Assessment Clues

| Finding | Possible Concern |
|---|---|
| Injury explanation does not match injury | Possible abuse |
| Delay in seeking care | Patient may be afraid |
| Bruises in different stages of healing | Repeated trauma |
| Frequent injuries/ED visits | Ongoing abuse |
| Patient fears caregiver/partner | Control or threat |
| Visitor answers for patient | Patient may not speak freely |
| Poor hygiene, malnutrition, dehydration | Neglect |
| Missing medications/appointments | Medical neglect |
| Anxiety, depression, PTSD, suicidal thoughts | Abuse-related mental health effects |

---

## Abuse Interview

If abuse is suspected, make every effort to interview the patient **alone**.

| Do | Avoid |
|---|---|
| Use a private room | Interviewing in front of suspected abuser |
| Ask open-ended questions | Blaming the victim |
| Use attentive listening | Pressuring for details |
| Assess safety | Confronting the suspected abuser |
| Ask about weapons | Promising secrecy |
| Assess anxiety and suicide risk | Telling the patient they "must leave" |
| Provide resources | Using family as interpreter |
| Document carefully | Ignoring inconsistent injury stories |

<div class="note">
Do not force the victim to discuss details they are not ready to share.
</div>

# Mandatory Reporting

| Group | Report? |
|---|---|
| Children | Yes |
| Older adults | Yes |
| Vulnerable/dependent adults | Yes |
| Disabled adults unable to protect self | Yes |
| Adult intimate partner violence | Follow state/facility policy; still assess safety and provide resources |

<div class="warning">
For NCLEX: suspected child abuse and elder abuse must be reported. Suspicion is enough.
</div>

---

# Documentation

| Avoid | Better |
|---|---|
| "Patient was beaten." | "Patient states, 'My boyfriend punched me.'" |
| "Mother is abusive." | "Bruising noted to bilateral upper arms." |
| "Patient is lying." | "Explanation is inconsistent with injury pattern." |
| "Patient is dramatic." | "Patient tearful and shaking during interview." |

Include: exact patient quotes, injury location/size/color/shape, patient behavior, who was present, safety concerns, who was notified, resources provided.

---

# Child Abuse

Children may not clearly report abuse, so the nurse watches for clues.

| Clue | Concern |
|---|---|
| Bruises on back, buttocks, ears, neck, upper arms | Suspicious injury location |
| Injuries in different healing stages | Repeated abuse |
| Patterned burns | Physical abuse |
| Fractures inconsistent with history | Possible abuse |
| Poor hygiene/malnutrition | Neglect |
| Missed medical care/immunizations | Medical neglect |
| Sexualized behavior beyond age | Possible sexual abuse |
| Fear of caregiver | Safety concern |

### Priority

1. Ensure safety
2. Assess injuries
3. Report suspected abuse
4. Document objectively
5. Do not confront caregiver

Helpful response to child: *"I'm glad you told me. You are not in trouble."*

---

# Elder Abuse

Elder abuse may include physical, emotional, sexual, financial abuse, neglect, or abandonment.

| Clue | Concern |
|---|---|
| Unexplained weight loss | Neglect |
| Bruising/fractures | Physical abuse |
| Pressure injuries | Neglect/poor care |
| Poor hygiene | Neglect |
| Missed appointments | Withholding care |
| Missing medications | Medical neglect |
| Fear of caregiver | Control or abuse |
| Caregiver refuses privacy | Red flag |
| Pension checks taken | Financial abuse |
| Forced to sign property over | Exploitation |

<div class="note">
For an older client with unexplained weight loss and bruising, first collect physical data and report findings to the charge nurse/provider.
</div>

# Domestic Violence

Domestic violence is abuse by a current or former partner. It may include physical violence, emotional abuse, sexual coercion, financial control, isolation, threats, and control of phone, money, transportation, children, or pets.

## Cycle of Abuse

| Phase | What Happens |
|---|---|
| **Tension-building** | Stress increases; victim feels like they are "walking on eggshells" |
| **Acute abuse/explosion** | Abuse occurs |
| **Honeymoon** | Abuser apologizes, gives gifts, promises change |
| **Calm** | Temporary peace before cycle repeats |

### Questions to Ask (when alone)

- "Do you feel safe at home?"
- "Has anyone hit, kicked, choked, or hurt you?"
- "Has anyone threatened you?"
- "Are there weapons in the home?"
- "Are you afraid of your partner?"
- "Does anyone control your money, phone, or transportation?"
- "Do you have a safe place to go if needed?"

### Safety Plan

| Item | Examples |
|---|---|
| Important papers | ID, insurance cards, birth certificates |
| Money | Cash/card hidden safely |
| Keys | Duplicate car/house keys |
| Contacts | Trusted person, shelter, hotline |
| Safe place | Friend, family, shelter |
| Emergency bag | Clothes, medications, papers |
| Weapon plan | Know if weapons are in home |
| Children/pets plan | Safe exit plan for dependents |

<div class="note">
Do not simply tell the victim to leave. Leaving can be dangerous. Assess safety, provide resources, and help make a safety plan.
</div>

# Sexual Assault / Rape Trauma Syndrome

Rape trauma syndrome is a PTSD-like response after rape or sexual assault.

## Nursing Priorities

| Priority | Nursing Action |
|---|---|
| Safety | Make sure patient is safe from attacker |
| Medical stability | Treat injuries and urgent needs |
| Emotional support | Be empathetic and nonjudgmental |
| Privacy | Provide a private room |
| Patient control | Explain care and ask permission |
| Evidence | Preserve evidence per policy |
| Resources | SANE nurse, advocate, counseling |
| Follow-up | STI/pregnancy prevention as ordered |

Helpful responses: *"I believe you." / "This was not your fault." / "You are safe here." / "You are in control of what happens next."*

Avoid: *"Why were you there?" / "Why didn't you fight back?" / "Are you sure?" / "You should have called sooner."*

# Therapeutic Communication

| Technique | Example |
|---|---|
| **Silence** | Sit quietly without rushing |
| **Active listening** | Calm presence, eye contact, nodding |
| **Clarifying** | "What do you mean by unsafe?" |
| **Open-ended questions** | "Tell me what happened." |
| **Restating** | "You feel scared to go home." |
| **Offering self** | "I can stay with you while we talk." |

# Priority Charts

## Suicide

| Situation | First Nursing Action |
|---|---|
| Newly admitted after suicide attempt | Search patient/belongings for harmful objects |
| "I want to die." | Ask directly about suicide |
| Suicidal thoughts present | Assess plan, means, intent, lethality |
| Plan + access to method | Stay with patient and remove harmful objects |
| Giving belongings away | Assess suicide risk |
| Sudden happy mood after depression | Assess suicide risk |
| Command hallucinations | Ask what the voices are saying |

## Abuse

| Situation | First Nursing Action |
|---|---|
| Controlling visitor answers for patient | Interview patient alone safely |
| Child has suspicious injuries | Assess and report suspected abuse |
| Elder has bruising/weight loss | Collect physical data and report findings |
| Sexual assault disclosure | Ensure safety and support patient |
| Victim afraid to leave | Assess safety and provide safety plan |
| Injury story does not fit | Further assess and document objectively |

## Final Takeaway
