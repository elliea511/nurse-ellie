# Abuse and Suicide

<p class="mh-subtitle">Safety-first review for suicide risk, self-injury, abuse, and crisis care</p>

Abuse and suicide questions are **safety-first** questions.

Think: **Is the patient safe? Do I need to ask directly? Do I need to remove harmful objects? Do I need to report suspected abuse?**

<div class="priority-focus">
  <div>
    <h3>Priority Focus</h3>
    Safety • Direct Assessment • Remove Harm • Private Interview • Mandatory Reporting
  </div>
</div>

<div class="mh-cards">
  <div class="mh-card purple">
    <div class="mh-card-icon">🚨</div>
    <h3>Suicide</h3>
    <p>Ask directly, assess plan/means/lethality, prevent harm, and maintain supervision when needed.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="mh-card pink">
    <div class="mh-card-icon">🩹</div>
    <h3>Self-Injury</h3>
    <p>Assess intent, triggers, coping, injuries, and whether suicide risk is also present.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="mh-card blue">
    <div class="mh-card-icon">🛡️</div>
    <h3>Abuse</h3>
    <p>Interview privately, assess safety, document objectively, and report suspected abuse when required.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="mh-card green">
    <div class="mh-card-icon">💬</div>
    <h3>Communication</h3>
    <p>Use calm, direct, nonjudgmental, supportive communication. Never promise secrecy when safety is at risk.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="report-now">
  <h3>Report Immediately</h3>
  <ul>
    <li>Suicidal ideation with a plan, access, and intent</li>
    <li>Homicidal ideation or threats toward others</li>
    <li>Command hallucinations telling the patient to harm self or others</li>
    <li>New suicidal admission with access to harmful objects</li>
    <li>Suspected child abuse</li>
    <li>Suspected elder abuse</li>
    <li>Suspected abuse of a vulnerable or dependent adult</li>
    <li>Sexual assault disclosure with current safety concerns</li>
  </ul>
</div>

<div class="nclex-tip">
  <h3>NCLEX Tip</h3>
  Safety comes before teaching, coping skills, or long-term therapy. If there is risk for harm, the nurse acts to protect the patient first.
</div>

<div class="mh-cards">
  <div class="mh-card purple">

    <p>Thinking about killing oneself.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="mh-card pink">
    <div class="mh-card-icon">⚠️</div>
    <h3>Suicide Attempt</h3>
    <p>Self-harm with intent to die.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="mh-card blue">
    <div class="mh-card-icon">🩸</div>
    <h3>Non-Suicidal Self-Injury</h3>

  </div>

  <div class="mh-card green">
    <div class="mh-card-icon">🔥</div>

## Suicide Risk Factors and Warning Signs

<div class="key-findings">
  <h3>📋 Key Assessment Findings</h3>
  <ul>
    <li>Previous suicide attempt</li>
    <li>Family history of suicide</li>
    <li>Depression, bipolar disorder, schizophrenia, or substance use</li>
    <li>Drug or alcohol use that lowers impulse control</li>
    <li>Impulsivity</li>
    <li>Giving things away</li>
    <li>Sudden switch from sad to happy</li>
    <li>Statements like “I won’t be a bother much longer”</li>
    <li>Specific suicide plan</li>
    <li>Access to the method</li>
    <li>Poor support system</li>
  </ul>
</div>

<div class="nclex-tip">
  <h3>NCLEX Tip</h3>
  A sudden improvement in mood after severe depression can be a warning sign that the patient has made a suicide decision.
</div>

## Suicide Assessment

Ask directly. Talking about suicide does **not** give the patient the idea.

<div class="mh-table-card" markdown="1">

<h3>Questions to Ask</h3>

| Question | What It Checks |
|---|---|
| “Are you thinking about killing yourself?” | Suicidal ideation |
| “Do you have a plan?” | Specificity |
| “What is your plan?” | Lethality |
| “Do you have access to that method?” | Means |
| “When were you planning to do this?” | Immediacy |
| “Have you attempted suicide before?” | History |
| “Who can support you right now?” | Support system |
| “Do you feel safe being alone?” | Current safety |

</div>

<div class="report-now">
  <h3>Highest Risk</h3>
  <ul>
    <li>Specific plan</li>
    <li>Lethal method</li>
    <li>Access to means</li>
    <li>Intent to act</li>
  </ul>
</div>

## Lethality

<div class="mh-cards">
  <div class="mh-card green">
    <div class="mh-card-icon">⬇️</div>
    <h3>Lower Lethality</h3>
    <ul>
      <li>Superficial cutting</li>
      <li>Small pill ingestion</li>
      <li>Vague plan</li>
      <li>No access to method</li>
      <li>No set time</li>
    </ul>
  </div>

  <div class="mh-card pink">
    <div class="mh-card-icon">⬆️</div>
    <h3>Higher Lethality</h3>
    <ul>
      <li>Firearm</li>
      <li>Hanging</li>
      <li>Carbon monoxide</li>
      <li>Serious overdose with access</li>
      <li>Jumping from height</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="nclex-tip">
  <h3>NCLEX Tip</h3>
  A self-inflicted gunshot wound is considered a highly lethal method.
</div>

## Care of the Suicidal Patient

<div class="mh-cards">
  <div class="mh-card purple">
    <div class="mh-card-icon">1</div>
    <h3>Prevent Harm</h3>
    <p>Search patient and belongings. Remove harmful objects.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="mh-card pink">
    <div class="mh-card-icon">2</div>
    <h3>Supervise</h3>
    <p>Maintain 1:1 observation if indicated. Do not leave an actively suicidal patient alone.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="mh-card blue">
    <div class="mh-card-icon">3</div>
    <h3>Assess</h3>
    <p>Ask about plan, means, intent, lethality, and past attempts.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="mh-card green">
    <div class="mh-card-icon">4</div>
    <h3>Notify + Document</h3>
    <p>Notify provider/team and document exact statements, risk level, and actions taken.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="report-now">
  <h3>Environmental Safety</h3>
  <ul>
    <li>Remove belts, shoelaces, razors, scissors, tweezers, and nail files</li>
    <li>Remove matches, lighters, cords, glass, medications, and weapons</li>
    <li>Monitor perfume, shampoo, plastic bags, and other potential hazards</li>
    <li>Use and count plastic utensils</li>
    <li>Keep door open if observation is needed</li>
    <li>Use safe room precautions and reduce ligature risks</li>
    <li>Recheck safety after the patient leaves and returns to the unit</li>
  </ul>
</div>

<div class="nclex-tip">
  <h3>NCLEX Tip</h3>
  For a newly admitted suicidal patient, search belongings and remove harmful objects first.
</div>

## Therapeutic Communication for Suicide

<div class="mh-table-card" markdown="1">

<h3>Best Responses</h3>

| Patient Says | Best Response |
|---|---|
| “I don’t want to live anymore.” | “Are you thinking about killing yourself?” |
| “I have a way to end it.” | “What is your plan?” |
| “I won’t be a bother much longer.” | “Are you having thoughts of suicide?” |
| “Promise you won’t tell anyone.” | “I cannot promise secrecy if your safety is at risk.” |
| “I feel better now.” | “I still need to make sure you are safe. Are you having suicidal thoughts?” |

</div>

<div class="do-not-miss">
  <h3>Avoid Saying</h3>
  <ul>
    <li>“You have so much to live for.”</li>
    <li>“Everything will be fine.”</li>
    <li>“You would never do that.”</li>
    <li>“That is selfish.”</li>
    <li>“Why would you do that?”</li>
    <li>Promising secrecy</li>
  </ul>
</div>

## Non-Suicidal Self-Injury

Self-injury is intentional body harm without conscious intent to die, but the nurse still must assess suicide risk.

Examples: cutting, burning, scratching, picking wounds, hitting self.

<div class="mh-cards">
  <div class="mh-card purple">
    <div class="mh-card-icon">🩹</div>
    <h3>Treat Injuries</h3>
    <p>Assess wounds and prevent infection or complications.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="mh-card pink">
    <div class="mh-card-icon">❓</div>
    <h3>Assess Intent</h3>
    <p>Self-harm can overlap with suicide risk. Ask directly about suicidal thoughts.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="mh-card blue">
    <div class="mh-card-icon">🔍</div>
    <h3>Identify Triggers</h3>
    <p>Help the patient connect feelings, triggers, and behavior.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="mh-card green">
    <div class="mh-card-icon">🌱</div>
    <h3>Teach Safer Coping</h3>
    <p>Use empathy, reduce shame, and teach safer alternatives.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="key-findings">
  <h3>📋 Coping Options</h3>
  <ul>
    <li>Deep breathing</li>
    <li>Grounding</li>
    <li>Journaling</li>
    <li>Calling a support person</li>
    <li>Walking</li>
    <li>Music or art</li>
    <li>Holding ice instead of cutting</li>
    <li>Crisis plan</li>
  </ul>
</div>

## Abuse Assessment Clues

<div class="key-findings">
  <h3>📋 Possible Abuse Findings</h3>
  <ul>
    <li>Injury explanation does not match injury</li>
    <li>Delay in seeking care</li>
    <li>Bruises in different stages of healing</li>
    <li>Frequent injuries or ED visits</li>
    <li>Patient fears caregiver or partner</li>
    <li>Visitor answers for patient</li>
    <li>Poor hygiene, malnutrition, or dehydration</li>
    <li>Missing medications or appointments</li>
    <li>Anxiety, depression, PTSD, or suicidal thoughts</li>
  </ul>

## Abuse Interview

If abuse is suspected, make every effort to interview the patient **alone**.

<div class="mh-cards">
  <div class="mh-card green">
    <div class="mh-card-icon">✅</div>
    <h3>Do</h3>
    <ul>
      <li>Use a private room</li>
      <li>Ask open-ended questions</li>
      <li>Use attentive listening</li>
      <li>Assess safety</li>
      <li>Ask about weapons</li>
      <li>Assess anxiety and suicide risk</li>
      <li>Provide resources</li>
      <li>Document carefully</li>
    </ul>
  </div>

  <div class="mh-card pink">
    <div class="mh-card-icon">🚫</div>
    <h3>Avoid</h3>
    <ul>
      <li>Interviewing in front of suspected abuser</li>
      <li>Blaming the victim</li>
      <li>Pressuring for details</li>
      <li>Confronting the suspected abuser</li>
      <li>Promising secrecy</li>
      <li>Telling the patient they “must leave”</li>
      <li>Using family as interpreter</li>
      <li>Ignoring inconsistent injury stories</li>
    </ul>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="nclex-tip">
  <h3>NCLEX Tip</h3>
  Do not force the victim to discuss details they are not ready to share.
</div>

<div class="mh-table-card" markdown="1">

<h3>Who Must Be Reported?</h3>

| Group | Report? |
|---|---|
| Children | Yes |
| Older adults | Yes |
| Vulnerable/dependent adults | Yes |
| Disabled adults unable to protect self | Yes |
| Adult intimate partner violence | Follow state/facility policy; still assess safety and provide resources |

</div>

<div class="report-now">
  <h3>Mandatory Reporting Priority</h3>
  <ul>
    <li>Suspected child abuse must be reported.</li>
    <li>Suspected elder abuse must be reported.</li>
    <li>Suspected abuse of a vulnerable or dependent adult must be reported.</li>

  </ul>

## Documentation

<div class="mh-table-card" markdown="1">

<h3>Objective Documentation</h3>

| Avoid | Better |
|---|---|
| “Patient was beaten.” | “Patient states, ‘My boyfriend punched me.’” |
| “Mother is abusive.” | “Bruising noted to bilateral upper arms.” |
| “Patient is lying.” | “Explanation is inconsistent with injury pattern.” |
| “Patient is dramatic.” | “Patient tearful and shaking during interview.” |

</div>

<div class="key-findings">
  <h3>📋 Include in Documentation</h3>
  <ul>
    <li>Exact patient quotes</li>
    <li>Injury location, size, color, and shape</li>
    <li>Patient behavior</li>
    <li>Who was present</li>
    <li>Safety concerns</li>
    <li>Who was notified</li>
    <li>Resources provided</li>
  </ul>
</div>

## Child Abuse

Children may not clearly report abuse, so the nurse watches for clues.

<div class="key-findings">
  <h3>📋 Child Abuse Clues</h3>
  <ul>
    <li>Bruises on back, buttocks, ears, neck, or upper arms</li>
    <li>Injuries in different healing stages</li>
    <li>Patterned burns</li>
    <li>Fractures inconsistent with history</li>
    <li>Poor hygiene or malnutrition</li>
    <li>Missed medical care or immunizations</li>
    <li>Sexualized behavior beyond age</li>
    <li>Fear of caregiver</li>
  </ul>
</div>

<div class="quick-review">
  <h3>Priority</h3>
  <ol>
    <li>Ensure safety</li>
    <li>Assess injuries</li>
    <li>Report suspected abuse</li>
    <li>Document objectively</li>
    <li>Do not confront caregiver</li>
  </ol>
</div>

<div class="nclex-tip">
  <h3>Helpful Response to Child</h3>
  “I’m glad you told me. You are not in trouble.”
</div>

## Elder Abuse

Elder abuse may include physical, emotional, sexual, financial abuse, neglect, or abandonment.

<div class="key-findings">
  <h3>📋 Elder Abuse Clues</h3>
  <ul>
    <li>Unexplained weight loss</li>
    <li>Bruising or fractures</li>
    <li>Pressure injuries</li>
    <li>Poor hygiene</li>
    <li>Missed appointments</li>
    <li>Missing medications</li>
    <li>Fear of caregiver</li>
    <li>Caregiver refuses privacy</li>
    <li>Pension checks taken</li>
    <li>Forced to sign property over</li>
  </ul>
</div>

<div class="nclex-tip">
  <h3>NCLEX Tip</h3>
  For an older client with unexplained weight loss and bruising, first collect physical data and report findings to the charge nurse/provider.
</div>

## Domestic Violence

Domestic violence is abuse by a current or former partner. It may include physical violence, emotional abuse, sexual coercion, financial control, isolation, threats, and control of phone, money, transportation, children, or pets.

<div class="mh-table-card" markdown="1">

<h3>Cycle of Abuse</h3>

| Phase | What Happens |
|---|---|
| **Tension-building** | Stress increases; victim feels like they are “walking on eggshells” |
| **Acute abuse/explosion** | Abuse occurs |
| **Honeymoon** | Abuser apologizes, gives gifts, promises change |
| **Calm** | Temporary peace before cycle repeats |

</div>

<div class="key-findings">
  <h3>📋 Questions to Ask When Alone</h3>
  <ul>
    <li>“Do you feel safe at home?”</li>
    <li>“Has anyone hit, kicked, choked, or hurt you?”</li>
    <li>“Has anyone threatened you?”</li>
    <li>“Are there weapons in the home?”</li>
    <li>“Are you afraid of your partner?”</li>
    <li>“Does anyone control your money, phone, or transportation?”</li>
    <li>“Do you have a safe place to go if needed?”</li>
  </ul>
</div>

<div class="mh-cards">
  <div class="mh-card purple">
    <div class="mh-card-icon">📄</div>
    <h3>Important Papers</h3>
    <p>ID, insurance cards, birth certificates, and needed documents.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="mh-card pink">
    <div class="mh-card-icon">💵</div>
    <h3>Money + Keys</h3>
    <p>Cash/card hidden safely and duplicate car/house keys if possible.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="mh-card blue">
    <div class="mh-card-icon">📞</div>
    <h3>Contacts + Safe Place</h3>
    <p>Trusted person, shelter, hotline, friend, family, or safe location.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="mh-card green">
    <div class="mh-card-icon">🎒</div>
    <h3>Emergency Bag</h3>
    <p>Clothes, medications, papers, and plans for children, pets, and weapons in the home.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="nclex-tip">
  <h3>NCLEX Tip</h3>
  Do not simply tell the victim to leave. Leaving can be dangerous. Assess safety, provide resources, and help make a safety plan.
</div>

## Sexual Assault / Rape Trauma Syndrome

Rape trauma syndrome is a PTSD-like response after rape or sexual assault.

<div class="mh-cards">
  <div class="mh-card purple">
    <div class="mh-card-icon">🛡️</div>
    <h3>Safety</h3>
    <p>Make sure the patient is safe from the attacker.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="mh-card pink">
    <div class="mh-card-icon">🩺</div>
    <h3>Medical Stability</h3>
    <p>Treat injuries and urgent needs.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="mh-card blue">
    <div class="mh-card-icon">💬</div>
    <h3>Emotional Support</h3>
    <p>Be empathetic, nonjudgmental, and supportive.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="mh-card green">
    <div class="mh-card-icon">🤍</div>
    <h3>Patient Control</h3>
    <p>Explain care, ask permission, preserve privacy, and involve SANE/advocate resources per policy.</p>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="key-findings">
  <h3>📋 Helpful Responses</h3>
  <ul>
    <li>“I believe you.”</li>
    <li>“This was not your fault.”</li>
    <li>“You are safe here.”</li>
    <li>“You are in control of what happens next.”</li>
  </ul>
</div>

<div class="do-not-miss">
  <h3>Avoid Saying</h3>
  <ul>
    <li>“Why were you there?”</li>
    <li>“Why didn’t you fight back?”</li>
    <li>“Are you sure?”</li>
    <li>“You should have called sooner.”</li>
  </ul>
</div>

## Final Takeaway

<div class="quick-review" id="quick-review">
  <h3>Quick Review</h3>
  <ol>
    <li>Ask: is the patient safe right now?</li>
    <li>Ask directly about suicide when suicide risk is suspected.</li>
    <li>Assess plan, access, means, lethality, and intent.</li>
    <li>Remove harmful objects and maintain supervision when needed.</li>
    <li>Interview suspected abuse victims alone when safe.</li>
    <li>Report suspected child, elder, or vulnerable adult abuse.</li>
    <li>Document exact quotes and objective findings.</li>
  </ol>
</div>

<div class="do-not-miss">
  <h3>Do Not Miss</h3>
  <ul>
    <li>Talking about suicide does not give the patient the idea.</li>
    <li>Never promise secrecy when safety is at risk.</li>
    <li>Plan + means + access + intent is highest suicide risk.</li>
    <li>Search belongings first for a newly admitted suicidal patient.</li>
    <li>Interview abuse victims privately when possible.</li>
    <li>Do not confront the suspected abuser.</li>
    <li>Suspicion of child or elder abuse is enough to report.</li>
    <li>Document objectively using exact patient quotes.</li>
  </ul>
</div>

<div class="cheer-banner">
  💜 Safety questions are priority questions. You’ve got this — assess, protect, report, document. 💜
</div>
