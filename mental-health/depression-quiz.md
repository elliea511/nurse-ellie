## Question 1
A nurse is admitting a client who has major depressive disorder. The client states, “My family would be better off without me.” Which of the following actions should the nurse take **first**?

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> Place the client on one-to-one observation</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> Ask the client whether they have a specific suicide plan</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> Remove all sharp objects from the client’s room</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> Notify the provider of the client’s statement</p>

<details class="quiz-answer-block">
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: B</strong></p>

<div class="quiz-rationale">
<p><strong>Rationale:</strong></p>

<p><strong>A. Incorrect:</strong> One-to-one observation may be necessary, but the nurse should first assess the immediacy and severity of the suicide risk unless the client is actively attempting self-harm.</p>
<p><strong>B. Correct:</strong> The nurse should directly assess whether the client has suicidal intent, a specific plan, access to the method, and a timeframe. Asking directly does not increase suicide risk.</p>
<p><strong>C. Incorrect:</strong> Removing hazards is appropriate after determining the level of risk, but this action does not replace a focused suicide assessment.</p>
<p><strong>D. Incorrect:</strong> The provider should be notified, but the nurse must first gather immediate safety information.</p>

</div>
</details>

## Question 2
A nurse is caring for a client who has severe depression and has remained in bed for most of the day. The client has eaten only a few bites at each meal. Which of the following interventions is the nurse’s **priority**?

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> Encourage the client to attend a coping-skills group</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> Ask the client to identify three personal strengths</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> Discuss the long-term benefits of antidepressant therapy</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> Offer high-calorie, high-protein finger foods and fluids frequently</p>

<details class="quiz-answer-block">
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: D</strong></p>

<div class="quiz-rationale">
<p><strong>Rationale:</strong></p>

<p><strong>A. Incorrect:</strong> Group participation can promote socialization, but the client’s immediate physiological needs take priority.</p>
<p><strong>B. Incorrect:</strong> Identifying strengths may improve self-esteem later, but it does not address the current risk for inadequate nutrition.</p>
<p><strong>C. Incorrect:</strong> Medication teaching is important, but the client may have limited concentration and is currently at risk for physical deterioration.</p>
<p><strong>D. Correct:</strong> Clients with severe depression can lack the energy and motivation to eat. Small, nutrient-dense foods require less effort and help prevent dehydration and malnutrition.</p>

</div>

## Question 3
A client who has major depressive disorder tells the nurse, “Nothing is ever going to get better. There is no reason for me to keep trying.” Which of the following responses should the nurse make?

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> “You sound as though you feel hopeless right now.”</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> “You should focus on the positive things in your life.”</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> “Why do you believe that nothing will improve?”</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> “Once your medication starts working, you will feel much better.”</p>

<details class="quiz-answer-block">
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: A</strong></p>

<div class="quiz-rationale">
<p><strong>Rationale:</strong></p>

<p><strong>A. Correct:</strong> This response reflects the client’s feelings and encourages further communication without offering false reassurance.</p>
<p><strong>B. Incorrect:</strong> Telling the client to focus on positive things minimizes their feelings and gives advice.</p>
<p><strong>C. Incorrect:</strong> “Why” questions can sound judgmental and may cause the client to become defensive.</p>
<p><strong>D. Incorrect:</strong> This is false reassurance. The nurse cannot guarantee the client’s response to treatment.</p>

</div>
</details>

## Question 4 — Select All That Apply
A nurse is assessing a client who recently began taking an SSRI for major depressive disorder. Which of the following findings should the nurse identify as possible manifestations of **serotonin syndrome**?

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> Bradycardia</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> Diaphoresis</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> Hyperreflexia</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> Agitation</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>E.</strong> Constipation</p>

<details class="quiz-answer-block">
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: B, C, D</strong></p>

<div class="quiz-rationale">
<p><strong>Rationale:</strong></p>

<p><strong>A. Incorrect:</strong> Serotonin syndrome more commonly causes tachycardia and autonomic instability rather than bradycardia.</p>
<p><strong>B. Correct:</strong> Diaphoresis is a common sign of autonomic hyperactivity.</p>
<p><strong>C. Correct:</strong> Hyperreflexia, tremors, and clonus are characteristic neuromuscular findings.</p>
<p><strong>D. Correct:</strong> Agitation, confusion, and restlessness can occur due to central nervous system overstimulation.</p>
<p><strong>E. Incorrect:</strong> Diarrhea is more consistent with serotonin syndrome. Constipation is more commonly associated with anticholinergic effects.</p>

</div>

## Question 5 — Select All That Apply
A nurse is developing a safety plan for a client who has major depressive disorder and suicidal ideation. Which of the following interventions should the nurse include?

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> Ask the client to sign a contract promising not to attempt suicide</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> Search the client’s belongings for hazardous objects according to facility policy</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> Place the client in a private room at the end of the hallway</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> Maintain close observation based on the client’s assessed level of risk</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>E.</strong> Ensure that staff members know the client’s suicide precautions</p>

<details class="quiz-answer-block">
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: B, D, E</strong></p>

<div class="quiz-rationale">
<p><strong>Rationale:</strong></p>

<p><strong>A. Incorrect:</strong> A no-suicide contract does not reliably prevent self-harm and should not replace assessment, observation, and environmental safety measures.</p>
<p><strong>B. Correct:</strong> Removing access to medications, weapons, cords, sharp objects, and other hazards reduces the opportunity for self-harm.</p>
<p><strong>C. Incorrect:</strong> A secluded room decreases visibility. A high-risk client should be placed where staff can observe them closely.</p>
<p><strong>D. Correct:</strong> The level of observation should match the client’s risk, including continuous one-to-one observation when indicated.</p>
<p><strong>E. Correct:</strong> Consistent communication among staff is necessary to maintain suicide precautions across shifts and settings.</p>

</div>
</details>

## Question 6
A nurse is caring for a client who has major depressive disorder and started taking fluoxetine 10 days ago. The client is now getting dressed, attending meals, and interacting more with others but continues to state, “I still wish I were dead.” Which action should the nurse take **first**?

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> Reinforce the importance of taking the medication daily</p>

<details class="quiz-answer-block">
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: C</strong></p>

<div class="quiz-rationale">
<p><strong>Rationale:</strong></p>

<p><strong>A. Incorrect:</strong> Group therapy may be beneficial, but it does not address the client’s immediate safety risk.</p>
<p><strong>B. Incorrect:</strong> This information is accurate, but teaching is not the priority while the client continues to express suicidal ideation.</p>
<p><strong>C. Correct:</strong> Antidepressants can improve energy and motivation before hopelessness and suicidal thoughts resolve. The client may now have enough energy to carry out a suicide plan, so observation should be increased immediately.</p>
<p><strong>D. Incorrect:</strong> Medication adherence is important, but suicide precautions take priority.</p>

## Question 7
A nurse is preparing a client who has severe major depressive disorder for electroconvulsive therapy. Which action should the nurse take?

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> Verify that the client has remained NPO for the prescribed period</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> Administer the client’s scheduled anticonvulsant before the procedure</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> Explain that the client will remain awake during the treatment</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> Encourage the client to drink fluids immediately before transport</p>

<details class="quiz-answer-block">
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: A</strong></p>

<div class="quiz-rationale">
<p><strong>Rationale:</strong></p>

<p><strong>A. Correct:</strong> The client should remain NPO, commonly for 6 to 8 hours, to reduce the risk of aspiration during anesthesia.</p>
<p><strong>B. Incorrect:</strong> Anticonvulsants can increase the seizure threshold and reduce the effectiveness of ECT. They are usually withheld as prescribed.</p>
<p><strong>C. Incorrect:</strong> ECT is performed under general anesthesia with a short-acting muscle relaxant.</p>
<p><strong>D. Incorrect:</strong> Oral fluids before anesthesia increase aspiration risk.</p>

</div>
</details>

## Question 8 — Select All That Apply
A nurse is caring for a client immediately following electroconvulsive therapy. Which actions should the nurse take?

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> Position the client on their side until protective reflexes return</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> Reorient the client frequently to time and place</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> Encourage the client to ambulate independently as soon as they awaken</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> Monitor the client’s respiratory status and oxygen saturation</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>E.</strong> Explain that temporary memory impairment can occur</p>

<details class="quiz-answer-block">
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: A, B, D, E</strong></p>

<div class="quiz-rationale">
<p><strong>Rationale:</strong></p>

<p><strong>A. Correct:</strong> Side-lying positioning helps maintain a patent airway and reduces aspiration risk while the client is recovering from anesthesia.</p>
<p><strong>B. Correct:</strong> Temporary confusion and disorientation are common after ECT, so calm, repeated reorientation is appropriate.</p>
<p><strong>C. Incorrect:</strong> The client may have confusion, dizziness, or residual anesthesia effects and should not ambulate independently.</p>
<p><strong>D. Correct:</strong> Airway, breathing, and oxygenation are the immediate postprocedure priorities.</p>
<p><strong>E. Correct:</strong> Short-term memory loss and temporary confusion are expected adverse effects and should be explained to the client.</p>

</div>

## Question 9

## Question 10 — Select All That Apply
A nurse is assessing a client who has major depressive disorder and recently started taking a tricyclic antidepressant. Which findings should the nurse identify as adverse effects of this medication class?

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> Orthostatic hypotension</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> Urinary retention</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> Excessive salivation</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> Blurred vision</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>E.</strong> Cardiac dysrhythmias</p>

<details class="quiz-answer-block">
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: A, B, D, E</strong></p>

<div class="quiz-rationale">
<p><strong>Rationale:</strong></p>

<p><strong>A. Correct:</strong> Tricyclic antidepressants can cause alpha-adrenergic blockade, resulting in orthostatic hypotension and fall risk.</p>
<p><strong>B. Correct:</strong> Anticholinergic effects can cause urinary retention.</p>
<p><strong>C. Incorrect:</strong> TCAs more commonly cause dry mouth rather than excessive salivation.</p>
<p><strong>D. Correct:</strong> Blurred vision can occur because of anticholinergic effects.</p>
<p><strong>E. Correct:</strong> TCAs can cause cardiac conduction abnormalities and are highly dangerous in overdose.</p>

</div>
</details>

## Question 11
A nurse is caring for a client who has major depressive disorder and has been taking phenelzine for 3 weeks. Which meal selection should the nurse identify as requiring intervention?

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> Grilled chicken, green beans, and rice</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> Turkey sandwich with fresh fruit</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> Aged cheddar cheese, salami, and red wine</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> Baked fish, steamed broccoli, and potatoes</p>

<details class="quiz-answer-block">
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: C</strong></p>

<div class="quiz-rationale">
<p><strong>Rationale:</strong></p>

<p><strong>A. Incorrect:</strong> Fresh meats, vegetables, and rice are generally safe with an MAOI.</p>

## Question 12
A nurse is assessing a client who has major depressive disorder and takes phenelzine. The client reports a severe occipital headache, palpitations, and neck stiffness after eating at a restaurant. Which action should the nurse take **first**?

<div class="quiz-rationale">

## Question 13 — Select All That Apply
A nurse is planning discharge teaching for a client who has major depressive disorder and is starting bupropion. Which instructions should the nurse include?

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> Report any seizure activity immediately</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> Avoid abruptly stopping the medication</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>C.</strong> Expect significant weight gain during therapy</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>D.</strong> Notify the provider if agitation or suicidal thoughts increase</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>E.</strong> Avoid using this medication if there is a history of an eating disorder</p>

<details class="quiz-answer-block">
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: A, B, D, E</strong></p>

<div class="quiz-rationale">
<p><strong>Rationale:</strong></p>

<p><strong>A. Correct:</strong> Bupropion lowers the seizure threshold, so seizure activity requires immediate medical attention.</p>
<p><strong>B. Correct:</strong> Abrupt discontinuation can cause withdrawal symptoms or recurrence of depression.</p>
<p><strong>C. Incorrect:</strong> Bupropion is less likely than many antidepressants to cause weight gain and may cause decreased appetite.</p>
<p><strong>D. Correct:</strong> Increased agitation, behavioral changes, or suicidal ideation should be reported immediately, especially early in treatment.</p>
<p><strong>E. Correct:</strong> Bupropion is generally avoided in clients with anorexia nervosa or bulimia nervosa because electrolyte disturbances increase seizure risk.</p>

</div>
</details>

<p><strong>D. Correct:</strong> Bupropion lowers the seizure threshold and is generally contraindicated in clients with anorexia nervosa or bulimia nervosa, particularly when purging causes electrolyte imbalance.</p>

## Question 40 — Select All That Apply
A nurse is teaching a client who is starting venlafaxine. Which instructions should the nurse include?

<p class="answer-choice"><strong>A.</strong> Monitor blood pressure regularly</p>
<p class="answer-choice"><strong>B.</strong> Double the next dose after missing a dose</p>

<details class="quiz-answer-block">
<summary>Click to see answer and rationale</summary>
<p class="quiz-answer"><strong>Answer: A, C, D</strong></p>

<div class="quiz-rationale">
<p><strong>Rationale:</strong></p>

<p><strong>A. Correct:</strong> Venlafaxine can increase blood pressure, particularly at higher doses.</p>
<p><strong>B. Incorrect:</strong> The client should not double doses because doing so increases adverse-effect risk.</p>
<p><strong>C. Correct:</strong> New or worsening suicidal ideation requires immediate evaluation, especially early in treatment.</p>
<p><strong>D. Correct:</strong> Abrupt discontinuation can cause dizziness, nausea, irritability, sensory disturbances, and other withdrawal symptoms.</p>
<p><strong>E. Incorrect:</strong> Antidepressant effects generally take several weeks rather than several hours.</p>
