# Erythromycin Eye Ointment

## Medication

|                 |                                                  |
| --------------- | ------------------------------------------------ |
| Medication Name | Erythromycin Eye Ointment                        |
| Category/Class  | Macrolide antibiotic / ophthalmic anti-infective |

## Purpose / Expected Action

* Prevents newborn eye infection after birth.
* Protects against ophthalmia neonatorum.
* Helps prevent infection that can occur from exposure to bacteria during vaginal birth.

## Therapeutic Use

* Routine newborn eye prophylaxis.
* Prevention of neonatal conjunctivitis.

## Route / Site

* Ophthalmic ointment
* Applied to both eyes

## Given When

* Given shortly after birth per facility policy.

## Complications / Side Effects

* Temporary blurred vision
* Mild eye irritation
* Redness
* Swelling
* Rare allergic reaction

## Contraindications / Precautions

Use caution with:

* Allergy to erythromycin or macrolide antibiotics

## Nursing Interventions

* Apply ointment to both eyes.
* Apply from inner canthus to outer canthus.
* Avoid touching the tube tip to the eye.
* Do not rinse ointment out after administration.
* Document time given.
* Monitor for irritation or allergic reaction.
* Encourage bonding before administration if facility policy allows because ointment can temporarily blur vision.

## Client Education

* This medication helps prevent serious newborn eye infection.
* It may make the baby’s eyes look shiny or blurry for a short time.
* It is not used because the baby already has an infection; it is preventative.

## Evaluation of Medication Effectiveness

Medication is effective if:

* Ointment is administered correctly.
* Newborn shows no signs of eye infection.
* No severe reaction occurs.

## NCLEX / ATI Must Know

Erythromycin eye ointment is given to prevent **ophthalmia neonatorum**.

It is a routine newborn medication, along with **Vitamin K** and **Hepatitis B vaccine**.

## Memory Hook

**Erythromycin eyes = Eye infection prevention.**
