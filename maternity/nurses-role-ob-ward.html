--- # Nurse's Role When a Patient Presents to the OB Ward to Give Birth ← Back to Maternity ## Overview When a patient presents to the OB ward to give birth, the nurse’s role is to quickly determine if the mother and fetus are stable, identify whether the patient is in true labor, monitor for complications, support the labor process, assist with birth, provide immediate newborn care, and continue postpartum assessment and teaching. The nurse is constantly asking: * Is the mother stable? * Is the fetus oxygenating well? * Is this true labor? * Are there signs of an emergency? * Is bleeding normal or excessive? * Is the newborn breathing, warm, and transitioning well? --- # 1. Initial OB Triage ## First Nursing Priorities When the patient arrives: 1. Verify identity. 2. Ask why she came in. 3. Assess gestational age. 4. Assess maternal vital signs. 5. Assess fetal movement. 6. Apply fetal monitor. 7. Check for bleeding, rupture of membranes, pain, and contractions. ## Important Questions to Ask * How many weeks pregnant are you? * When did contractions start? * How far apart are they? * Did your water break? * What time did it break? * What color was the fluid? * Any vaginal bleeding? * Is the baby moving? * Any headache, blurry vision, swelling, or epigastric pain? * Are you GBS positive? * What is your blood type/Rh status? * Any previous C-section? * Any pregnancy complications? --- # 2. Review Pregnancy History ## Assess Antepartum Information Collect: * Gravida and para * GTPAL * Estimated due date * Gestational age * Prenatal care history * Blood type and Rh status * Rubella status * GBS status * STI screening * Glucose screening * Hypertension or preeclampsia history * Gestational diabetes history * Placenta previa or abruption history * Previous C-section * Multiple gestation * Decreased fetal movement ## GTPAL | Letter | Meaning | | ------ | -------------------------- | | G | Gravida: total pregnancies | | T | Term births | | P | Preterm births | | A | Abortions or miscarriages | | L | Living children | --- # 3. Maternal Assessment ## Vital Signs Assess: * Blood pressure * Pulse * Respirations * Temperature * Oxygen saturation * Pain level ## Preeclampsia Red Flags Report: * BP 140/90 or higher * Severe headache * Visual changes * Epigastric or RUQ pain * Facial or hand swelling * Hyperreflexia * Clonus * Proteinuria ## If Preeclampsia Is Suspected Nursing actions: 1. Place patient on side. 2. Initiate seizure precautions. 3. Notify provider. 4. Prepare for labs. 5. Anticipate magnesium sulfate. 6. Monitor respirations, reflexes, urine output, and LOC. --- # 4. Fetal Assessment ## Apply Fetal Monitor Apply: * Ultrasound transducer for fetal heart rate * Tocotransducer for contractions ## Normal FHR ```text 110-160 bpm ``` ## Assess FHR For * Baseline * Variability * Accelerations * Decelerations * Contraction pattern --- # 5. Fetal Heart Rate Interpretation ## Variability | Finding | Meaning | | -------- | ------------------------------- | | Absent | Concerning | | Minimal | May be concerning if persistent | | Moderate | Reassuring | | Marked | May need continued assessment | ## Decelerations | Deceleration | Cause | Nursing Action | | ------------ | ---------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------- | | Early | Head compression | Usually benign | | Variable | Cord compression | Reposition patient | | Late | Uteroplacental insufficiency | Reposition, stop Pitocin, fluids, notify provider | | Prolonged | Possible emergency | Immediate intervention | ## Nonreassuring FHR Interventions 1. Reposition patient, usually left side. 2. Stop oxytocin if infusing. 3. Increase IV fluids. 4. Assess maternal vital signs. 5. Give oxygen if ordered or per protocol. 6. Notify provider. 7. Prepare for possible emergency birth. --- # 6. Determine True Labor ## True Labor | Finding | True Labor | | ------------ | ----------------------------------------- | | Contractions | Regular | | Frequency | Get closer together | | Intensity | Get stronger | | Pain | Often starts in back and moves to abdomen | | Cervix | Dilates and effaces | | Activity | Walking increases contractions | ## False Labor | Finding | False Labor | | ------------ | ------------------------------------------- | | Contractions | Irregular | | Frequency | Do not get closer together | | Intensity | Do not get stronger | | Cervix | No major change | | Activity | Rest or hydration may decrease contractions | --- # 7. Assess Membranes ## Ask * Did your water break? * What time? * Was it a gush or trickle? * What color was the fluid? * Any odor? ## Amniotic Fluid Findings | Finding | Meaning | | ----------- | ---------------------------- | | Clear/pale | Expected | | Green/brown | Meconium | | Foul odor | Infection | | Bloody | Possible abruption or trauma | | Yellow | Possible fetal hemolysis | ## Nitrazine Test * Detects alkaline amniotic fluid. * Blue result suggests rupture of membranes. * False positives can occur with blood, semen, or infection. ## Fern Test * Amniotic fluid creates a fern-like pattern under the microscope. * Helps confirm rupture of membranes. --- # 8. Vaginal Exam ## Assess * Dilation * Effacement * Station * Presentation * Position * Membranes * Possible cord prolapse ## Dilation | Dilation | Labor Phase | | -------- | ----------- | | 0-3 cm | Latent | | 4-7 cm | Active | | 8-10 cm | Transition | | 10 cm | Complete | ## Station | Station | Meaning | | ---------------- | ---------------------------- | | Negative station | Baby is above ischial spines | | 0 station | Baby is engaged | | Positive station | Baby is descending | | Crowning | Birth is near | --- # 9. OB Emergencies ## Prolapsed Cord Signs: * Sudden fetal bradycardia * Recurrent variable decelerations * Cord visible or felt during vaginal exam Nursing actions: 1. Call for help. 2. Stay with patient. 3. Insert gloved hand and lift presenting part off cord. 4. Keep hand in place until delivery. 5. Place patient in knee-chest or Trendelenburg. 6. Stop Pitocin. 7. Prepare for emergency C-section. ## Placenta Previa Classic sign: ```text Painless bright red bleeding ``` Nursing priority: * Do not perform vaginal exam. ## Placental Abruption Classic sign: ```text Painful bleeding with rigid, tender uterus ``` Other signs: * Dark red bleeding * Abdominal pain * Uterine tenderness * Fetal distress * Maternal shock ## Uterine Rupture Risk factor: * Previous uterine surgery or C-section Signs: * Sudden severe abdominal pain * Abnormal FHR * Loss of fetal station * Vaginal bleeding * Maternal hypotension * Cessation of contractions --- # 10. Admission Tasks ## Common Nursing Actions * Start IV access. * Draw labs. * Review prenatal record. * Verify GBS status. * Verify blood type and Rh. * Obtain urine specimen if ordered. * Assess pain and coping. * Discuss birth plan. * Notify provider. * Continue fetal monitoring. ## Common Labs * CBC * Type and screen * Blood type/Rh * Urinalysis * Protein/creatinine ratio if preeclampsia suspected * CMP if hypertensive * Coagulation labs if bleeding --- # 11. GBS and Rh Status ## GBS Positive Nursing role: * Start IV antibiotics as ordered. * Monitor for allergic reaction. * Document timing. * Teach that antibiotics reduce newborn infection risk. ## Rh Negative Mother Check: * Did she receive Rhogam at 28 weeks? * What is newborn’s blood type? * Does she need Rhogam postpartum? Postpartum Rhogam is given if: * Mother is Rh negative. * Baby is Rh positive. * It is given within 72 hours after birth. * It is given to the mother, not the baby. --- # 12. First Stage of Labor The first stage begins with contractions and ends at 10 cm dilation. ## Phases | Phase | Dilation | Nursing Focus | | ---------- | -------- | ------------------------------------ | | Latent | 0-3 cm | Comfort, hydration, education | | Active | 4-7 cm | Pain control, assessment, support | | Transition | 8-10 cm | Stay with patient, prepare for birth | ## Nursing Role * Monitor FHR and contractions. * Assess maternal vital signs. * Encourage position changes. * Promote rest and hydration. * Encourage voiding. * Provide emotional support. * Offer pain management. * Watch for complications. --- # 13. Pain Management ## Nonpharmacologic Measures * Position changes * Breathing techniques * Massage * Counterpressure * Hydrotherapy if allowed * Heat or cold * Music * Dim lights * Support person * Birthing ball ## IV Opioids Nursing considerations: * Assess FHR first. * Assess maternal respirations and sedation. * Avoid giving too close to birth. * Monitor newborn for respiratory depression. ## Epidural Before epidural: * Obtain baseline vitals. * Assess FHR. * Start or increase IV fluids as ordered. * Help position patient. After epidural: * Monitor BP frequently. * Watch for hypotension. * Reposition patient. * Assess bladder. * Maintain fall precautions. * Continue FHR monitoring. If hypotension occurs: 1. Turn patient to side. 2. Increase IV fluids. 3. Notify anesthesia/provider. 4. Give oxygen if needed. 5. Administer vasopressor if ordered. --- # 14. Induction and Augmentation ## Oxytocin / Pitocin Used to start or strengthen contractions. Nursing responsibilities: * Use IV pump. * Monitor FHR continuously. * Monitor contractions. * Titrate per protocol. * Stop infusion for tachysystole or nonreassuring FHR. ## Tachysystole Usually means: ```text More than 5 contractions in 10 minutes averaged over 30 minutes ``` Nursing actions: 1. Stop Pitocin. 2. Reposition patient. 3. Increase IV fluids. 4. Notify provider. 5. Give oxygen if needed. 6. Administer tocolytic if ordered. --- # 15. Second Stage of Labor The second stage begins at 10 cm and ends with birth of the baby. ## Signs * Complete dilation * Urge to push * Rectal pressure * Bulging perineum * Crowning * Increased bloody show ## Nursing Role * Confirm complete dilation. * Support pushing. * Monitor FHR. * Encourage rest between contractions. * Assess maternal fatigue. * Prepare delivery supplies. * Prepare newborn warmer. * Notify provider. ## Do Not Encourage Pushing If * Cervix is not fully dilated. * There is an anterior lip. * Provider has not approved. * FHR needs urgent intervention. --- # 16. Birth of the Baby ## Nurse’s Role * Assist provider. * Support patient. * Maintain sterile field as appropriate. * Note time of birth. * Assess newborn immediately. * Dry and stimulate newborn. * Promote skin-to-skin if stable. * Maintain newborn temperature. * Assess maternal bleeding. * Prepare for placenta delivery. --- # 17. Immediate Newborn Care ## First Priorities 1. Airway 2. Breathing 3. Circulation 4. Temperature 5. Identification 6. Safety ## Immediate Actions * Dry newborn. * Stimulate newborn. * Keep warm. * Position airway. * Suction only if needed. * Assess respirations, tone, and color. * APGAR at 1 and 5 minutes. * Apply ID bands and security tag. * Promote skin-to-skin if stable. * Encourage feeding within first hour if desired. --- # 18. APGAR Scoring | Category | 0 | 1 | 2 | | ------------ | ----------- | --------------------------- | ---------------- | | Appearance | Blue/pale | Body pink, extremities blue | Completely pink | | Pulse | Absent | Less than 100 | Greater than 100 | | Grimace | No response | Grimace | Cry/cough/sneeze | | Activity | Limp | Some flexion | Active motion | | Respirations | Absent | Slow/irregular | Strong cry | ## Interpretation | Score | Meaning | | ----- | ---------------- | | 7-10 | Usually normal | | 4-6 | Needs assistance | | 0-3 | Severe distress | --- # 19. Newborn Thermoregulation ## Heat Loss Types | Type | Example | | ----------- | -------------------------- | | Evaporation | Wet newborn after birth | | Conduction | Cold scale or blanket | | Convection | Draft or fan | | Radiation | Cold window or wall nearby | ## Nursing Actions * Dry immediately. * Remove wet linens. * Place hat on baby. * Use warm blankets. * Use radiant warmer if needed. * Promote skin-to-skin. * Delay bath until temperature is stable. --- # 20. Newborn Medications ## Vitamin K Purpose: * Prevents bleeding. Route: * IM injection. ## Erythromycin Purpose: * Prevents newborn eye infection. Route: * Eye ointment. ## Hepatitis B Vaccine Purpose: * Begins protection against hepatitis B. Nursing role: * Verify consent per policy. * Administer as ordered. * Document. Reminder: IM injection's for Hepatitis B and Vitamin K can be given at same time but not in same location (only 0.5 mL in each vastus lateralis muscle)  --- # 21. Third Stage of Labor The third stage begins after birth of the baby and ends with delivery of the placenta. ## Nursing Role * Monitor maternal bleeding. * Monitor uterine tone. * Watch for placental separation. * Administer oxytocin as ordered. * Support bonding if stable. ## Signs of Placental Separation * Gush of blood * Lengthening cord * Uterus becomes globular * Fundus rises --- # 22. Fourth Stage of Labor The fourth stage is the immediate recovery period after birth, usually the first 1-2 hours. ## Priority Assessments * Fundus * Lochia * Vital signs * Bladder * Perineum * Pain * Bonding * Newborn transition --- # 23. Postpartum Assessment ## Fundus Expected: * Firm * Midline * At or near umbilicus Abnormal: * Boggy * High * Deviated right * Difficult to locate ## Lochia Assess: * Amount * Color * Odor * Clots Expected: * Rubra first 3 days * Gradual decrease * Earthy/fleshy odor Abnormal: * Foul odor * Large clots * Saturating pad quickly * Constant trickle ## Bladder A full bladder can push the uterus up and to the right, preventing contraction and increasing hemorrhage risk. Signs: * Fundus high * Fundus deviated right * Excessive lochia * Frequent small voids * Bladder discomfort --- # 24. Postpartum Vital Signs ## Temperature Expected: * Up to 100.4°F in first 24 hours can be normal. Concern: * Fever over 100.4°F after 24 hours * Fever with chills, foul lochia, wound pain, or dysuria ## Pulse Expected: * 60-90 bpm * Sometimes 50-60 bpm can be normal postpartum Concern: * Tachycardia, which may indicate pain, dehydration, infection, hemorrhage, or shock ## Blood Pressure Concern: * Increased BP may indicate postpartum preeclampsia. * Decreased BP may indicate hypovolemia or hemorrhage. --- # 25. Postpartum Hemorrhage ## Major Causes * Uterine atony * Birth canal trauma * Retained placental fragments * Clotting problems ## Uterine Atony Findings: * Boggy uterus * Increased lochia * Clots * Fundus high or hard to locate Nursing actions: 1. Massage fundus. 2. Assess lochia. 3. Assist patient to void. 4. Increase IV fluids as ordered. 5. Administer oxytocin as ordered. 6. Notify provider if unresolved. ## Firm Fundus With Heavy Bleeding Think: ```text Laceration ``` Nursing actions: * Notify provider. * Monitor vital signs. * Prepare for repair. * Continue assessing bleeding. ## Shock Signs * Tachycardia * Hypotension * Pale, cold, clammy skin * Anxiety or confusion * Increased respirations * Decreased urine output --- # 26. Postpartum Perineal and Cesarean Care ## Perineal Care First 24 hours: * Ice packs * Cold perineal pads After 24 hours: * Warm packs * Sitz baths Teaching: * Use peri bottle. * Clean front to back. * Pat dry. * Change pads frequently. * Wash hands before and after pad changes. * Report foul odor, severe pain, or drainage. ## Cesarean Care Assess: * Fundus * Lochia * Dressing/incision * Urinary catheter * Pain * Respiratory status * Bowel sounds * Lower extremities Interventions: * Encourage coughing and deep breathing. * Use incentive spirometer. * Splint incision with pillow. * Encourage early ambulation. * Use SCDs if ordered. * Provide pain control. --- # 27. Postpartum Medications ## Oxytocin Purpose: * Contracts uterus. * Prevents or treats hemorrhage. ## Methergine Purpose: * Contracts uterus. Do not give with: ```text Hypertension ``` ## Rhogam Given when: * Mother is Rh negative. * Baby is Rh positive. * Within 72 hours postpartum. * Given to the mother. ## Rubella Vaccine Given when: * Mother is not immune. Teaching: * Given postpartum. * Avoid pregnancy after vaccination according to provider/facility guidance. --- # 28. Newborn Ongoing Assessment ## Normal Newborn Vital Signs | Vital Sign | Expected | | ---------- | -------------------- | | HR | 110-160 bpm | | RR | 30-60/min | | Temp | 97.7-99.5°F axillary | ## Respiratory Red Flags Report: * Grunting * Nasal flaring * Retractions * Cyanosis * Apnea * Persistent RR over 60 * Poor tone * Poor feeding ## Hypoglycemia Risk Risk factors: * Infant of diabetic mother * Large for gestational age * Small for gestational age * Preterm * Cold stress * Poor feeding Signs: * Jitteriness * Lethargy * Poor feeding * Hypothermia * Weak cry * Seizures --- # 29. Feeding Support ## Breastfeeding Nursing role: * Encourage skin-to-skin. * Assist with latch. * Assess feeding cues. * Teach feeding frequency. * Monitor output. * Assess nipple pain or damage. Good latch signs: * Wide mouth * Lips flanged outward * Chin touching breast * Rhythmic sucking and swallowing * Minimal nipple pain ## Formula Feeding Teaching: * Support feeding choice. * Teach safe formula preparation. * Do not prop bottle. * Burp infant. * Watch feeding cues. * Monitor intake and output. --- # 30. Jaundice and Phototherapy ## Jaundice Signs * Yellow skin * Yellow sclera * Poor feeding * Lethargy ## Phototherapy Nursing Care * Protect eyes. * Expose as much skin as possible. * Keep diaper on. * Monitor temperature. * Monitor hydration. * Encourage feeding. * Reposition infant. * Do not apply lotions unless ordered. --- # 31. Discharge Teaching ## Maternal Warning Signs Report: * Heavy bleeding * Saturating a pad in 1 hour or less * Large clots * Foul-smelling lochia * Fever or chills * Severe headache * Vision changes * Epigastric pain * Chest pain * Shortness of breath * Leg pain, redness, warmth, or swelling * Burning with urination * Incision separation or foul drainage * Thoughts of harming self or baby ## Newborn Warning Signs Report: * Fever * Poor feeding * Lethargy * Blue color * Trouble breathing * Worsening jaundice * Fewer wet diapers than expected * Vomiting repeatedly * Cord redness, swelling, odor, or drainage --- # 32. Postpartum Mood Teaching ## Postpartum Blues Usually: * Mild * Starts in first week * Resolves within 2 weeks Symptoms: * Crying * Mood swings * Irritability * Feeling overwhelmed ## Postpartum Depression Signs: * Persistent sadness * Hopelessness * Anxiety * Difficulty bonding * Loss of interest * Sleep or appetite changes * Thoughts of self-harm ## Postpartum Psychosis Emergency signs: * Hallucinations * Delusions * Confusion * Paranoia * Thoughts of harming self or baby Nursing priority: 1. Stay with patient. 2. Keep mother and baby safe. 3. Notify provider immediately. --- # 33. Priority Action Cheat Sheet ## OB Arrival | Situation | First Action | | ------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------- | | Contractions | Assess mother and fetus | | Decreased fetal movement | Apply fetal monitor | | Ruptured membranes | Assess FHR and fluid | | Vaginal bleeding | Assess bleeding, pain, FHR, notify provider | | Severe headache/vision changes | Assess BP and preeclampsia symptoms | ## Labor | Situation | First Action | | ----------------------- | ----------------------------------------------- | | Late decelerations | Reposition mother | | Variable decelerations | Reposition mother | | Tachysystole on Pitocin | Stop Pitocin | | Epidural hypotension | Turn side and increase fluids | | Urge to push | Check cervix | | Cord prolapse | Lift presenting part off cord and call for help | ## Delivery and Newborn | Situation | First Action | | ----------------------- | -------------------------------------- | | Newborn born | Dry and stimulate | | Poor respiratory effort | Support airway and breathing | | Cold newborn | Warm immediately | | Low APGAR | Begin resuscitation steps per protocol | ## Postpartum | Situation | First Action | | ------------------------- | ---------------------------------------- | | Boggy fundus | Massage fundus | | Fundus high/right | Assist to void | | Firm fundus with bleeding | Suspect laceration | | Saturating pad quickly | Assess fundus and call for help | | Foul lochia with fever | Suspect infection | | Calf pain/redness/warmth | Suspect DVT | | Chest pain/dyspnea | Suspect PE | | Thoughts of harming baby | Stay with patient and get emergency help | --- # 34. Big NCLEX Picture The OB nurse’s job is to protect both patients: mother and baby. Always prioritize: 1. Airway, breathing, circulation 2. Maternal stability 3. Fetal oxygenation 4. Hemorrhage prevention 5. Infection prevention 6. Newborn thermoregulation 7. Feeding and bonding 8. Safe discharge teaching --- # 35. Most Important Things to Remember * Assess mother and fetus first. * FHR baseline should be 110-160 bpm. * Moderate variability is reassuring. * Late decelerations are nonreassuring. * Variable decelerations usually mean cord compression. * Stop Pitocin for tachysystole or nonreassuring FHR. * Never do a vaginal exam with suspected placenta previa. * Prolapsed cord is an emergency. * Dry and warm newborn immediately. * Boggy fundus means massage. * High/right fundus usually means full bladder. * Firm fundus with bleeding means possible laceration. * Foul lochia suggests infection. * Chest pain or shortness of breath postpartum may be pulmonary embolism. * Thoughts of harming self or baby are an emergency.
