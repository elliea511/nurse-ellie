# Methergine

<div class="ati-drug-card">

<div class="ati-header">
  <h2>Medication</h2>
  <h1>Methergine</h1>
  <p><strong>Generic:</strong> Methylergonovine</p>
</div>

<div class="ati-grid">

<div class="ati-box">
<h3>Category / Class</h3>

- Uterotonic
- Ergot alkaloid

</div>

<div class="ati-box">
<h3>Purpose / Expected Action</h3>

- Causes strong uterine contractions
- Helps firm the uterus
- Decreases postpartum bleeding

</div>

<div class="ati-box">
<h3>Therapeutic Use</h3>

- Postpartum hemorrhage
- Uterine atony

</div>

<div class="ati-box">
<h3>Complications / Side Effects</h3>

- Hypertension
- Headache
- Nausea/vomiting
- Cramping
- Chest pain if severe

</div>

<div class="ati-box">
<h3>Contraindications / Precautions</h3>

- Hypertension
- Preeclampsia
- Eclampsia
- Severe cardiac disease

</div>

<div class="ati-box">
<h3>Nursing Interventions</h3>

- Check blood pressure before giving
- Assess fundal tone
- Monitor lochia/bleeding
- Massage fundus as ordered
- Hold and notify provider for high BP

</div>

<div class="ati-box">
<h3>Client Education</h3>

- Cramping is expected because the uterus is contracting
- Report severe headache, chest pain, or heavy bleeding
- Explain that medication helps prevent excessive bleeding

</div>

<div class="ati-box">
<h3>Evaluation of Medication Effectiveness</h3>

- Fundus firm
- Bleeding decreases
- Vital signs stable
- No signs of hemorrhage

</div>

</div>

<div class="ati-nclex">
<h3>NCLEX / ATI Must Know</h3>

Methergine is used for postpartum hemorrhage, but it is **held for hypertension or preeclampsia**.

</div>

</div>
