# Vitamin K

## Medication

|                 |                           |
| --------------- | ------------------------- |
| Medication Name | Vitamin K                 |
| Generic Name    | Phytonadione              |
| Category/Class  | Vitamin / antihemorrhagic |

## Purpose / Expected Action

* Helps the newborn make clotting factors.
* Prevents abnormal bleeding after birth.
* Supports normal blood clotting.

## Therapeutic Use

* Prevents hemorrhagic disease of the newborn.
* Given routinely after birth.

## Why Newborns Need It

Newborns are at risk for low vitamin K because:

* Their gut is sterile at birth.
* They cannot make enough vitamin K yet.
* The liver is immature.
* Breast milk is low in vitamin K.

## Route / Site

* IM injection
* Given in the vastus lateralis

## Complications / Side Effects

* Pain at injection site
* Redness at injection site
* Rare allergic reaction

## Contraindications / Precautions

Use caution with:

* Allergy to vitamin K or medication components

## Nursing Interventions

* Give after birth per facility policy.
* Use the vastus lateralis muscle.
* Monitor injection site.
* Monitor for signs of bleeding.
* Document administration.
* Teach parents why the newborn needs it.

## Client Education

* Vitamin K is not a vaccine.
* It helps the baby’s blood clot normally.
* Newborns do not make enough vitamin K right after birth.
* Refusing vitamin K increases the newborn’s risk for serious bleeding.

## Evaluation of Medication Effectiveness

Medication is effective if:

* Newborn has no abnormal bleeding.
* Injection site remains stable.
* No signs of hemorrhage occur.

## NCLEX / ATI Must Know

Vitamin K helps activate clotting factors **II, VII, IX, and X**.

It prevents bleeding. It is **not** a vaccine.

## Memory Hook

**Vitamin K = K for Koagulation.**
