# Magnesium Sulfate

## Medication

|                 |                                 |
| --------------- | ------------------------------- |
| Medication Name | Magnesium Sulfate               |
| Category/Class  | Anticonvulsant / CNS depressant |

## Purpose / Expected Action

* Decreases neuromuscular irritability.
* Prevents seizures in patients with preeclampsia.
* Treats seizures in eclampsia.
* May be used for fetal neuroprotection in preterm labor.

## Therapeutic Use

* Severe preeclampsia
* Eclampsia
* Seizure prevention
* Fetal neuroprotection before preterm birth

## Route

* IV infusion

## Complications / Side Effects

* Flushing
* Warmth
* Nausea
* Drowsiness
* Muscle weakness
* Respiratory depression
* Decreased level of consciousness
* Cardiac dysrhythmias with toxicity

## Signs of Magnesium Toxicity

* Respiratory rate less than 12/min
* Absent or decreased deep tendon reflexes
* Urine output less than 30 mL/hr
* Decreased level of consciousness
* Severe muscle weakness
* Respiratory arrest

## Antidote

* Calcium gluconate

## Contraindications / Precautions

Use caution with:

* Renal impairment
* Myasthenia gravis
* Respiratory depression
* Very low urine output

## Nursing Interventions

* Monitor respiratory rate.
* Monitor deep tendon reflexes.
* Monitor urine output.
* Monitor level of consciousness.
* Monitor blood pressure.
* Monitor fetal heart rate and uterine activity.
* Keep calcium gluconate available.
* Maintain seizure precautions.
* Use an IV pump.
* Hold medication and notify provider if toxicity signs occur.

## Client Education

* Feeling warm, flushed, or sleepy can be expected.
* Report trouble breathing, chest heaviness, extreme weakness, or feeling very difficult to arouse.
* Explain that the medication is given to prevent seizures.

## Evaluation of Medication Effectiveness

Medication is effective if:

* No seizures occur.
* Blood pressure remains controlled with ordered therapy.
* Reflexes remain present.
* Respirations remain adequate.
* Urine output stays at least 30 mL/hr.
* Maternal and fetal status remain stable.

## NCLEX / ATI Must Know

Magnesium sulfate toxicity is suspected with **absent reflexes, respiratory depression, and low urine output**.

The antidote is **calcium gluconate**.

## Memory Hook

**Magnesium = relaxes the body, but too much can relax breathing.**
